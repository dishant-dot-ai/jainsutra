Kashaya

The Passions

कषाय

The 4 passions — anger, ego, deceit, greed — in 4 intensities. The engine of bondage.

Siddhanta Principle

"Just as a sticky substance binds dust to a garment, Kashayas bind Karma to the Atman."

The Anatomy of Bondage

Explanation: Krodh (Anger), Maan (Ego), Maya (Deceit), Lobh (Greed). At their most intense (Anantanubandhi), they block Samyaktva entirely. At their subtlest (Sanjwalan), even Arihants work to dissolve them. These are not personality flaws — they are karmic chains.

01. Krodh

Anger

The fire that consumes the self before it burns others. It obscures the soul's innate quality of infinite forgiveness and peace.

02. Maan

Ego

The mountain of illusion. It creates the false 'I', blocking the realization of the soul's fundamental equality with all living beings.

03. Maya

Deceit

The tangled web of crookedness. It fractures the soul's integrity, replacing Satya (Truth) with masks and manipulation.

04. Lobh

Greed

The ditch with no bottom. The father of all sins, it tethers the soul to temporary matter, obscuring eternal bliss.

The Four Intensities

Anantanubandhi

Life-long intensity. Blocks the path of right vision (Samyaktva) entirely.

Apratyakhyana

Blocks the beginning of partial renunciation and self-restraint.

Pratyakhyana

Blocks complete renunciation. Found in those with partial vows.

Sanjwalan

Flickering intensity. The last subtle traces even ascetics must dissolve.

Dissolving the Alchemical Lead

Liberation is not the acquisition of something new, but the dissolution of the Kashayas. When the leaden weight of passion is removed, the soul naturally ascends to its pristine state of infinite knowledge and bliss.
