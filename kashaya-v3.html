Kashaya: The Four Passions

Time Limits of the Kashayas (Passions)

Each passion operates at four intensities, defined by how long it binds the soul before it can be overcome.

Anantanubandhi Life-long

Binds across infinite lifetimes

Apratyakhyana Year-long

Prevents partial renunciation

Pratyakhyana Four-month

Prevents total renunciation

Sanjvalana Fleeting

Momentary flicker, easily shed

क्रोध

Krodh / Anger

The fire that consumes the soul's tranquility. Anger is a blinding force that obscures discernment and binds the self to destructive karmas.

Maan Ego & Pride

The mountain of self-importance that prevents the descent into humility. Pride hardens the heart against wisdom.

  • Pride of Knowledge (Jnana)
  • Pride of Lineage (Kula)
  • Pride of Beauty (Rupa)

Maya Deceit

The tangled web of illusion and dishonesty. It creates a barrier between the inner truth and outward expression.

"Straightness of heart is the remedy for the crookedness of deceit."

Lobh Greed

The insatiable desire for more—whether material or emotional. Greed is the root of all other passions, anchoring the soul to the worldly mire.

Attachment Possessiveness Desire Accumulation

"He who conquers these four passions—Anger, Ego, Deceit, and Greed—is a true victor (Jina), for the external enemies are but shadows of these internal foes."

Mahavira Swami
