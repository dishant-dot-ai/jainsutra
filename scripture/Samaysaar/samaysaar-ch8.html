Part 1 · G237–G241 · The Oil-Smeared Man: Attachment Is the Cause
Analogy
The Sneha-Bhāva Experiment Adhikar 7 opens with a controlled philosophical experiment: a man smeared with oil exercises in a dusty arena, cutting trees and causing harm to living and non-living things. Dust sticks to him everywhere. Then the same man, in the same arena, doing the same exercise — but without oil. No dust adheres. Kundakunda asks by niścaya: what is the cause of the dust-bondage? Not the exercise. Not the trees cut. Not the arena. The oil — the sneha-bhāva — alone.
7.237

जह णाम को वि पुरिसो णेहबत्तो दु रेणुबहुलम्मि।
ठाणम्मि ठाइदूण य करेिद सत्थेिहं वायामं।।२३७।।

Just as a certain man, smeared with oil (sneha-yukta), standing in a place full of dust, performs exercise with weapons.

The simple version: A man covered in oil enters a dusty arena and exercises with weapons.

Sneha-Bhāva Rajo-Bandha Analogy
7.238

छिंदिद भिंदिद य तदा तालीतलकयिलवंसिपंडीओ।
सिच्चाचित्ताणं करेिद दव्वाणमुवघादं।।२३८।।

He cuts and splits palmyra, tāla, plantain, and bamboo clusters — causing harm (upghāta) to sacit (sentient) and acit (insentient) substances.

Kundakunda does not minimize the activity. Trees are cut. Harm occurs. The action is real and verifiable. This is deliberate — the philosophical point does not require denying the external event.

The simple version: He cuts living and non-living things — causing real harm to both.

Upghāta Sacit-Acit
7.239

उवघादं कुव्वंतस्स तस्स णाणाविहेिहं करणेिहं।
णिच्छयदो चिंतेज्ज हु किंपच्चयगो दु रयबंधो।।२३९।।

For this one causing upghāta by many types of instruments — by niścaya (absolute standpoint), reflect: what is the real cause of rajo-bandha (dust-bondage)?

"Niścayadaḥ" is the pivot-word. Niścaya-naya pierces through appearances to find the essential cause. Among all the visible variables — instruments, exercise, location, objects — which one truly causes the dust to adhere?

The simple version: Among all instruments and activities — by niścaya, ask: what really caused the dust to bind?

Niścaya-Naya Pratyaya (Cause)
7.240

जो सो दु णेहभावो तम्हि णरे तेण तस्स रयबंधो।
णिच्छयदो विण्णेयं ण कायचेट्ठाइहं सेसाहिं।।२४०।।

That sneha-bhāva (the nature of oil) in that man — by that alone is his rajo-bandha. By niścaya, know: not by the remaining bodily activities and so on.

This is the master-verse of the analogy. Sneha-bhāva = rāga-bhāva in the soul. The dust = karma-pudgalas. The exercise = yoga (activity of body-speech-mind). By niścaya: it is the sneha (rāga) — not the yoga — that causes karma-bondage. This directly refutes every view that holds physical action as the cause of bondage.

Contemplate: You can cease all activity. You can sit still in a cave. If rāga remains, karma will still bind. If rāga is absent, karma will not bind even in full activity.

The simple version: The oiliness caused the dust to stick — not the exercise. By niścaya: not the other bodily activities.

Sneha = Rāga Yoga ≠ Bandha Cause Niścaya Verdict
7.241

एवं मिच्छाटिट्ठी वट्टंतो बहुविहासु चिट्ठासु।
रागादी उवओगे कुव्वंतो लिप्पिद रएण।।२४१।।

Similarly, the mithyādṛṣṭi, engaging in many types of activities, making rāgādi in his upayoga (consciousness), is stained by karma-dust.

Upayoga (the activity of consciousness) is the key. When consciousness is colored by rāga-dveṣa-moha, every action — whether outwardly good or bad, violent or nonviolent — results in karma-binding. The mithyādṛṣṭi is smeared with the inner oil of rāga.

The simple version: The mithyādṛṣṭi, engaged in many activities while keeping rāga in consciousness, gets coated with karma.

Mithyādṛṣṭi Upayoga Rāgādi
Part 2 · G242–G246 · The Man Without Oil: Right Vision Does Not Bind
7.242

Just as that same man, having removed all oil, standing in that same dusty place, performs exercise with weapons — cuts palmyra, tāla, plantain, bamboo — causes upghāta to sacit and acit dravyas.

Kundakunda holds all variables constant — the dusty arena, the exercise, the cutting, the trees, the harm — and changes only one: the sneha (oil) has been removed. This is a controlled philosophical experiment. Does dust adhere to the oil-free man?

The simple version: Same man, same place, same exercise, same trees cut — but now without oil. Same action, different inner state.

Controlled Analogy Nikṣipta-Sneha
7.244

For this one (now without oil) causing upghāta by many instruments — by niścaya, reflect: what is the cause of rajo-bandha? (G244) — The sneha-bhāva that was in that man was the cause of rajo-bandha — by niścaya; the remaining bodily activities are NOT the cause. (G245)

The counter-case confirms the positive case. Sneha (rāga) caused binding in its presence. Its absence means no binding — even with the same actions. Both cases together prove: sneha alone is the cause, activity is never the cause.

The simple version: No oil, no dust-bondage. Bodily activity was never the cause — proved by both presence and absence of the sole cause.

7.246

एवं सम्मािदट्ठी वट्टंतो बहुविहासु चिट्ठासु।
ण करेिद रागमादी उवओगे ण लिप्पिद रएण।।२४६।।

Similarly, the samyag-dṛṣṭi, engaged in many activities, not making rāgādi in upayoga, is not stained by karma-dust.

This verse is the liberation-promise of Adhikar 7. The samyag-dṛṣṭi does not need to stop activity. His consciousness, established in ātma-svarūpa, does not take rāga into its upayoga. Therefore karma does not bind.

Contemplate: Freedom is not in stopping action. Freedom is in purifying the upayoga — the quality of consciousness itself.

The simple version: The samyag-dṛṣṭi — active in many ways, not making rāga in consciousness — is not stained by karma.

Samyag-Dṛṣṭi Upayoga Without Rāga Karma-Free Activity
Part 3 · G247–G258 · Three False Mental Resolutions: I Kill, I Give Life, I Make Happy
Teaching
The Three False Inner Claims Kundakunda identifies three categories of mūḍha-adhyavasāna — wrong inner determination — that form the root of karma-bondage for the ajñānī. Each is refuted by niścaya: (1) "I kill / I am killed" — refuted by āyu-karma doctrine. (2) "I give life / others give me life" — refuted by āyuḥ-karma-udaya. (3) "I make others happy / miserable" — refuted by karma-vipāka doctrine. Both sides of each claim — the positive and its negation — are mūḍha-mati when held as a claim about one's own agency.
7.247

जो मण्णदे हणामि त्ति जो मण्णदे हण्णामि चेव त्ति।
उभो वि मूढमदी अण्णाणी ते विवरीया।।२४७।।

One who thinks "I kill" and one who thinks "I am killed" — both are of mūḍha-mati (confused mind), both are ajñānī. The opposite (jñānī) is the knower.

The simple version: "I kill" — mūḍha. "I am killed" — also mūḍha. The jñānī knows neither claim is accurate.

Mūḍha-Mati Ajñānī Hiṃsā Misconception
7.248

Death occurs through āyu-karma-kṣaya (exhaustion of life-span karma) alone. No one can take another's āyu-karma or remove it. The so-called "killer" has not touched the other's āyu-karma — it was going to exhaust at that moment, by its own karma-dynamic. Therefore "I killed" is mithyā-adhyavasāna.

The simple version: Death comes when āyu-karma is exhausted — by that alone. You cannot take another's āyu-karma. "I killed" is false at the niścaya level.

Āyu-Karma Karma-Kṣaya Niścaya Refutation
7.250

One who thinks "I give life to others" or "others give me life" — both are mūḍha-ajñānī. Life continues through āyuḥ-karma-udaya (arising of life-karma) alone. No one can give another's āyuḥ. Life is not in another's hands — it is in each jīva's own karma-trajectory.

The simple version: Life comes from one's own āyuḥ-karma — no one can give it to you or extend it. "I give life" — also mūḍha.

Āyuḥ-Karma-Udaya Mūḍha-Ajñānī
7.253

One who thinks "I make beings miserable" or "I make beings happy" — mūḍha-ajñānī. Happiness and misery arise through each jīva's own karma-udaya alone. You cannot give another their karma-result. You cannot inject suffering or joy into another's consciousness — their karma delivers it.

This completes the three-fold refutation: hiṃsā (killing), jīvana (giving life), and sukha-duḥkha (happiness/misery). In all three, the inner claim of being the kartā (doer) for others is mithyā — and is itself the cause of karma-bondage.

Contemplate: Even the most compassionate action, held with "I am making them happy" — binds karma if the kartā-bhāva remains. Compassion without kartā-bhāva is the samyag-dṛṣṭi's path.

The simple version: Happiness and misery come to each jīva through their own karma. "I made them happy" — mūḍha.

Sukha-Duḥkha Karma-Udaya Kartā-Bhāva
7.257

G257: One who dies and is miserable — by his own karma-udaya alone. So "I killed him, I made him miserable" is mithyā.

G258: One who does not die and is not miserable — also by his own karma-udaya. So "I did not kill him, I did not cause him misery" is equally a claim in the same error.

This is subtle and profound. Even taking credit for non-harm — if held as a claim about one's own power — is still the same mūḍha-mati operating in reverse. The jñānī transcends both claims: neither "I killed" nor "I didn't kill" as an assertion of agency.

The simple version: "I killed him" — false. "I didn't kill him" — also false as a claim about your power. Both are mūḍha-mati.

Negation Also Mūḍha Akartā-Bhāva
Part 4 · G259–G262 · Passionate Determination Alone Is Bondage — Ultimate Standpoint Summary
7.259

G259: The mūḍha-mati — the wrong inner conviction "I make beings miserable/happy" — binds shubha-ashubha karma.

G260: Whatever adhyavasāna one makes with the intention of "making beings miserable/happy" — that becomes pāpa-bandha or puṇya-bandha.

G261: Similarly for "I kill / I give life" — the adhyavasāna's quality (shubha or ashubha) determines whether it produces puṇya or pāpa bondage.

The simple version: The mūḍha-mati's inner determination — regardless of whether the act was "good" or "bad" externally — binds karma according to the quality of the inner state.

7.262

अज्झिवसदेण बंधो सत्ते मारेउ मा व मारेउ।
एसो बंधसमासो जीवाणं णिच्छयणयस्स।।२६२।।

अध्यवसानेन बन्धः सत्त्वान् मारयतु मा वा मारयतु। एषः बन्धसमासः जीवानां निश्चयनयस्य।।

By adhyavasāna (inner determination) alone is bondage — whether beings are killed or not. This is the brief (samāsa) summary of bondage for all jīvas according to niścaya-naya.

"Satte māreü mā va māreü" — whether beings die or not. The external event is irrelevant. Even if no being is killed, if the adhyavasāna of violence is present, karma binds. Even if beings do die near you, if the adhyavasāna is absent, karma does not bind. Niścaya-naya cuts through all external criteria and establishes adhyavasāna as the only relevant variable.

Contemplate: The courtroom of karma has only one witness — your adhyavasāna. It does not examine what happened outside. It asks only: what was your inner determination?

The simple version: Bondage happens through inner determination alone — whether beings are killed or not. This is niścaya-naya's complete definition of bondage.

Adhyavasāna Bandha-Samāsa Niścaya-Naya External Event Irrelevant
Part 5 · G263–G265 · The Object Is Not the Cause
7.263

G263: In alīka (falsehood), adatta (theft), abrahmacarya (non-celibacy), parigraha (possessiveness) — the adhyavasāna binds pāpa-karma.

G264: In satya (truth), datta (charity), brahmacarya (celibacy), aparigraha (non-possessiveness) — the adhyavasāna binds puṇya-karma.

Even good actions (satya, datta, brahmacarya) bind puṇya-karma if the adhyavasāna is externally oriented. This is why puṇya-karma, though shubha, still keeps the soul in saṃsāra — it is still bondage, just of a more comfortable variety.

The simple version: Falsehood/theft/non-celibacy → adhyavasāna binds pāpa. Truth/charity/celibacy → adhyavasāna binds puṇya. Both bind through adhyavasāna.

7.265

The soul's adhyavasāna takes a vastu (object/situation) as its basis — but bondage is NOT from the vastu; bondage is from the adhyavasāna alone.

The vastu (truth, falsehood, the object) is merely the occasion — not the cause. Adhyavasāna uses vastu as its basis, as its point of reference, as the stage on which it plays. But the play — the karma-cause — is the adhyavasāna alone, not the stage.

Contemplate: You can do good actions and still bind karma (puṇya-karma, which still keeps the soul in saṃsāra). Liberation requires not just good action but ātma-centered adhyavasāna.

The simple version: The object or situation is just the occasion. Bondage is NOT from the vastu — bondage is from adhyavasāna alone.

Vastu ≠ Cause Adhyavasāna = Cause Puṇya-Bandha
Part 6 · G266–G267 · The Deluded Mind Is Purposeless
7.266

G266: "I make beings miserable/happy, I bind them/free them" — this mūḍha-mati is nirarthaka (purposeless) and mithyā (false for you — the soul).

G267: If beings bind through adhyavasāna-karma and become free when established on the moksha-path — what do YOU do? (Emphasizes akartā-bhāva — you are not the doer of others' bondage or liberation.)

G267 is the philosophical punchline. Each jīva binds through its own adhyavasāna and frees itself by its own moksha-path. You — the one claiming "I bind/free them" — have no actual mechanism of agency. Your mūḍha-mati is not just wrong; it is pointless.

The simple version: If each jīva binds by its own adhyavasāna and frees itself by its own path — what exactly are you doing when you claim "I bind/free them"? Nothing.

Nirarthaka Akartā-Bhāva Moksha-Path
Part 7 · G268–G272 · Soul Makes All Inner States; Passionate Determination Defined
7.268

G268: Soul, through adhyavasāna, makes itself tiryañca (animal), nāraka (hell-being), deva (deity), manuṣya (human) — all the many types of puṇya and pāpa.

G269: And similarly dharma-adharma, jīva-ajīva, loka-aloka — the soul makes all these as itself through adhyavasāna. Through its inner determination, the soul experiences and identifies with all the varied realities of existence.

G270: Those in whom these adhyavasānas are absent — those muni-kunjaras (elephants among munis, the great monks) — are not stained by shubha or ashubha karma. They move through the world without the inner oil of adhyavasāna. No karma adheres.

The simple version: The soul makes itself tiryañc, nāraka, deva, human — through adhyavasāna. The great munis in whom these adhyavasānas are absent bind neither shubha nor ashubha karma.

Muni-Kunjara Adhyavasāna-Free Shubha-Ashubha
7.271

Buddhi, vyavasāya, adhyavasāna, mati, vijñāna, citta, bhāva, pariṇāma — all these are ekārtha (synonyms, pointing to one and the same inner state).

A comprehensive synonymy that prevents conceptual fragmentation. Adhyavasāna is not a technical philosophical term divorced from lived experience. It is what you ordinarily call "what you were thinking/feeling" — your consciousness-mode at any given moment.

The simple version: All the words for inner state — buddhi, mati, citta, bhāva, pariṇāma — mean the same one thing: adhyavasāna. Your inner determination right now is your karma-cause.

Ekārtha Synonyms Pariṇāma Citta-Bhāva
7.272

Vyavahāra-naya is thus prohibited by niścaya-naya. The munis established in niścaya-naya attain nirvāṇa.

Vyavahāra-naya's external descriptions — actions, objects, rituals as causes of bondage/liberation — are overridden by niścaya-naya's internal insight: adhyavasāna alone is the cause. This does not abolish vyavahāra; it reveals what vyavahāra correctly points toward when understood by niścaya.

The simple version: Vyavahāra-naya is overruled by niścaya. Munis established in niścaya — knowing adhyavasāna is the sole cause — attain nirvāṇa.

Vyavahāra Prohibited Niścaya-Naya Nirvāṇa
Part 8 · G273–G277 · Non-Liberatable Soul, Conventional, and Ultimate Standpoint Descriptions
7.273

G273: Even the abhavya (one incapable of liberation) who performs all vrata (vows), samiti (carefulness), gupti (restraint), śīla (conduct), and tapa (austerity) is ajñānī and mithyādṛṣṭi — lacking jñāna-śraddhā in ātmā.

G274: The abhavya studies all 11 aṅgas — but scriptural study does not produce guṇa (spiritual quality) for one who lacks jñāna-śraddhā regarding moksha.

G275: The abhavya has śraddhā in dharma — but as bhoga-nimitta (for the purpose of sensual fruition, reaching heavens), not as karma-kṣaya-nimitta (for destroying karma toward liberation).

These three gathas establish that external practice — even perfect external practice — without the correct adhyavasāna (jñāna-śraddhā oriented toward karma-kṣaya, not toward bhoga) is insufficient. The teaching is about intention-quality, not fatalism.

Contemplate: Check the adhyavasāna behind your practice. Are you practicing for what? The destination of your inner intention determines what the practice builds.

The simple version: Perfect external practice without right jñāna-śraddhā produces nothing toward liberation. The abhavya's faith aims at better rebirth, not liberation — so all his practice is misdirected at the root.

Abhavya Bhoga-Nimitta Karma-Kṣaya-Nimitta
7.276

G276 (Vyavahāra-naya): Āyāra (Ācārāṅga) and scriptures = jñāna; jīva-ādi nava padārtha (nine realities) = darśana; ṣaṭ-jīvanikāya (six classes of living beings, rightly cared for) = cāritra.

G277 (Niścaya-naya): Ātmā is my jñāna. Ātmā is my darśana and cāritra. Ātmā is my pratyākhyāna (renunciation). Ātmā is my samvara (stoppage of karma) and yoga (union).

When the soul is established in its own svarūpa, jñāna = ātmā (not a text), darśana = ātmā (not a doctrine), cāritra = ātmā (not an action). Practice in vyavahāra is the means; ātmā-svarūpa is the reality the practice points toward.

The simple version: Vyavahāra: jñāna = scripture, darśana = doctrine, cāritra = conduct. Niścaya: ātmā IS my jñāna, ātmā IS my darśana, ātmā IS my cāritra, my renunciation, my samvara, my yoga.

Jñāna-Darśana-Cāritra Vyavahāra vs. Niścaya Ātmā as All
Part 9 · G278–G287 · Crystal Analogy, The Knower as Non-Doer, Lower Karma
Key Analogy
The Sphaṭika (Crystal) and the Jñānī A pure crystal placed near a red object appears red — but does not transform into redness. It remains crystal. The apparent redness is the reflected quality of the external substance. Similarly, the jñānī's soul is pure — it does not itself transform into rāga. Rāga appears from the arising (udaya) of pudgala-karma — para-dravya — not from the soul's own pariṇāma. The jñānī sees this distinction clearly. The ajñānī does not, and therefore transforms into rāga — binding karma again and again.
7.278

जह फलिहमणी सुद्धो ण सयं परिणमिद रागमादीहिं।
रंगिज्जिद अण्णेहिं दु सो रत्तादीहिं दव्वेहिं।।२७८।।

यथा स्फटिकमणिः शुद्धः न स्वयं परिणमते रागादिभिः। रज्यते अन्यैस्तु स रक्तादिभिः द्रव्यैः।।

एवं ज्ञानी शुद्धः न स्वयं परिणमते रागादिभिः। रज्यते अन्यैस्तु स रागादिभिः दोषैः।।

Just as the pure crystal (sphaṭika-maṇi) does not itself transform into rāga-rūpa (red form) — but by other dravyas (red substances placed near it) it appears reddened. (G278)

Similarly, the jñānī is pure — does not itself transform into rāga — but by other rāgādi-doṣas (which are para-dravya, belonging to pudgala-karma) it appears to be rāgī (one having rāga). (G279)

Contemplate: Two kinds of contact with rāga — knowing it as para-dravya (jñānī's way) and transforming into it (ajñānī's way). Only the second binds.

The simple version: Crystal stays crystal — it doesn't become red. It just reflects red. Jñānī's soul stays pure — it doesn't transform into rāga. What appears as rāga is para-dravya (pudgala-karma) — not the soul's own transformation.

Sphaṭika Analogy Para-Dravya Jñānī Akāraka
7.280

ण य रागदोसमोहं कुव्विद णाणी कसायभावं वा।
सयमप्पणो ण सो तेण कारगो तेसिं भावाणं।।२८०।।

न च रागद्वेषमोहं करोति ज्ञानी कषायभावं वा। स्वयमात्मनो न स तेन कारकस्तेषां भावानाम्।।

The jñānī does not create rāga, dveṣa, moha, or kaṣāya-bhāva in itself. Therefore it is not the kāraka (doer/maker) of those bhāvas.

The simple version: The jñānī does not create rāga-dveṣa-moha or kaṣāya. Therefore it is not the maker of those states.

Akārakatva Kaṣāya-Bhāva Jñānī
7.281

रागम्हि य दोसम्हि य कसायकम्मेसु चेव जे भावा।
तेहिं दु परिणमंतो रागादी बंधिद पुणो वि।।२८१।।

Ajñānī who transforms in rāga, dveṣa, kaṣāya-karma-bhāvas — binds rāgādi karma again and again.

G282: The ātmā/cetayitā (the soul as experiencer) transforming in those rāga-dveṣa-kaṣāya-bhāvas — binds rāgādi karma. This is the mechanism of saṃsāra: to pariṇama (undergo transformation) in rāga = to bind rāga-karma = to continue the cycle.

The simple version: The ajñānī transforms into rāga-dveṣa-kaṣāya — and that transformation binds karma again and again.

Pariṇāma in Rāga Ajñānī Binds Saṃsāra Mechanism
7.283

अप्पडिकमणं दुविहं अपच्चखाणं तहेव विण्णेयं।
एदेणुवदेसेण य अकारगो विण्णदो चेदा।।२८३।।

Apratikramaṇa (non-repentance) is of two kinds — dravya and bhāva. Apratyākhyāna (non-renunciation) likewise — know these. By this teaching, jīva is known as akāraka (non-doer). (G283)

G284: Two-fold apratikramaṇa — in dravya and bhāva — and apratyākhyāna also. By this teaching, jīva is known as akāraka.

G285: As long as ātmā performs apratikramaṇa and apratyākhyāna of dravya and bhāva — until then it is known as kartā (the doer). When resolved through niścaya-understanding — akāraka.

The deepest level of pratikramaṇa is recognizing through niścaya that the soul was never truly the kāraka of karma. This recognition IS the true bhāva-pratikramaṇa — the inner repentance that dissolves the very root of doership.

The simple version: Apratikramaṇa and apratyākhyāna are two-fold: dravya (external) and bhāva (inner). As long as these operate in the soul, it is kartā. When resolved — akāraka.

Pratikramaṇa Dravya-Bhāva Akārakatva
7.286

आधाकम्मादीया पोग्गलदव्वस्स जे इमे दोसा।
कह ते कुव्विद णाणी परदव्वगुणा दु जे णिच्चं।।२८६।।

आधाकर्मादयाः पुद्गलद्रव्यस्य ये इमे दोषाः। कथं तान् करोति ज्ञानी परद्रव्यगुणास्तु ये नित्यम्।।

आधाकर्म उद्दिष्टं च पुद्गलमयमिदं द्रव्यम्। कथं तन्मम भवति कृतं यन्नित्यमचेतनमुक्तम्।।

The defects of adhaḥkarma (aśubha food-karma made for a monk) and uddeśika (food prepared with intention toward a specific person) — these are defects of pudgala-dravya. How can the jñānī create those? — since those are always the qualities of para-dravya (another substance — the pudgala). (G286)

Adhaḥkarma and uddeśika — this paudgalika (made of pudgala) dravya — how can it be my (ātmā's) creation? — since what is always acetana (insentient) — that is what is thus said by niścaya. (G287)

The final gathas close Adhikar 7 with the ontological ground of akārakatva: ātmā is cetana (conscious). Pudgala is acetana (insentient). The two are fundamentally different dravyas. Ātmā cannot be the creator of pudgala-karma-qualities — their very substances are different in kind.

Contemplate: What is made of matter, moved by matter-causes, following matter-laws — that is not you. Knowing this clearly is liberation's beginning.

The simple version: Adhaḥkarma and uddeśika are pudgala — always insentient. How can the ātmā (which is cetan) claim to have made them? It cannot. By niścaya, ātmā is not their maker.

Adhaḥkarma Uddeśika Acetana ≠ Ātmā's Creation Ontological Akārakatva
Closing Colophon

इति बन्धो निष्क्रान्त:।
इति श्रीमदमृतचन्द्रसूरिविरचितायां समयसारव्याख्यायामात्मख्यातौ
बन्धप्ररूपकः सप्तमोऽङ्कः।।

Thus Bandha is departed. In the Ātmakhyāti — the commentary on Samaysaar composed by the venerable Ācārya Amṛtacandra Sūri — the seventh adhikar, the exposition of Bandha, is thus concluded.
