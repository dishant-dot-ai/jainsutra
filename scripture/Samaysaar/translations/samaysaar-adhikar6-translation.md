# Samaysaar — Adhikar 6: Nirjara (निर्जरा अधिकार)
## Shedding of Karma

**Gathas: G193–G236 (44 gathas)**
**Source:** Acharya Kundkund's Samaysaar (Prakrit original)
**Commentary basis:** Amritchandra Suri's Atmakhyati

---

## Part 1: What Is Nirjara? The Foundational Declaration (G193–G194)

### G193

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
(Text visible from prior session — opening of Nirjara Adhikar)

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
All upayoga-upabhoga (conscious experiencing) by the samyag-dṛṣṭi of insentient and sentient substances through the sense organs — all of it becomes a cause of nirjarā.

**COMMENTARY:**
The Adhikar opens with a revolutionary reframe. Every experience the samyag-dṛṣṭi has — through the senses, through the mind — becomes a cause of nirjarā (karma-shedding), not of new bondage. Why? Because the samyag-dṛṣṭi lacks the rāgādi inner bhāva that converts experience into bondage. Without that inner conversion, the karma simply exhausts itself through experience and drops away.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
For the one who knows the self, every experience dissolves karma instead of building it.

---

### G194

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
When any substance is experienced, sukha (pleasure) or duḥkha (pain) necessarily arises in the soul. Experiencing that — it goes to nirjarā (is shed, dissolved).

**COMMENTARY:**
Experience cannot be neutral — it produces either pleasant or unpleasant sensation. This is not a problem for the samyag-dṛṣṭi. These sensations, correctly known as karma-vipāka (the ripening of previously bound karma), pass through experience and exit as nirjarā. The key is "correctly known" — recognized as karma-products, not as self, not as worth craving or fleeing.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Every pleasure and pain that a knower experiences is karma burning off — not karma accumulating.

---

## Part 2: The Doctor and the Drunkard — Knowledge Neutralizes Karma (G195–G196)

### G195

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
जह विसमुवभुंजंतो वेज्जो पुिरसो ण मरणमुवय िद।
पोग्गलकम्मस्सुदयं तह भुंजिद णेव बज्झदे णाणी।।१९५।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
यथा विषमुपभुञ्जानो वैद्यः पुरुषो न मरणमुपयाति।
पुद्गलकर्मण उदयं तथा भुङ्क्ते नैव बध्यते ज्ञानी।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Just as a physician eating poison does not die — so the jñānī, experiencing the rise of pudgala-karma, does not bind new karma.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
The physician's knowledge of medicine gives him a counter-force that neutralizes the poison's killing power. Similarly, the jñānī's amogha-jñāna (infallible knowledge) — specifically the absence of rāgādi bhāva — counter-neutralizes karma's binding power. When karma rises and the jñānī experiences its fruits, karma cannot bind because the jñānī lacks the inner bhāva-āsrava (rāga-dveṣa-moha) that would convert experience into new bondage.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Knowledge is the antidote. Just as the knowledgeable physician eats poison without dying, the jñānī lives through karma's fruits without binding new karma.

---

### G196

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
जह मज्जं पिबमाणो अरदीभावेण मज्जिद ण पुिरसो।
दव्वुवभोगे अरदो णाणी िव ण बज्झिद तहेव।।१९६।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
यथा मद्यं पिबन् अरतिभावेन माद्यति न पुरुषः।
द्रव्योपभोगेऽरतो ज्ञान्यपि न बध्यते तथैव।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Just as a person drinking intoxicant with arati-bhāva (feeling of loathing/non-attachment) does not become intoxicated — so the jñānī, having vairāgya toward all sense-object enjoyment, does not bind karma.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
Arati means the inner state of loathing, non-attachment, absence of attraction — technically, the disposition of vairāgya. When the jñānī's consciousness engages with sense-objects, the engagement is without inner rāga (attraction, craving, ownership-feeling). Without rāga as the driver of experience, the experience cannot produce new karma. The jñānī experiences sense-objects because previous karma brings them — but the lack of inner rāga means the experience cannot generate future karma.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Intoxication comes from craving, not from the drink. Karma-bondage comes from inner rāga, not from external experience.

## Part 3: The Owner and the Instrument — Karma Cannot Be Imputed to the Jñānī (G197)

### G197

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
सेवंतो िव ण सेविद असेवमाणो िव सेवगो कोई।
पगरणचेट्ठा कस्स िव ण य पायरणो ित सो होिद।।१९७।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
सेवमानोऽपि न सेवते असेवमानोऽपि सेवकः कश्चित्।
प्रकरणचेष्टा कस्यापि न च प्राकरण इति स भवति।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
One who performs seva (service/activity) does not really do it; one who does not do it may still be the doer. The activity of the instrument (prakaraṇa) does not make someone the owner of that instrument's action.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
The prakaraṇa (instrument/tool) performs activity. But the activity of the instrument is not the activity of whoever wields it — the owner must claim it as their own for it to be "theirs." The samyag-dṛṣṭi, through the power of bheda-jñāna, does not claim karma-udaya as "mine." He is not the owner of the activity happening through his body-karma complex. The ajñānī claims ownership of karma-driven activity ("I did this, I felt this") — and that claiming is itself the bondage.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Not the activity but the ownership-claim makes you the doer. The jñānī doesn't claim karma's activities as theirs.

## Part 4: I Am Jñāyaka-Bhāva — Rāga Is Not My Svabhāva (G198–G200)

### G198

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
उदयिववागो िविवहो कम्माणं वण्णदो जिणवरेिहं।
ण तु ते मज्झ सहावा जाणगभावो तु अहमेको।।१९८।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
उदयविपाको विविधः कर्मणां वर्णितो जिनवरैः।
न तु ते मम स्वभावाः ज्ञायकभावस्त्वहमेकः।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The many types of karma-udaya-vipāka (fruition of arising karma) have been described in various ways by the Jinavars. But those are not my svabhāva. I am one — the jñāyaka-bhāva (pure knowing-state).

**COMMENTARY:**
The omniscient Jinas have described karma-vipāka exhaustively — the many types of karma, their udaya, their fruition, their effects on the soul's experience. Kundkund accepts all of this — and then draws the crucial line: "those are not my svabhāva." All the variegated karma-effects (sukha, duḥkha, different birth-conditions, etc.) are karma-products. They arise in my experiential field. But I, the ātmā, am not any of them. I am the single jñāyaka-bhāva — the pure knowing-consciousness.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Karma produces all kinds of results. None of them are you. You are the one who knows all of them.

---

### G199

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
पोग्गलकम्मं रागो तस्स िववागोदओ हविद एसो।
ण तु एस मज्झ भावो जाणगभावो हु अहमेको।।१९९।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
पुद्गलकर्म रागस्तस्य विपाकोदयो भवति एषः।
न त्वेष मम भावो ज्ञायकभावः खल्वहमेकः।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Rāga is pudgala-karma; this rāga-bhāva is its vipāka-udaya (rising fruition). But this is not my bhāva. I am certainly one — the jñāyaka-bhāva alone.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
This is a precise metaphysical claim: rāga (attraction, love, desire) is not a property of the soul — it is pudgala-karma in its fruition-arising form. When mohanīya-karma (the deluding karma) rises, its fruition appears as rāga-bhāva in the experiential field. The ajñānī misidentifies this as "my feeling, my nature." The jñānī sees: "this rāga is karma's product — its vipāka-udaya. Not my bhāva. I am jñāyaka-bhāva alone."

**SIMPLY PUT:**
That feeling of attraction or love you're experiencing? That is karma's ripening — not you. You are the awareness that knows it is happening.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
Right now, whatever feeling is strongest in you — notice that you are also aware of it. Which of these is more fundamentally you: the feeling, or the awareness?

---

### G200

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
एवं सम्मिदिट्ठी अप्पाणं मुणिद जाणगसहावं।
उदयं कम्मिवावागं च मुयिद तच्चं िवयाणंतो।।२००।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
एवं सम्यग्दृष्टिः आत्मानं मुनाति ज्ञायकस्वभावम्।
उदयं कर्मविपाकं च मुञ्चति तत्त्वं विजानन्।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Thus the samyag-dṛṣṭi knows the self as jñāyaka-svabhāva (the nature of the pure knower). And knowing tattva (reality correctly), releases/lets go of karma-vipāka-udaya.

**COMMENTARY:**
G198-G199 stated the principle; G200 shows its result. The samyag-dṛṣṭi who knows "I am jñāyaka-svabhāva; rāga is karma-vipāka" — thereby "muñcati" (releases, lets go of) the karma-udaya. The word "muñcati" is important: not that the jñānī fights the karma or suppresses it, but that knowing correctly, the karma-vipāka is naturally released. Like releasing your grip on something you've realized you were mistakenly holding.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Right knowledge of self as pure knower — combined with right knowledge of rāga as karma's product — results in natural release of karma.

## Part 5: Even a Paramāṇu of Rāgādi = Self-Ignorance (G201–G202)

### G201

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
परमाणुमित्तयं पि हु रागादीणं तु िवज्जदे जस्स।
ण िव सो जाणिद अप्पाणयं तु सव्वागमधरो िव।।२०१।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
परमाणुमात्रमपि खलु रागादीनां तु विद्यते यस्य।
नापि स जानात्यात्मानं तु सर्वागमधरोऽपि।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
In whom even a paramāṇu-measure (atom-measure) of rāgādi (rāga, dveṣa, etc.) exists — even if he is sarvāgama-dhara (carrier of all āgamas/scriptures) — he does not know the ātmā.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
The standard is absolute: even the smallest trace of rāgādi indicates the absence of true ātma-jñāna. Why? Because rāga is karma's vipāka-udaya (G199). If one truly knew the self as jñāyaka-bhāva, one could not mistake karma's product for the self. The presence of rāga — at any level — means the discrimination between self and karma has not fully occurred. And this is true even for someone who has memorized all scriptures. Textual knowledge without experiential self-discrimination is still ignorance of the self.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Even the tiniest trace of rāga reveals you haven't truly known the self yet — no matter how many scriptures you've studied.

---

### G202

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
अप्पाणमयाणंतो अणप्पयं चािव सो अयाणंतो।
कह होिद सम्मिदट्ठी जीवाजीवे अयाणंतो।।२०२।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
आत्मानमजानन् अनात्मानं चापि सोऽजानन्।
कथं भवति सम्यग्दृष्टिर्जीवाजीवावजानन्।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Not knowing the self — and thus also not knowing the non-self — how can he be samyag-dṛṣṭi, not knowing jīva and ajīva?

**COMMENTARY:**
Self-knowledge and non-self-knowledge arise together. When the soul truly knows itself as jīva (consciousness, jñāyaka-bhāva), it simultaneously knows what it is not — ajīva (matter, karma, body, rāga). These are two sides of one discrimination. Without self-knowledge, non-self-knowledge is also absent. Without knowing both jīva and ajīva correctly, samyak-darśana (right perception) cannot arise. The rāgī — who misidentifies with rāga — neither knows jīva correctly (thinks jīva = rāga) nor ajīva correctly (thinks rāga is not ajīva/karma-product).

**SIMPLY PUT:**
True knowledge of the self and true knowledge of what is not-self arise together. Without one, you don't have the other.

---

## Part 6: Grasp the Stable Single Jñāna-Bhāva (G203–G206)

### G203

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
आदम्हि दव्वभावे अपदे मोत्तूण गिण्ह तह णियदं।
िथरमेगिममं भावं उवलब्धंत सहावेण।।२०३।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
आत्मनि द्रव्यभावानपदानि मुक्त्वा गृहाण तथा नियतम्।
स्थिरमेकमिमं भावमुपलभ्यमानं स्वभावेन ।।२०३।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
In the ātmā — abandoning the dravya-bhāvas (material states) that are apad (not the true resting-place) — grasp that fixed, single, stable bhāva that is attainable through svabhāva.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
"Apad" means "not a stable station" — something that cannot be a permanent resting place. All dravya-bhāva — the states arising from karma's interaction with the soul — are apad: impermanent, unstable, borrowed. The soul's svabhāva (own nature) is the one stable bhāva: pure jñāna-consciousness, niyata (fixed), sthira (stable), eka (single/undivided). This is what the soul can actually inhabit permanently — its own nature.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
All the states that come and go are not your home. Your home is the one stable thing: pure knowing. Abandon the transients; grasp the permanent.

---

### G204

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
आभिणिसुदोिधमणकेवलं च तं होिद एक्केमेव पदं।
सो एसो परमट्ठो जं लिहदुं णिव्युिदं जािद।।२०४।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
आभिनिबोधिकश्रुतावधिमन:पर्ययकेवलं च तद्भवत्येकमेव पदम्।
स एष परमार्थः यं लब्ध्वा निर्वृतिं याति।।२०४।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Mati-jñāna, śruta-jñāna, avadhi-jñāna, manaḥparyaya-jñāna, and kevala-jñāna — that is one single pada (station/abode). That is the paramārtha (ultimate reality) — by attaining which one goes to nirvāṇa (liberation).

**ANVAYARTHA:**
The five types of jñāna (perception-based knowledge, scriptural knowledge, clairvoyance, mind-reading, omniscience) appear to be five different things. From one level they are — they have different scopes and capacities. But from the paramārtha (ultimate) standpoint, they are all one: the soul's consciousness expressing itself at different degrees of unfolding. The substrate is one. All five are "jñāna" — the same fundamental stuff. Recognizing jñāna as one single pada — one platform, one abode — and resting there: that is the practice. Attaining that awareness fully = nirvāṇa.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Whether you have little knowledge or omniscience, it's all one thing — knowing. Rest in knowing as such, not in its particular contents.

---

### G205

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
णाणगुणेण विहीणा एदं तु पदं बहू िव ण लहंते।
तं गिण्ह णियदमेदं जिद इच्छिस कम्मपिरमोक्खं।।२०५।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
ज्ञानगुणेन विहीना एतत्तु पदं बहवोऽपि न लभन्ते।
तद् गृहाण नियतमेतद् यदीच्छसि कर्मपरिमोक्षम्।।२०५।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Many people, devoid of jñāna-guṇa (the quality of knowledge), do not attain this pada. Therefore, O bhavya! If you desire complete liberation from karma — grasp this niyata (certain, fixed) jñāna.

**COMMENTARY:**
The instruction is urgent and direct: "If you want complete liberation from karma, take hold of this jñāna — and hold it with certainty." The word "niyatam" carries the force of "unwaveringly, with firm resolve, without alternative." Many beings do ritual, tapas, charitable acts — all without grasping the fundamental: that jñāna is the one path. Liberation comes from jñāna alone, not from karma-kāṇḍa (ritual action-based approaches).

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Millions miss liberation not for lack of effort but for lack of jñāna. Grasp jñāna firmly. It alone frees from karma.

---

### G206

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
एदगिह रदो णिच्चं संतुट्ठो होिह णिचमेदम्हि।
एदेण होिह तित्तो होहिद तुह उत्तमं सोक्खं।।२०६।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
एतस्मिन् रतो नित्यं सन्तुष्टो भव नित्यमेतस्मिन्।
एतेन भव तृप्तो भविष्यति तवोत्तमं सौख्यम्।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Be always ratī (devoted, content) in this (jñāna). Be always santushṭa (satisfied) in this. Be tṛpta (satiated) in this. This will become your uttama-sukha (highest happiness).

**ANVAYARTHA:**
Three imperatives — ratī (delight/devotion), santushṭa (satisfaction), tṛpta (fullness/satiation) — all directed toward the same object: jñāna itself. Not toward the contents of knowledge, not toward outcomes, but toward the very fact of knowing. When the soul takes jñāna itself as its object of delight and satisfaction — it is coming home to its own svabhāva. In that homecoming, the "uttama-sukha" (the highest happiness) arises — not as something separate to be achieved, but as the natural quality of resting in one's own nature.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Find your contentment in knowing itself — not in what you know or what you get. That is the highest joy there is.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
Right now: awareness is happening. That happening itself — can it be your object of delight? Try resting in "knowing is occurring" rather than in what is known.

## Part 7: The Self's Only Parigraha Is the Self (G207–G209)

### G207

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
को णाम भिणज्ज बुहो परदव्वं मम इमं हविद दव्वं।
अप्पाणमप्पणो पिरगहं तु णियदं वियाणंतो।।२०७।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
को नाम भणेद्बुधः परद्रव्यं ममेदं भवति द्रव्यम्।
आत्मानमात्मनः परिग्रहं तु नियतं विजानन्।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
What wise person would say "this para-dravya is my dravya" — the one who knows, as a fixed fact, that the self's parigraha (possession) is the self alone?

**COMMENTARY:**
"Parigraha" means possession — what one owns, what belongs to oneself. The jñānī, knowing with certainty (niyatam) that the self's true possession is the self alone — cannot coherently claim para-dravya (other-substance: body, objects, relationships) as "mine." The claiming-of-mine would require the delusion that para-dravya is self. Having dissolved that delusion through correct self-knowledge, no such claim can arise.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Once you know what is truly yours — the self alone — claiming anything else as yours becomes as absurd as claiming someone else's home as yours.

---

### G208

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
मज्झं पिरग्गहो जिद तदो अहमजीवदं तु गच्छेज्ज।
णादेव अहं जम्हा तम्हा ण पिरग्गहो मज्झ।।२०८।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
मम परिग्रहो यदि ततोऽहमजीवतां तु गच्छेयम्।
ज्ञातैवाहं यस्मात्तस्मान्न परिग्रहो मम।।२०८।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
If that (para-dravya) were my parigraha, then I would become ajīva (insentient). Since I am jñāta (the knower) alone — therefore that is not my parigraha.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
A precise logical argument: "mine-ness" in the ultimate sense means becoming that thing (sharing its svabhāva). If the body were truly mine, I would become body-svabhāva (ajīva). But I am jñāta — consciousness, not matter. Therefore para-dravya cannot be truly mine. The jñānī sees: my "swatva" (own-ness) is my jñāyaka-bhāva. What has a different svabhāva cannot be my possession in the ultimate sense.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
If the body were truly mine, I would BE body — insentient. But I know. I am the knower. Therefore the body is not mine.

---

### G209

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
छिज्जदु वा भिज्जदु वा णिज्जदु वा अहव जादु विप्पलयं।
जम्हा तम्हा गच्छदु तह वि हु ण पिरग्गहो मज्झ।।२०९।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
छिद्यतां वा भिद्यतां वा नीयतां वाथवा यातु विप्रलयम्।
यस्मात्तस्मात् गच्छतु तथापि खलु न परिग्रहो मम।।२०९।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Let it be cut, broken, taken away, or destroyed — let it go wherever it goes — even so, it is certainly not my parigraha.

**COMMENTARY:**
The jñānī's non-possession is unconditional. No circumstance changes it. Whether para-dravya is present or absent, intact or destroyed — the jñānī knows it was never truly theirs. This is the opposite of conventional renunciation (which is still emotionally engaged with what it gives up). The jñānī hasn't given up anything — they have simply correctly seen that it was never theirs to begin with.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
The jñānī is unconcerned with gain or loss of things — not because of effort, but because they correctly know: it was never mine.

---

## Part 8: Jñānī Is Knower, Not Possessor — Five Types of Non-Possession (G210–G214)

### G210

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
अपिरग्गहो अणिच्छो भणिदो णाणी य णेच्छदे धम्मं।
अपिरग्गहो दु धम्मस्स जाणगो तेण सो होिद।।२१०।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The jñānī, having been declared aparigraha (non-possessive) and aniccha (desire-free), does not desire dharma (punya/merit). Therefore he is the knower of dharma — not its possessor.

**COMMENTARY:**
"Icchā parigraha" — desire is possession. To desire something is to grasp it as "mine" in the psychological sense. The jñānī, being aniccha (free from desire based in ajñāna), does not desire even dharma (punya). The desire for merit — for good karma, for virtuous outcomes — is itself a form of parigraha. The jñānī sees dharma as something that happens in the field of experience — they know it, without owning or craving it.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Even craving for virtue is a form of grasping. The jñānī doesn't grasp even merit — they simply know it.

---

### G211

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
अपिरग्गहो अणिच्छो भणिदो णाणी य णेच्छिद अधम्मं।
अपिरग्गहो अधम्मस्स जाणगो तेण सो होिद।।२११।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The jñānī does not desire adharma (pāpa/demerit). He is the knower of adharma — not its possessor.

**COMMENTARY:**
The same principle extends to adharma. The jñānī's non-possession covers both merit and demerit. Note the structural elegance: "aparigraha" applies equally to punya (G210) and pāpa (G211) and food (G212) and drink (G213). The categories are not ethical ones (good vs. bad things to possess) — the principle is ontological: the jñānī's relationship to all bhāvas is knowing, not possessing. This same teaching applies by substitution to dveṣa, krodha, māna, māyā, lobha, karma, nokarma, mind, speech, body, ear, eye, smell-organ, taste-organ, touch-organ — sixteen substitutions.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
The jñānī doesn't own their vices any more than their virtues. They are the witness of both.

---

### G212

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
अपिरग्गहो अणिच्छो भणिदो णाणी य णेच्छदे असणं।
अपिरग्गहो दु असणस्स जाणगो तेण सो होिद।।२१२।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The jñānī does not desire food. He is the knower of food — not its possessor.

**COMMENTARY:**
When a muni takes food, is there not desire? Food-hunger arises from karma-udaya (aśātāvedanīya karma producing hunger, vīryāntarāya karma producing difficulty in enduring it, and cāritramoha karma producing the craving). The jñānī knows this craving as karma-udaya's product — not as their own desire. They treat it like a disease (duḥkha), seeking to address it without nurturing it. Their icchā toward food has no "anuragātmaka icchā" (following-desire, sustained wanting) — hence they are the knower of food, not its possessor.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Even when hungry and eating, the jñānī knows hunger as karma's product and eating as its address — not as "I desire food."

---

### G213

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
अपिरग्गहो अणिच्छो भणिदो णाणी य णेच्छदे पाणं।
अपिरग्गहो दु पाणस्स जाणगो तेण सो होिद।।२१३।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The jñānī does not desire drink. He is the knower of drink — not its possessor.

*(Same structural principle as G212 — extends to drink/liquid.)*

---

### G214

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
एमादिए दु िविविहे सव्वे भावे य णेच्छदे णाणी।
जाणगभावो णियदो णीरालंबो दु सव्वत्थ।।२१४।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
एवमादिकांस्तु विविधान् सर्वान् भावांश्च नेच्छति ज्ञानी।
ज्ञायकभावो नियतो निरालम्बस्तु सर्वत्र।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
And thus the jñānī does not desire any of these many types of bhāvas. His jñāyaka-bhāva is fixed — completely nirālamba (without support/anchorage) everywhere.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
"Nirālamba" — without external support or anchorage. The jñāyaka-bhāva does not lean on anything outside itself. It does not depend on any external condition, object, or state for its stability. It is entirely self-sufficient, resting in its own svabhāva. This is the ultimate non-parigraha: not merely non-possession of things, but the soul's standing in its own knowing-nature without needing anything else as support.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
The jñānī's awareness needs nothing to lean on. It stands in itself. That is the deepest non-possession.

---

## Part 9: Viyoga-Buddhi — Detachment at All Three Times (G215–G216)

### G215

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
उप्पण्णोदयभोगो वियोगबुद्धीए तस्त सो णिच्चं।
कंखामणागदस्स य उदयस्स ण कुव्वदे णाणी।।२१५।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Toward the upabhoga (enjoyment/experience) that has arisen through karma-udaya — the jñānī always has viyoga-buddhi (detachment-understanding). And toward the anāgata (future) karma-udaya, the jñānī does not make kāṅkṣā (desire/craving).

**COMMENTARY:**
Viyoga-buddhi toward present experience: the jñānī knows present sense-experience as something that will end — and therefore does not develop anuragātmaka icchā (attachment-desire) toward it. Karma-udaya produces experience; the jñānī receives it with viyoga-consciousness (knowing-of-separation). Toward future experiences, the jñānī makes no kāṅkṣā — no anticipatory craving, no planning for pleasures, no fear of pains. Past experience is past; current experience is known as impermanent; future experience is not craved.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Present pleasures: "this will end, I know I am not this." Future pleasures: "I don't crave them." This is the jñānī's relationship to experience.

---

### G216

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
जो वेदिद वेदिज्जिद समए समए विणस्सदे उभयं।
तं जाणगो दु णाणी उभयं पि ण कंखिद कयािव।।२१६।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
यो वेदयते वेद्यते समये समये विनश्यत्युभयम्।
तद्ज्ञायकस्तु ज्ञानी उभयमपि न काङ्क्षति कदापि।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The vedaka-bhāva (experiencing-state) and the vedya-bhāva (the experienced-state) — both perish moment by moment. The jñānī, knowing this, never desires either of them.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
Both the experiencer-modification and the experienced-object are momentary — arising and perishing in each instant. The vedaka-bhāva (the soul's experiencing-state) is itself a vibhāva (non-essential modification) — impermanent. The vedya (what is experienced) is also impermanent. The jñānī sees this impermanence — and knowing there is nothing stable in either the experiencing or the experienced, makes no desire toward either. This is the deepest basis for non-craving.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Both the one who wants and the object of wanting vanish moment by moment. What is there to crave?

---

## Part 10: No Rāga in the Jñānī — Gold and Iron Analogies (G217–G219)

### G217

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
बंधुवभोगिणिमित्ते अझ्वसाणोदएसु णाणिस्स।
संसारदेहिवसएसु णेव उप्पज्जदे रागो।।२१७।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
In the adhyavasāna-udayas (arising of inner determinations) related to bandha-upabhoga-nimitta (causes of bondage and sensory enjoyment) and samsāra-deha-viṣaya (subjects of samsāra and body) — rāga does not arise in the jñānī.

**COMMENTARY:**
Samsāra subjects (worldly objects) and body subjects (sensations, physical conditions) — these are the standard triggers of rāga for the ajñānī. Even their mere mention (the adhyavasāna-udaya, the arising of inner determinations toward them) triggers attraction or aversion. In the jñānī, these inner-determinations relating to bondage and enjoyment do not produce rāga — because the jñānī knows all these things as pāra-svabhāva (other-nature), not as their own.

---

### G218

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
णाणी रागप्पजहो सव्वदव्वेसु कम्ममज्झगदो।
णो लिप्पिद रजएण दु कद्ममज्झे जहा कणयं।।२१८।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The jñānī — having abandoned rāga toward all dravyas — even when in the midst of karma — is not stained by karma-dust. Just as gold in the middle of mud is not stained.

---

### G219

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
अण्णाणी पुण रत्तो सव्वदव्वेसु कम्ममज्झगदो।
लिप्पिद कम्मरएण दु कद्ममज्झे जहा लोहं।।२१९।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The ajñānī — attached to all dravyas — even when in the midst of karma — is stained by karma-dust. Just as iron in mud gets stained.

**ANVAYARTHA (G218-G219):**
Gold in mud: gold's svabhāva is aliptatva (non-sticking). Mud cannot adhere to gold's nature. The gold may be surrounded by mud, visually covered — but its inner svabhāva (the quality of goldness) is untouched. Similarly, the jñānī in the midst of karma-situations: karma cannot adhere because the jñānī's svabhāva is rāga-tyāga (rāga-abandonment). The ajñānī's svabhāva is rāgopādāna-śīlatva (the tendency to grasp through rāga) — therefore karma adheres like rust on iron. The difference is internal, not circumstantial.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
In the same karma-ocean: the jñānī is gold — karma doesn't stick. The ajñānī is iron — karma sticks and rusts.

---

## Part 11: The Conch Analogy — Only Self-Abandonment Causes Fall (G220–G223)

### G220-G221

**PRAKRIT TEXT (G220):**
भुंजंतस्स िव िविविहे सिच्ताचित्तमिस्सिसए दव्वे।
संखस्स सेदभावो ण िव सक्किद किण्हगो काडंु।।२२०।।

**PRAKRIT TEXT (G221):**
तह णाणिस्स िव िविविहे सिच्ताचित्तमिस्सिसए दव्वे।
भुंजंतस्स िव णाणं ण सक्कमण्णाणदं णेदुं।।२२१।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G220):** Just as the whiteness of a conch — even while eating various saccitta (sentient), acitta (insentient), and mixed substances — cannot be made black by anyone.

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G221):** Similarly, the jñānī, even while enjoying various saccitta, acitta, and mixed substances — their jñāna cannot be made ajñāna by anyone (from outside).

### G222-G223

**PRAKRIT TEXT (G222):**
जइया स एव संखो सेदसहावं तयं पिजहिदूण।
गच्छेज्ज किण्हभावं तइया सुक्कत्तणं पिजहे।।२२२।।

**PRAKRIT TEXT (G223):**
तह णाणी िव हु जइया णाणसहावं तयं पिजहिदूण।
अण्णाणेण पिरणदो तइया अण्णाणदं गच्छे।।२२३।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G222):** But when the conch itself abandons its white svabhāva and takes on blackness — then it loses its whiteness.

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G223):** Similarly, when the jñānī itself abandons its jñāna-svabhāva and becomes transformed by ajñāna — then it attains ajñānatā (state of non-knowledge).

**COMMENTARY:**
The conch analogy is elegant: the conch's whiteness cannot be blackened by external substances — only when the conch itself transforms. Similarly, external experiences cannot make the jñānī ajñānī — only when the jñānī itself abandons jñāna-svabhāva. The key implication: the responsibility for spiritual fall is always internal, never external. No external object, experience, or circumstance causes the jñānī to lose their state — only their own abandonment of jñāna-nature does.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Nothing outside you can destroy your knowing. Only you can abandon it.

---

## Part 12: The King Analogy — Karma Gives Fruits Only to Those Who Serve It (G224–G227)

### G224-G225

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G224):** Just as a person serves the king for livelihood purposes — and the king gives him various happiness-producing enjoyments.

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G225):** Similarly, the jīva-puruṣa serves karma-rāja (karma-king) for the sake of sukha — and karma gives him various happiness-producing enjoyments.

### G226-G227

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G226):** But when that same person does NOT serve the king for livelihood purposes — the king does not give him the various enjoyments.

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G227):** Similarly, the samyag-dṛṣṭi does NOT serve karma-rāja for sense-objects — so karma does not give him the various happiness-producing enjoyments.

**COMMENTARY:**
"Serving karma" means seeking karma's products — craving pleasure from karma's fruits, doing actions with the motive of obtaining karma-produced sukha. The ajñānī serves karma (desires its products) → karma gives those products. The samyag-dṛṣṭi doesn't serve karma (doesn't seek karma-produced sukha as the goal of actions) → karma cannot deliver its rañjita-pariṇāma (rāga-tinged fruits) to someone who isn't craving them. Karma delivers to those who order from its menu. The jñānī has closed the menu.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Karma can only deliver to someone who's placed an order. The jñānī has stopped ordering.

---

## Part 13: Samyag-Dṛṣṭi Are Free from the Seven Fears (G228)

### G228

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
सम्मािदट्ठी जीवा णिस्सका होंित िणभ्भया तेण।
सत्तभयिवप्पमुक्का जम्हा तम्हा दु िणस्सका।।२२८।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
सम्यग्दृष्टयो जीवा निरशङ्का भवन्ति निर्भयास्तेन।
सप्तभयविप्रमुक्ता यस्मात्तस्मातु निरशङ्काः।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Samyag-dṛṣṭi jīvas are niḥśaṅka (free from doubt) and therefore nirbhaya (fearless). Because they are free from the seven fears — therefore they are niḥśaṅka.

**COMMENTARY:**
The seven fears: (1) this-world fear (loka-bhaya), (2) next-world fear (paraloka-bhaya), (3) agupti-bhaya (fear from lack of protection), (4) vedanā-bhaya (fear of suffering), (5) maraṇa-bhaya (fear of death), (6) ākasmika-bhaya (fear of sudden/unexpected events), (7) other fears related to karma. The samyag-dṛṣṭi is niḥśaṅka — free from all seven — because being established in jñāyaka-bhāva, they know: this consciousness cannot be harmed by any external condition. What could one who correctly knows the self truly fear?

**SIMPLY PUT:**
When you know you are the eternal knower, every fear dissolves. What is there to fear if you know you cannot be harmed?

## Part 14: The Eight Qualities of Samyag-Dṛṣṭi (G229–G236)

These eight gathas describe the eight qualities (aṅgas/guṇas) of samyag-darśana — each producing nirjarā instead of bandha.

### G229 — Niḥśaṅka (Doubt-free)

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
जो चत्तािर िव पाए छिदिद ते कम्मबंधमोहकरे।
सो िणस्सको चेदा सम्मािदिट्ठ मुणेदव्वो।।२२९।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The cetayitā (experiencing consciousness) who cuts the four pādas (foot-like causes) — the karma-bandha-moha-causing ones — is niḥśaṅka and should be known as samyag-dṛṣṭi.

**COMMENTARY:** The four pādas are the four forms of mithyātva (false views) that create confusion and doubt about the nature of karma-bondage. The cetayitā who cuts these doubt-causing misconceptions stands in niḥśaṅka — unwavering, doubt-free.

---

### G230 — Niḥkāṅkṣa (Craving-free)

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
जो दु ण करेिद कंखं कम्मफलेसु तह सव्वधम्मेसु।
सो िणकांखो चेदा सम्मािदिट्ठ मुणेदव्वो।।२३०।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The cetayitā who does not make kāṅkṣā (craving/desire) toward karma-fruits and toward all dharmas — is niḥkāṅkṣa and should be known as samyag-dṛṣṭi.

**COMMENTARY:** No desire for karma's products — no craving for pleasant birth-conditions, good fortune, or the fruits of virtue. No desire even for diverse dharmas as means to specific ends. The niḥkāṅkṣa quality: absence of craving-based bondage.

---

### G231 — Nirvidicikitsa (Disgust-free)

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
जो ण करेिद दुगुंछं चेदा सव्वेिसमेव धम्माणं।
सो खलु िणिव्विदेिगच्छो सम्मािदिट्ठ मुणेदव्वो।।२३१।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The cetayitā who does not make juguptsā (disgust/aversion/revulsion) toward all dharmas — is nirvidicikitsa (free from disgust) and should be known as samyag-dṛṣṭi.

**COMMENTARY:** No juguptsā toward the natural properties of things (hunger, thirst, heat, cold) and no disgust toward impure or unclean objects. The samyag-dṛṣṭi knows these as karma-udaya products — as events in the field, not as objects of personal revulsion.

---

### G232 — Amūḍha-Dṛṣṭi (Non-deluded vision)

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
जो हविद असम्मूढो चेदा सिदिट्ठ सव्वभावेसु।
सो खलु अमूिढदिट्ठ सम्मािदिट्ठ मुणेदव्वो।।२३२।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The cetayitā who is asammūḍha (non-deluded) in all states and has yathārtha-dṛṣṭi (correct vision) — is amūḍha-dṛṣṭi and should be known as samyag-dṛṣṭi.

**COMMENTARY:** Not deluded by external forms, not taking karma-udaya effects for the nature of things. Correct vision about the true svabhāva of each substance — knowing dev, guru, śāstra, and worldly events for what they actually are, not through the distorted lens of moha.

---

### G233 — Upagūhana (Concealment/Protection)

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
जो िसद्धभित्तजुत्तो उवगूहणगो दु सव्वधम्माणं।
सो उवगूहणकारी सम्मािदिट्ठ मुणेदव्वो।।२३३।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The cetayitā who is endowed with siddha-bhakti (devotion to the Siddhas) and is upagūhanaka (concealer/protector) of all dharmas — is upagūhana-kārī and should be known as samyag-dṛṣṭi.

**COMMENTARY:** Upagūhana means concealment — protecting dharma from being defamed, not exposing the faults of fellow practitioners, nurturing the growth of the path. The basis is siddha-bhakti: devotion directed toward liberated souls focused on pure consciousness.

---

### G234 — Sthitikāraṇa (Stabilization)

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
उम्मग्गं गच्छंतं सगं पि मग्गे ठवेिद जो चेदा।
सो ठिदकरणाजुत्तो सम्मािदिट्ठ मुणेदव्वो।।२३४।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The cetayitā who places even those going astray (unmārga) — even their own ātmā — back on the right path — is sthitikāraṇa-yukta and should be known as samyag-dṛṣṭi.

**COMMENTARY:** "Even their own ātmā" — even when the samyag-dṛṣṭi's own soul slips from the moksha-path (sthiti), they bring it back. Self-stabilization is the primary meaning; stabilization of others is secondary.

---

### G235 — Vātsalya (Affection for the Path)

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
जो कुणिद वच्छलत्तं ितण्हं साहूण मोक्खमग्गम्हि।
सो वच्छलभावजुदो सम्मािदिट्ठ मुणेदव्वो।।२३५।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The cetayitā who performs vātsalya (affection/fondness) toward the three sādhas on the moksha-mārga — is vātsalya-bhāva-yuta and should be known as samyag-dṛṣṭi.

**COMMENTARY:** Vātsalya = deep affection-love toward the three sādhas (samyag-darśana, samyak-jñāna, samyak-cāritra). The samyag-dṛṣṭi loves the moksha-path itself. This love is prīti (devotion-love) directed at the path as one's own svabhāva-rūpa (of the nature of one's own nature).

---

### G236 — Prabhāvana/Jina-Jñāna-Prabhāvin (Propagation of Knowledge)

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
िवज्जारहमारूढो मणोरहपहेसु भमइ जो चेदा।
सो िजणणाणपहावी सम्मािदिट्ठ मुणेदव्वो।।२३६।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
विद्यारथमारूढः मनोरथपथेषु भ्रमति यश्चेतयिता।
स जिनज्ञानप्रभावी सम्यग्दृष्टिज्ञातव्यः।।२३६।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The cetayitā who, mounted on the vidyā-ratha (knowledge-chariot), roams in the paths of manoratha (thought-paths/mental paths) — is jina-jñāna-prabhāvin (one who propagates/manifests Jina-knowledge) and should be known as samyag-dṛṣṭi.

**COMMENTARY:**
The eighth quality: prabhāvana — manifesting, propagating, making radiant the Jina's knowledge. The cetayitā who is "mounted on the vidyā-chariot" — i.e., established in jñāna as their vehicle — and roams in the mental paths (investigating, contemplating, applying): this is the samyag-dṛṣṭi who spreads the influence of Jina-jñāna. The samyag-dṛṣṭi's own practice is itself a propagation — by making jñāna radiant in their own consciousness, they embody and propagate it.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
The eight quality: let your knowledge radiate. Mount the knowledge-chariot and let it move through your thinking — that itself is prabhāvana.

**Featured Verse:** G199 — "Rāga is pudgala-karma. Its vipāka-udaya is this bhāva. But this is not my bhāva. I am certainly one — the jñāyaka-bhāva alone."

**Central Doctrine:** Nirjarā is the natural consequence of jñāyaka-svabhāva. The soul that knows itself as pure knower — not as the experiencer of karma's products, not as the owner of body/objects/dharma/adharma — naturally sheds karma through experience without generating new bondage. The eight qualities of samyag-darśana are not ethical ideals but the direct expression of jñāyaka-bhāva in all dimensions of life.
