# Samaysaar — Adhikar 7: Bandh (Karma-Bondage)
## बन्ध अधिकार | Gathas 237–287 (51 Gathas)

**Central Doctrine:** Rāgādi-adhyavasāna (inner determination colored by attachment) is the SOLE cause of karma-bondage — not external activity, not physical harm, not location, not the vastu (object). Whether beings are killed or not, bondage arises through adhyavasāna alone. The jñānī (one established in ātma-jñāna) does not bind karma even while engaged in activity, just as the oil-free man doing exercise in dust does not accumulate dust on his body.

---

## Part 1: G237–G241 — The Oil-Smeared Man: Sneha-Bhāva Is the Cause of Rajo-Bandha

### G237
**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
जह णाम को वि पुरिसो णेहबत्तो दु रेणुबहुलम्मि।
ठाणम्मि ठाइदूण य करेिद सत्थेिहं वायामं।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
यथा नाम कोऽपि पुरुषः स्नेहयुक्तस्तु रेणुबहुले।
स्थाने स्थित्वा च करोति शस्त्रैः व्यायामम्।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Just as a certain man, smeared with oil, standing in a place full of dust, performs exercise with weapons.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
As one slick with oil (sneha = oil/attachment) exercises amid dust — so the mithyādṛṣṭi moves through the world smeared with rāga.

**COMMENTARY:**
Kundakunda opens Adhikar 7 with a vivid analogy. The oil-smeared man is active — doing real work, wielding weapons. The dust is real. The question is: what causes the dust to stick?

**SIMPLY PUT:**
A man covered in oil goes to a dusty arena and exercises with weapons.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
Before this verse asks what sticks to you — ask what you are smeared with.

---

### G238
**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
छिंदिद भिंदिद य तदा तालीतलकयिलवंसिपंडीओ।
सिच्चाचित्ताणं करेिद दव्वाणमुवघादं।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
छिनत्ति भिनत्ति च तदा तालीतालकयलीवंशपिण्डान्।
सचित्ताचित्तानां करोति द्रव्याणामुपघातम्।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
He cuts and splits palmyra, tāla, plantain, and bamboo clusters — causing harm (upghāta) to sacit (sentient) and acit (insentient) substances.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
The oil-smeared man does real work — he cuts living trees (sacit) and inert objects (acit). He causes genuine upghāta. The activity is real, verifiable, outwardly visible.

**COMMENTARY:**
This is crucial: Kundakunda does not deny that the action happens. Trees are cut. Harm occurs. The verse acknowledges the full reality of the external act. This sets up the philosophical point — if the action is real, what then is the cause of dust-bondage?

**SIMPLY PUT:**
He cuts living and non-living things — causing real harm to both.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
The action is real. The harm is real. The question is not whether harm happened — but what made it stick to you.

---

### G239
**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
उवघादं कुव्वंतस्स तस्स णाणाविहेिहं करणेिहं।
िणच्छयदो िचंतेज्ज हु िकंपच्चयगो दु रयबंधो।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
उपघातं कुर्वतस्तस्य नानाविधैः करणैः।
निश्चयतः चिन्तयेत् खलु किम्प्रत्ययको रजोबन्धः।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
For this one causing upghāta by many types of instruments — by niścaya (absolute standpoint), reflect: what is the real cause of rajo-bandha (dust-bondage)?

**ANVAYARTHA:**
Having established that the action is real, Kundakunda now asks the niścaya question: among all the causes (instruments, exercise, location, objects), which one is truly responsible for the dust sticking to the man's body?

**COMMENTARY:**
The word "niścayadaḥ" is pivotal. Niścaya-naya pierces through appearances to find the essential cause. The exercise, the weapons, the trees — all are vyavahāra causes. By niścaya — what is the pratyaya (cause) of rajo-bandha?

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Among all instruments and activities — by niścaya, ask: what really caused the dust to bind?

**CONTEMPLATE:**
Most spiritual inquiry stops at the visible cause. Niścaya demands the invisible one.

---

### G240
**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
जो सो दु णेहभावो तम्हि णरे तेण तस्स रयबंधो।
िणच्छयदो िवण्णेयं ण कायचेट्ठाइहं सेसािहं।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
यः स तु स्नेहभावः तस्मिन् नरे तेन तस्य रजोबन्धः।
निश्चयतः विज्ञेयं न कायचेष्टादिभिः शेषैः।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
That sneha-bhāva (the nature of oil/attachment) in that man — by that alone is his rajo-bandha. By niścaya, know: not by the remaining bodily activities and so on.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
Answer delivered: the sneha (oiliness = attachment-quality) in the man is what causes dust to bind. Not the exercise, not the weapons, not the trees he cut — the oil on his skin. Similarly: rāga in the soul is the sole cause of karma-bondage. Activity is not.

**COMMENTARY:**
This is the master-verse of the analogy. Sneha-bhāva = rāga-bhāva in the soul. The dust = karma-pudgalas. The exercise = yoga (activity of body-speech-mind). By niścaya: it is the sneha (rāga) — not the yoga — that causes karma-bondage. This directly refutes Mīmāṃsaka and other schools that hold physical action as the cause of bondage.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
The oiliness on the man's body is what caused dust to stick — not his exercise. Know this by niścaya: not the other bodily activities.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
You can cease all activity. You can sit still in a cave. If rāga remains, karma will still bind. If rāga is absent, karma will not bind even in full activity.

---

### G241
**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
एवं िमच्छािदट्ठी वट्टंतो बहुिवहासु िचट्ठासु।
रागादी उवओगे कुव्वंतो िलप्पिद रएण।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
एवं मिथ्यादृष्टिः वर्तमानः बहुविधासु चेष्टासु।
रागादीन् उपयोगे कुर्वन् लिप्यते रजसा।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Similarly, the mithyādṛṣṭi, engaging in many types of activities, making rāgādi in his upayoga (consciousness), is stained by karma-dust.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
The analogy is applied: mithyādṛṣṭi = oil-smeared man. Many activities = exercise in dusty arena. Rāgādi in upayoga = the oil on the skin. The staining is inevitable.

**COMMENTARY:**
Upayoga (the activity of consciousness) is the key word. When consciousness is colored by rāga-dveṣa-moha, every action — whether outwardly good or bad, violent or nonviolent — results in karma-binding. The mithyādṛṣṭi is smeared with the inner oil of rāga.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Just so — the mithyādṛṣṭi, engaged in many activities while keeping rāga in consciousness, gets coated with karma.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
It is not what you do. It is what your consciousness is colored with while you do it.

## Part 2: G242–G246 — The Man Without Oil: Samyag-Dṛṣṭi Does Not Bind

### G242–G243
**PRAKRIT TEXT (G242):**
जह णाम सो चिय पुरिसो िणक्खित्तणेहो हु तम्हि चिय।
ठाणम्मि ठाइदूण य करेिद सत्थेिहं वायामं।।

**PRAKRIT TEXT (G243):**
छिंदिद भिंदिद य तदा तालीतलकयिलवंसिपंडीओ।
सिच्चाचित्ताणं करेिद दव्वाणमुवघादं।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Just as that same man, having removed/abandoned the oil, standing in that same dusty place, performs exercise with weapons (G242) — cuts palmyra, tāla, plantain, bamboo — causes upghāta to sacit and acit dravyas (G243).

**ANVAYARTHA:**
Everything is the same — the dusty arena, the exercise, the cutting, the trees, the harm. The only difference: the oil has been removed. Now: does dust bind?

**COMMENTARY:**
Kundakunda holds all variables constant and changes only one: the sneha (oil). This is a controlled philosophical experiment. The upghāta is real. The activity is real. But what happens?

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Same man, same place, same exercise, same trees cut — but now without oil. Same action, different inner state.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
The outer action has not changed. Only the inner coating has changed. Watch what changes.

---

### G244–G245
**PRAKRIT TEXT (G244):**
उवघादं कुव्वंतस्स तस्स णाणाविहेिहं करणेिहं।
िणच्छयदो िचंतेज्ज हु िकंपच्चयगो दु रयबंधो।।

**PRAKRIT TEXT (G245):**
जो सो दु णेहभावो तम्हि णरे तेण तस्स रयबंधो।
िणच्छयदो िवण्णेयं ण कायचेट्ठाइहं सेसािहं।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
For this one (now without oil) causing upghāta by many instruments — by niścaya, reflect: what is the cause of rajo-bandha? (G244) — The sneha-bhāva that was in that man was the cause of rajo-bandha — by niścaya; the remaining bodily activities are NOT the cause. (G245)

**ANVAYARTHA:**
Answer: there is no rajo-bandha for the oil-free man. Because the sneha-bhāva is gone. The activities are irrelevant — they never were the cause. Now confirmed by the counter-case.

**COMMENTARY:**
The parallel structure with G239-G240 is deliberate. G244 asks the same question as G239. G245 gives the same answer as G240 — but now it serves as confirmation: the sneha-bhāva is the sole cause, proved by both presence and absence.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Ask the same question for the oil-free man — and realize: no oil, no dust-bondage. Bodily activity was never the cause.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
Both cases prove the same thing. Oil causes binding. No oil — no binding. The activity was irrelevant all along.

---

### G246
**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
एवं सम्मािदट्ठी वट्टंतो बहुिवहासु िचट्ठासु।
ण करेिद रागमादी उवओगे ण िलप्पिद रएण।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
एवं सम्यग्दृष्टिः वर्तमानः बहुविधासु चेष्टासु।
न करोति रागादीन् उपयोगे न लिप्यते रजसा।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Similarly, the samyag-dṛṣṭi, engaged in many activities, not making rāgādi in upayoga, is not stained by karma-dust.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
The application is complete. Samyag-dṛṣṭi = oil-free man. Rāgādi absent from upayoga = no oil on skin. Many activities = same exercise. Result: no karma-binding.

**COMMENTARY:**
This verse is the liberation-promise of Adhikar 7. The samyag-dṛṣṭi does not need to stop activity. He does not need to cease cutting, moving, speaking. His consciousness, established in ātma-svarūpa, does not take rāga into its upayoga. Therefore karma does not bind. This is the niścaya understanding of bandha.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Similarly — samyag-dṛṣṭi, active in many ways, not making rāga in consciousness, is not stained by karma.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
Freedom is not in stopping action. Freedom is in purifying the upayoga — the quality of consciousness itself.

## Part 3: G247–G249 — "I Kill / I Am Killed" — Mithyā-Adhyavasāya Type 1

### G247
**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
जो मण्णदे हणामि त्ति जो मण्णदे हण्णामि चेव त्ति।
उभो वि मूढमदी अण्णाणी ते विवरीया।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
One who thinks "I kill" and one who thinks "I am killed" — both are of mūḍha-mati (confused mind), both are ajñānī — the opposite (jñānī) is the knower.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
"I kill" — mūḍha. "I am killed" — also mūḍha. Both are in ajñāna. The jñānī knows differently.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
The Gītā's famous teaching echoes here, but Kundakunda grounds it in ātma-jñāna: you cannot kill because you are not the body's kartā.

---

### G248–G249
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (summary):**
Death occurs through āyu-karma-kṣaya alone. No one can take another's āyu-karma or remove it. The one who "kills" has not touched the āyu-karma. The āyu was going to exhaust at that moment — that is the only cause of death.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
Death = āyu-karma-udaya completing. The so-called "killer" does not access another's āyu-karma. He cannot extend or reduce another's āyuḥ. Therefore "I killed" is mithyā-adhyavasāna.

**COMMENTARY:**
This is not a denial of cause-and-effect at the vyavahāra level. At niścaya: each jīva exhausts its own āyu-karma at the moment it must. No external agent can alter this.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Death comes when āyu-karma is exhausted — by that alone. You cannot take another's āyu-karma. So "I killed" is a false inner determination.

---

## Part 4: G250–G252 — "I Give Life / Others Give Me Life" — Mithyā-Adhyavasāya Type 2

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (summary):**
One who thinks "I give life to others" or "others give me life" — both are mūḍha-ajñānī. Life continues through āyuḥ-karma-udaya alone. No one can give another's āyuḥ. Life is not in another's hands.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
The flip side of Type 1. Not only "I kill" but "I give life" — both are mithyā. Life is not a gift you can give or receive. It unfolds through each jīva's own karma-udaya.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
"I give life" — also mūḍha. Life comes from one's own āyuḥ-karma — no one can give it to you.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
You cannot give life. You cannot take it. The inner claim of being a giver or taker of life — is the very root of karma-bondage.

---

## Part 5: G253–G256 — "I Make Others Happy/Miserable" — Mithyā-Adhyavasāya Type 3

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (summary):**
G253: One who thinks "I make beings miserable" or "I make beings happy" — mūḍha-ajñānī.
G254-G256: Happiness and misery arise through karma-udaya alone. You cannot give another their karma-result. The one who is miserable is so through his own karma-udaya. You cannot inject suffering into another's consciousness.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
Even well-intentioned action — "I made them happy" — is mūḍha-adhyavasāna if held as kartā-bhāva. Karma-udaya alone delivers happiness or misery to each jīva's experience.

**COMMENTARY:**
This completes the three-fold refutation: hiṃsā (killing), jīvana (giving life), and sukha-duḥkha (happiness/misery). In all three, the inner claim of being the kartā (doer) for others is mithyā and is itself the cause of karma-bondage.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Happiness and misery come to each jīva through their own karma. "I made them happy" — mūḍha.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
The most compassionate action, done with "I am making them happy" — binds karma if held as kartā-bhāva. Compassion without kartā-bhāva is the samyag-dṛṣṭi's path.

## Part 6: G257–G258 — Both Sides of Mūḍha-Mati Bind

### G257
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
One who dies and is miserable — all by his own karma-udaya alone. So the claim "I killed him, I made him miserable" — is false (mithyā).

### G258
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
One who does not die and is not miserable — also by his own karma-udaya. So the claim "I did not kill him, I did not cause him misery" — is equally a claim in the same error (mithyā).

**COMMENTARY:**
This is subtle and profound. G258 shows that even the negation — "I didn't kill" — if held as a self-referential claim about one's own power, is still the same mūḍha-mati operating in the opposite direction. The jñānī doesn't say "I killed" or "I didn't kill" — he knows neither is his kartavya (domain of agency).

**SIMPLY PUT:**
"I killed him" — false. "I didn't kill him" — also false as a claim about your power. Both are mūḍha-mati.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
Even taking credit for non-harm is ego's subtle game. The jñānī transcends both claims.

---

## Part 7: G259–G262 — Adhyavasāna Alone Is Bandha (Niścaya Summary)

### G259
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
That mūḍha-buddhi (the wrong inner conviction: "I make beings miserable/happy") — that mūḍha-mati — binds shubha-ashubha karma.

### G260
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Whatever adhyavasāna one makes with the intention of "making beings miserable/happy" — that becomes pāpa-bandha or puṇya-bandha.

### G261
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Similarly for "I kill / I give life" — one adhyavasāna can cause both puṇya or pāpa bondage depending on its quality.

### G262 — FEATURED VERSE
**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
अज्झिवसदेण बंधो सत्ते मारेउ मा व मारेउ।
एसो बंधसमासो जीवाणं िणच्छयणयस्स।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
अध्यवसानेन बन्धः सत्त्वान् मारयतु मा वा मारयतु।
एषः बन्धसमासः जीवानां निश्चयनयस्य।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
By adhyavasāna (inner determination) alone is bondage — whether beings are killed or not. This is the brief (samāsa) summary of bondage for all jīvas according to niścaya-naya.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
Kundakunda compresses all of Adhikar 7's doctrine into one verse: bandha = adhyavasāna. The killing or not killing of beings is irrelevant to karma-bondage. Adhyavasāna is the sole cause.

**COMMENTARY:**
"Satte māreü mā va māreü" — whether beings die or not. This is the revolutionary statement. Even if no being is killed, if the adhyavasāna of violence is present, karma binds. And even if beings do die in proximity to you, if the adhyavasāna is absent, karma does not bind. The niścaya-naya cuts through all external criteria and establishes adhyavasāna as the only relevant variable.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Bondage happens through inner determination alone — whether beings are killed or not. This is the niścaya-naya's complete definition of bondage.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
The external event is irrelevant. The courtroom of karma has only one witness — your adhyavasāna. It does not examine what happened outside. It only asks: what was your inner determination?

## Part 8: G263–G265 — External Vastu Is Not the Cause

### G263
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
In alīka (falsehood), adatta (theft), abrahmacarya (non-celibacy), parigraha (possessiveness) — the adhyavasāna binds pāpa-karma.

### G264
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
In satya (truth), datta (charity), brahmacarya (celibacy), aparigraha (non-possessiveness) — the adhyavasāna binds puṇya-karma.

### G265
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The soul's adhyavasāna takes a vastu (object/situation) as its basis — but bondage is NOT from the vastu; bondage is from the adhyavasāna alone.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
G263-G264 establish that even good actions (satya, datta, brahmacarya) bind puṇya-karma if the adhyavasāna is externally oriented. G265 is the key: the vastu (truth, falsehood, the object) is merely the occasion — not the cause. Adhyavasāna uses vastu as basis but bandha is from adhyavasāna alone.

**COMMENTARY:**
This extends the doctrine beyond killing/not-killing to all ethical categories. Lying binds pāpa through the adhyavasāna of lying, not through the untruth itself. Giving charity binds puṇya through the adhyavasāna, not through the act of giving. The vastu is the stage; adhyavasāna is the player.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Falsehood, theft, non-celibacy, possessiveness → adhyavasāna binds pāpa. Truth, charity, celibacy, non-possessiveness → adhyavasāna binds puṇya. But: bondage is NOT from the vastu — bondage is from adhyavasāna alone.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
You can do good actions and still bind karma (puṇya-karma, which still keeps the soul in saṃsāra). Liberation requires not just good action but ātma-centered adhyavasāna.

## Part 9: G266–G267 — Mūḍha-Mati Is Purposeless

### G266
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
"I make beings miserable/happy, I bind them/free them" — this mūḍha-mati is nirarthaka (purposeless) and mithyā (false for you — the soul).

### G267
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
If beings bind through adhyavasāna-karma and become free when established on the moksha-path — what do YOU do? (Emphasizes akartā-bhāva — you are not the doer of others' bondage or liberation.)

**ANVAYARTHA:**
G267 is the philosophical punchline. Each jīva binds through its own adhyavasāna and frees itself by its own niścaya-established moksha-path. You — the one claiming "I bind/free them" — have no actual mechanism of agency here. Your mūḍha-mati is not just wrong; it is pointless.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
If each jīva binds by its own adhyavasāna and frees itself by its own path — what exactly are you doing when you claim "I bind/free them"? Nothing. It is purposeless.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
This teaching is not an excuse for indifference. It is a call to focus: your only real work is your own adhyavasāna. You cannot do anyone else's inner work.

## Part 10: G268–G270 — Soul Makes All Bhāvas As Itself Through Adhyavasāna

### G268
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Soul, through adhyavasāna, makes itself tiryañca (animal), nāraka (hell-being), deva (deity), manuṣya (human) — all the many types of puṇya and pāpa.

### G269
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
And similarly dharma-adharma, jīva-ajīva, loka-aloka — the soul makes all these as itself through adhyavasāna. (The soul, through its inner determination, experiences and identifies with all the varied realities of existence.)

### G270
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Those in whom these adhyavasānas are absent — those muni-kunjaras (elephants among munis, the great monks) — are not stained by shubha or ashubha karma.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
The positive vision: the mahāmunis who have transcended all mūḍha-adhyavasāna — not just ashubha (bad) but even shubha (good) karma-binding adhyavasāna — are free from all karma-staining. Their consciousness has no oil. The dust of karma does not adhere.

**COMMENTARY:**
"Muni-kunjara" — elephants among munis — expresses the majesty of those who have truly transcended adhyavasāna. They still move through the world. But their upayoga is established in ātma-svarūpa, free from both rāga and dveṣa at the level of adhyavasāna.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
The soul makes itself tiryañc, nāraka, deva, human — through adhyavasāna. The great munis in whom these adhyavasānas are absent — they bind neither shubha nor ashubha karma.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
Liberation is not about becoming a certain kind of being. It is about ceasing the adhyavasāna that makes you one thing and then another.

## Part 11: G271–G272 — Adhyavasāna Defined; Vyavahāra Prohibited

### G271
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Buddhi, vyavasāya, adhyavasāna, mati, vijñāna, citta, bhāva, pariṇāma — all these are ekārtha (synonyms, pointing to one and the same inner state).

**ANVAYARTHA:**
A comprehensive synonymy. All the terms used in philosophical discourse for the inner state of the soul — its determination, intention, consciousness-mode, thought-form — are one reality. Adhyavasāna is not a technical term divorced from lived experience. It is what you ordinarily call "what you were thinking/feeling."

### G272
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Vyavahāra-naya is thus prohibited by niścaya-naya. The munis established in niścaya-naya attain nirvāṇa.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
Vyavahāra-naya's external descriptions (actions, objects, rituals as causes of bondage/liberation) are overridden by niścaya-naya's internal insight: adhyavasāna alone is the cause. The path to nirvāṇa is through niścaya-established understanding.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
All the inner-state words (buddhi, mati, citta, bhāva, pariṇāma) mean the same thing: adhyavasāna. Vyavahāra-naya is overruled by niścaya. Munis in niścaya attain nirvāṇa.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
The tradition has many words for the inner state. They all point to one thing. Your inner determination — right now — is your karma-cause.

## Part 12: G273–G275 — Abhavya and the Limits of Vyavahāra-Dharma

### G273
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Even the abhavya (one incapable of liberation) who performs all vrata (vows), samiti (carefulness), gupti (restraint), śīla (conduct), and tapa (austerity) is ajñānī and mithyādṛṣṭi — lacking jñāna-śraddhā (right knowledge and faith in ātmā).

### G274
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The abhavya studies all 11 aṅgas (canonical texts) — but scriptural study does not produce guṇa (spiritual quality) for one who lacks jñāna-śraddhā regarding moksha.

### G275
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The abhavya has śraddhā in dharma — but as bhoga-nimitta (for the purpose of sensual fruition, reaching heavens), not as karma-kṣaya-nimitta (for the purpose of destroying karma toward liberation).

**ANVAYARTHA:**
These three gathas establish that external practice — even perfect external practice — without the correct adhyavasāna (jñāna-śraddhā oriented toward karma-kṣaya, not toward bhoga) is insufficient. The abhavya's śraddhā is directed at enjoyment-fruits, not liberation. Therefore all his practice merely generates better-quality saṃsāra, not liberation.

**COMMENTARY:**
This is not a teaching about fatalism regarding abhavya. It is a teaching about intention-quality. If one's dharma-practice is oriented toward puṇya-fruits (heavenly pleasures), it is still mithyā-oriented at the core, regardless of external perfection.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Even perfect external practice without right jñāna-śraddhā produces nothing. The abhavya's faith is aimed at better rebirth, not liberation — so all his practice is misdirected at the root.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
Check your adhyavasāna behind practice. Are you practicing for what? The destination of your inner intention determines what the practice builds.

## Part 13: G276–G277 — Vyavahāra vs. Niścaya Descriptions of Jñāna-Darśana-Cāritra

### G276
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Vyavahāra-naya: Āyāra (Ācārāṅga) and other scriptures = jñāna; jīva-ādi nava padārtha (nine realities) = darśana; ṣaṭ-jīvanikāya (six classes of living beings, cared for properly) = cāritra.

### G277
**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Niścaya-naya: Ātmā is my jñāna, ātmā is my darśana and cāritra; ātmā is my pratyākhyāna (renunciation); ātmā is my samvara (stoppage of karma) and yoga (union).

**ANVAYARTHA:**
Vyavahāra defines jñāna, darśana, cāritra in terms of external objects and texts. Niścaya defines all three — and pratyākhyāna, samvara, yoga — as ātmā itself. Not as ātmā's attributes or ātmā's practice — but as ātmā. The soul IS its own jñāna. The soul IS its own right vision. The soul IS its own right conduct.

**COMMENTARY:**
This is the ontological statement. When the soul is established in its own svarūpa, jñāna = ātmā (not a text), darśana = ātmā (not a doctrine), cāritra = ātmā (not an action). Practice in vyavahāra is the means; ātmā-svarūpa is the reality. The niścaya teaching does not abolish vyavahāra — it reveals what vyavahāra points toward.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Vyavahāra says jñāna = scripture, darśana = doctrine of nine realities, cāritra = care for six life-classes. Niścaya says: ātmā is my jñāna, ātmā is my darśana, ātmā is my cāritra, my renunciation, my samvara, my yoga.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
You are not reading about the truth. If the reading is rightly done, you ARE the truth that is being described.

## Part 14: G278–G287 — Crystal Analogy, Jñānī Akāraka, Apratikramaṇa, Adhaḥkarma

### G278–G279 — The Sphaṭika (Crystal) Analogy

**PRAKRIT TEXT (G278):**
जह फिलहमणी सुद्धो ण सयं पिरणमिद रागमादीिहं।
रंिगज्जिद अण्णेिहं दु सो रत्तादीिहं दव्वेिहं।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G278):**
Just as the pure crystal (sphaṭika-maṇi) does not itself transform into rāga-rūpa (red form) — but by other dravyas (red substances placed near it) it appears reddened.

**PRAKRIT TEXT (G279):**
एवं णाणी सुद्धो ण सयं पिरणमिद रागमादीिहं।
राइज्जिद अण्णेिहं दु सो रागादीिहं दोसेिहं।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G279):**
Similarly, the jñānī is pure — does not itself transform into rāga — but by other rāgādi-doṣas (which are para-dravya, belonging to the pudgala-karma) it appears to be rāgī (one having rāga).

**ANVAYARTHA:**
The crystal's redness is not the crystal's own transformation — it is the reflected quality of the external red object placed near it. The crystal remains crystal. Similarly, rāga appears in the jñānī's apparent expression — but it is the movement of pudgala-karma, not the jñānī's own pariṇāma.

**COMMENTARY:**
This analogy does not mean the jñānī "has no rāga" in the ordinary sense. It means that at the niścaya level, the soul's svarūpa remains pure — it does not fundamentally transform into a rāga-substance. The rāga that appears is from para-dravya influence. The jñānī knows this distinction and therefore is not bound as the ajñānī is.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Crystal stays crystal — it doesn't become red. It just reflects red. Jñānī's soul stays pure — it doesn't transform into rāga. What appears as rāga is para-dravya (pudgala-karma) — not the soul's own pariṇāma.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
What appears colored in you — is it you, or the influence of external substances? The jñānī has this discrimination. The ajñānī has identified completely with the color.

---

### G280 — Jñānī Is Akāraka

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
ण य रागदोसमोहं कुव्विद णाणी कसायभावं वा।
सयमप्पणो ण सो तेण कारगो तेिसं भावाणं।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The jñānī does not create rāga, dveṣa, moha, or kaṣāya-bhāva in itself. Therefore it is not the kāraka (doer/maker) of those bhāvas.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
Building on the crystal analogy: since rāga appears from para-dravya influence (not from the soul's own svarūpa-pariṇāma), the jñānī is not the kāraka of rāga-dveṣa-moha. These arise from karma's udaya (fruition), which is pudgala's movement — not the soul's creation.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
The jñānī does not create rāga-dveṣa-moha or kaṣāya. Therefore it is not the maker of those states.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
"I am not the maker of my anger" — this is not an excuse. It is a diagnostic. Recognize where it comes from. Then it begins to lose its grip.

---

### G281–G282 — Ajñānī Binds Again and Again

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G281):**
Ajñānī who transforms in rāga, dveṣa, kaṣāya-karma-bhāvas — in those bhāvas — binds rāgādi karma again and again.

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G282):**
The ātmā/cetayitā (the soul as the experiencer) transforming in those rāga-dveṣa-kaṣāya-bhāvas — binds rāgādi karma.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
The contrast: while the jñānī does not create rāga (G280), the ajñānī does transform in rāga — and that transformation is the mechanism of karma-binding. To pariṇama (undergo transformation) in rāga = to bind rāga-karma = to continue the saṃsāra cycle.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
The ajñānī transforms into rāga-dveṣa-kaṣāya — and that transformation binds karma again and again.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
Two kinds of contact with rāga: knowing it as para-dravya (jñānī's way) and transforming into it (ajñānī's way). Only the second binds.

### G283–G285 — Apratikramaṇa-Apratyākhyāna Teaching

**PRAKRIT TEXT (G283):**
अप्पिडकमणं दुिवहं अपच्चखाणं तहेव िवण्णेयं।
एदेणुवदेसेण य अकारगो िवण्णदो चेदा।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G283):**
Apratikramaṇa (non-repentance) is of two kinds — dravya and bhāva. Apratyākhyāna (non-renunciation) likewise — know these. By this teaching, jīva is known as akāraka (non-doer).

**PRAKRIT TEXT (G284):**
अप्पिडकमणं दुिवहं दव्वे भावे अपच्चखाणं िप।
एदेणुवदेसेण य अकारगो िवण्णदो चेदा।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G284):**
Two-fold apratikramaṇa — in dravya and bhāva — and apratyākhyāna also. By this teaching, jīva is known as akāraka.

**PRAKRIT TEXT (G285):**
जावं अप्पिडकमणं अपच्चखाणं च दव्वभावाणं।
कुव्विद आदा तावं कत्ता सो होिद णादव्वो।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G285):**
As long as ātmā performs apratikramaṇa and apratyākhyāna of dravya and bhāva — until then it is known as kartā (the doer).

**ANVAYARTHA:**
The two-fold distinction (dravya and bhāva) is critical:
- Dravya-apratikramaṇa: failure to perform the external ritual of pratikramaṇa (repentance)
- Bhāva-apratikramaṇa: failure to undergo inner repentance — the real apratikramaṇa

As long as both dravya and bhāva apratikramaṇa are being "done" (or left undone in the context of this adhyavasāna), the soul is kartā. When bhāva-pratikramaṇa is complete — the soul knows itself as akāraka.

**COMMENTARY:**
The teaching here is about the deepest level of apratikramaṇa: recognizing through niścaya that the soul was never truly the kāraka of karma. This recognition IS the true bhāva-pratikramaṇa. It is the inner repentance that dissolves the very root of doership.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Apratikramaṇa and apratyākhyāna are both of two kinds: dravya (external) and bhāva (inner). By this understanding — jīva is akāraka. As long as these are still operating in the soul, it is kartā. When resolved — akāraka.

### G286–G287 — Adhaḥkarma and Uddeśika Are Para-Dravya

**PRAKRIT TEXT (G286):**
आधाकम्मादीया पोग्गलदव्वस्स जे इमे दोसा।
कह ते कुव्विद णाणी परदव्वगुणा दु जे िणच्चं।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G286):**
The defects of adhaḥkarma (aśubha-karma made for a monk) and uddeśika (food prepared with intention toward a specific person) — these are defects of pudgala-dravya. How can the jñānī create those? — since those are always the qualities of para-dravya (another substance — the pudgala).

**PRAKRIT TEXT (G287):**
आधाकम्मं उदेिसयं च पोग्गलमयं इमं दव्वं।
कह तं मम होिद कयं जं िणच्चमचेदणं वुत्तं।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION (G287):**
Adhaḥkarma and uddeśika — this paudgalika (made of pudgala) dravya — how can it be my (ātmā's) creation? — since what is always acetana (insentient) — that is what is thus said (by niścaya).

**ANVAYARTHA:**
The final gathas of Adhikar 7 address a specific Jain monastic concern: adhaḥkarma (food that violates monastic rules, especially prepared specifically for a monk) and uddeśika. Kundakunda asks by niścaya: how can the jñānī be the kāraka of these pudgala-qualities? The pudgala is para-dravya, always acetana. The ātmā is cetan. The two do not interpenetrate in the kārakatva (doership) sense.

**COMMENTARY:**
This closes Adhikar 7 with the ontological ground of akārakatva: ātmā cannot be the creator of pudgala-dravya qualities because ātmā is cetana and pudgala is acetana. They are fundamentally different dravyas. The jñānī who knows this distinction cannot claim — or accept — kārakatva over pudgala-karma-qualities.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Adhaḥkarma and uddeśika are pudgala — always insentient. How can the ātmā (which is cetan) claim to have made them? It cannot. By niścaya, ātmā is not their maker.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
What is made of matter, moved by matter-causes, following matter-laws — that is not you. Knowing this clearly is liberation's beginning.

## Closing Colophon

**ORIGINAL PRAKRIT/SANSKRIT:**
इति बन्धो निष्क्रान्त:। इति श्रीमदमृतचन्द्रसूरिविरचितायां समयसारव्याख्यायामात्मख्यातौ बन्धप्ररूपकः सप्तमोऽङ्क:।।

**TRANSLATION:**
Thus Bandha is departed. In the Ātmakhyāti — the commentary on Samaysaar composed by the venerable Ācārya Amṛtacandra Sūri — the seventh adhikar, the exposition of Bandha, is thus concluded.

## Doctrinal Summary: The Five Keys of Adhikar 7

1. **Sneha-Bhāva = Rāga = Sole Cause**: Just as oil on skin causes dust to adhere, rāga in upayoga causes karma-binding. Not the activity — the inner quality.

2. **Adhyavasāna Alone Is Bandha**: Whether beings are killed or not is irrelevant. The inner determination — its quality and direction — is the only variable karma tracks (G262, the featured verse).

3. **Vastu Is Not the Cause**: Objects, actions, situations are the occasion but not the cause of bondage. Adhyavasāna uses vastu as basis; bondage comes from adhyavasāna (G265).

4. **Jñānī as Akāraka**: The crystal stays crystal while appearing red. The jñānī's soul does not transform into rāga. Rāga-appearance is para-dravya influence. Therefore jñānī is not kāraka of rāga-dveṣa-moha (G278-G280).

5. **Ātmā as Para-Dravya's Non-Creator**: Pudgala is acetana. Ātmā is cetana. Ātmā cannot be the maker of pudgala-qualities. This is the ontological basis of akārakatva (G286-G287).
