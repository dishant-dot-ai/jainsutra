# Samaysaar — Adhikar 9: Sarvavishuddhajñāna (सर्वविशुद्धज्ञान अधिकार)
## Pure All-Knowledge · Gathas 308–415 · 108 Gathas

**Central Thesis:** The soul (*jīva*) is *jñāyaka* — the eternal knower — alone. Jñāna (knowledge) is non-separate (*avyatiriktam*) from jñāyaka. This knowing-nature has nothing to do with any para-dravya (other substance): not karma, not body, not sense-objects, not dharma-astikāya, not the act of doing or experiencing. Every trace of ownership (*mamakāra*), agency (*kartṛtva*), and enjoyership (*bhoktṛtva*) projected onto the soul is mithyā. The path to liberation is darśana-jñāna-cāritra — not any external garb (*liṅga*).

---

## FEATURED VERSE — G403

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
> जम्हा जाणिदे णिचं तम्हा जीवो दु जाणगो णाणी।
> णाणं च जाणयादो अव्विदिरित्तं मुणेयव्वं।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
> यस्माज्जानाति नित्यं तस्माज्जीवस्तु ज्ञायको ज्ञानी।
> ज्ञानं च ज्ञायकादव्यतिरिक्तं ज्ञातव्यम्।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Because (jīva) always knows (*nityaṃ jānāti*), therefore jīva is *jñāyaka* — the knower — the *jñānī*. And jñāna should be understood as *avyatiriktam* (non-separate, non-different, non-distinct) from the jñāyaka.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
The soul is the knower because it always knows. And knowledge is not some separate thing from the knower — they are one. You, the soul, ARE knowing itself.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
Jñāna is not an activity the soul performs. Jñāna is not a product produced by the soul. Jñāna is what the soul *is*. The knower and the knowing are one substance. This is the summit of Adhikar 9.

## PART 1 — Ananya-Pariṇāma: The Soul and Its Modifications Are Non-Separate (G308–G315)

### G308 — Gold-Bracelet Analogy

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
> जह णवि होदि सुवण्णं विणा कडगसोहणेण अण्णेण।
> तह णवि होंति जीवा विणा णियएण पज्जएण।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Just as gold does not exist without its bracelet-form (or some other form) — similarly jīvas do not exist without their own *paryāyas* (modifications).

**ANVAYARTHA:**
*Dravya* and its *guṇas* are *ananya* (non-separate). The gold is inseparable from some modification; the soul is inseparable from its own paryāyas.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
You cannot have a soul floating free of all modifications. The soul always manifests as some particular form of knowing, seeing, or feeling. But those forms are its *own* — not borrowed from karma or body.

---

### G309 — Jīva and Ajīva Are Non-Separate from Their Paryāyas

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Jīva is ananya (non-separate) from its own pariṇāmas; ajīva is ananya from its own pariṇāmas. This non-separateness applies across all dravyas.

---

### G310 — Ātmā Is No One's Kārya and No Kāraṇa

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The ātmā is not the *kārya* (product) of any other dravya, and is not the *kāraṇa* (cause) of any other dravya. Each dravya arises from its own svabhāva.

---

### G311–G313 — Mutual Nimitta-Naimittika and Saṃsāra

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Karma depends on the *cetayitā* (conscious soul) and cetayitā depends on karma — this mutual nimitta-naimittika relationship is the mechanism of *saṃsāra*. As long as cetayitā holds the paryāyas of karma (*prakṛti-paryāyas*) as its own, it remains ajñāyaka and mithyādṛṣṭi.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
The soul is not "inside" karma and karma is not "inside" the soul. They are distinct dravyas with a nimitta relationship. The delusion is *mistaking* this nimitta for identity.

---

### G314–G315 — Release vs. Bondage

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
**G314:** As long as the cetayitā does not release the *prakṛti-paryāyas* (karma's modifications), it remains ajñāyaka (non-knower), mithyādṛṣṭi, and asaṃyata.

**G315:** When the cetayitā releases — it becomes *vimukta* (free), *jñāyaka*, *darśaka*, and *muni*.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
The watershed is not a cosmological event — it is the inner release of the false identification with karma's modifications.

---

## PART 2 — Jñānī Does Not Experience; Only Knows (G316–G323)

### G316 — The Core Distinction

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
> अण्णाणी जीवो पुण खवेदि कम्मं खु णाणिणो णेव।
> होंति अवेदया कम्मं णाणिणो णादू।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The ajñānī (*ignorant*) experiences the karmaphala within the *prakṛti-svabhāva* (karma's own nature). The jñānī only *knows* — does not experience. The jñānī is *avedaka* (non-experiencer) of karma; only *jñātā* (knower).

**SIMPLY PUT:**
When pain arises, the ajñānī becomes the pain — "I am in agony." The jñānī knows the pain happening in karma — "pain is arising in that pudgala-process." Same event; two different modes of the soul.

---

### G317–G319 — Snake-Milk Analogy; Avedaka in Nirveda

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
**G317:** The abhavya (one incapable of liberation) is like snake-milk — even if placed in a golden vessel, the milk becomes poison. Some souls cannot transform.

**G318:** The jñānī, established in *nirveda* (detachment), is *avedaka* — does not experience even what is experienced.

**G319:** The jñānī neither does nor experiences karma — only knows. This is the absolute definition.

---

### G320–G323 — The Eye Analogy: Akāraka and Avedaka

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
**G320:** Just as the eye (*akṣi*) is *akāraka* (non-doer) and *avedaka* (non-experiencer) — it illuminates without doing anything — similarly jñāna is akāraka and avedaka.

**G321–G323:** Just as one who says "Viṣṇu created the world" and one who says "the ātmā created the six kāya (living beings)" — both commit the same error of kartā-bhāva (attribution of agency). Jñāna does not create its objects.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Your eye doesn't "do" the seeing — it illuminates. Similarly, jñāna doesn't "do" the knowing — it illuminates the objects. No karma is made in the mere act of knowing.

---

## PART 3 — Para-Dravya Is Not Mine; The Four-Way Mithyatva Logic (G324–G348)

### G324–G327 — Para-Dravya Is Never Mine

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
**G324:** By *niścaya* (absolute standpoint) — not even a single *paramāṇu* (atom) of para-dravya is mine.

**G325:** Village, country, body — all mamakāra (ownership) is ajñānī delusion.

**G326–G327:** The mithyādṛṣṭi who claims para-dravya as his own; those who have *kartṛtva-vyavasāya* (determination of agency) toward para-dravyas — they are *dṛṣṭi-rahita* (sight-deprived).

---

### G328–G331 — The Four-Way Mithyatva Logic

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Four forms of mithyātva arise when jīva treats para as sva:
1. Claiming karma's modifications as one's own paryāyas
2. Claiming karma's fruit as one's own experience
3. Claiming one's pariṇāmas as caused by para-dravya
4. Claiming one's own pariṇāmas as belonging to para-dravya

All four are *mithyā*. Niścaya sees each dravya arising from its own svabhāva alone.

---

### G332–G339 — Karma Is the Kartā; Jīvas Are Akāraka

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
**G332–G334:** Karma makes everything: the ajñānī, the jñānī, sleep, waking, happiness, unhappiness, mithyātva, asaṃyama, wandering in three worlds. Karma is the kartā.

**G335–G339:** All jīvas are *akāraka* (non-doers). Karma desires karma — there is no brahmacārī-breaker. Karma kills karma — there is no killer. The karma-doctrine: nothing happens except through karma's own momentum.

**COMMENTARY:**
This appears Sāṃkhya-like, but the distinction is crucial: Kārikā Sāṃkhya says *prakṛti* is the agent and puruṣa is inert. Samaysaar says: when the soul mistakenly identifies with prakṛti-paryāyas, karma binds; when it releases that identification, karma exhausts itself. The jīva's non-agency is the *result* of jñāna — not an inherent inertness.

---

### G340–G348 — Sāṃkhya Parallel; Buddhist Kṣaṇika Refutation; Syādvāda

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
**G340:** The teaching "prakṛti does everything, all jīvas are akāraka" — this is Sāṃkhya-like, but the purpose is the release of kartā-bhāva, not the denial of the soul.

**G341:** Even "ātmā does its own ātmā" is mithyā — ātmā cannot make itself.

**G342–G343:** Ātmā is *nitya* (eternal), *asaṃkhyāta-pradeśī* (innumerable-spaced), *lokamātra* (space of the cosmos in size).

**G344:** The jñāyaka-bhāva, abiding in jñāna-svabhāva — ātmā cannot produce itself through any cause.

**Kalaśas 205–207:** Buddhist *kṣaṇika-vāda* (momentariness doctrine) refuted — a truly momentary consciousness cannot accumulate the continuity of jñāna. The *jñāyaka* must be nitya.

**G345–G348:** *Syādvāda* on doer-experiencer: "the doer and the experiencer are different" is mithyādṛṣṭi. "Another does, another experiences" is also mithyādṛṣṭi. Only the correct *anekānta* position: jīva is kartā of its own pariṇāmas, avedaka of karma's pariṇāmas.

---

## PART 4 — The Śilpī Analogy and Seṭikā Analogy (G349–G365)

### G349–G352 — Śilpī (Craftsman) Vyavahāra View

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
From the *vyavahāra* (conventional) standpoint, the craftsman (soul) does karma, uses karma, grasps karma, enjoys karma's fruit — but is not *tammaya* (identified) with karma.

---

### G353–G355 — Śilpī Niścaya View

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
From *niścaya* — the craftsman who becomes *ananya* (one) with the *cheṭṭā* (activity) is perpetually *duḥkhī* (suffering). The jīva that identifies with its activities of karma is bound.

---

### G356–G360 — The Seṭikā (Washerman's Stone) — Niścaya

**PRAKRIT TEXT (G356):**
> सेडिया सेडियामेव जाणगो जाणगो हवे।
> पासओ पासगो हवे संजदो संजदो हवे।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The *seṭikā* (washing stone) is seṭikā alone. The *jñāyaka* is jñāyaka alone. The *pāśaka* (observer) is pāśaka alone. The *saṃyata* (restrained) is saṃyata alone.

**ANVAYARTHA:**
By niścaya, each thing is only what it is. The seṭikā whitens clothes — but niścaya says: the seṭikā is seṭikā; the whiteness belongs to the cloth's own transformation. Similarly: the jñāyaka is jñāyaka; knowing is its own; what is "known" belongs to the jñeya's own nature.

**G357–G360:** Darśana is darśana alone. The jñāyaka is the jñāyaka alone. These are not modified by what they observe.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
The soul is not "a knower of karma" or "a knower of the body." The soul is *jñāyaka* — period. That is its identity, completely, without residue.

---

### G361–G365 — The Seṭikā — Vyavahāra

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
From vyavahāra: the seṭikā whitens para-dravya by its own svabhāva → similarly: the jñātā knows (by own svabhāva), the jīva sees (by own svabhāva), the jñātā abandons (by own svabhāva), the samyag-dṛṣṭi has śraddhā (by own svabhāva). All by sva-svabhāva.

---

## PART 5 — Jñāna Outside Para-Dravya; Rāga Is Own Pariṇāma (G366–G382)

### G366–G368 — Darśana-Jñāna-Cāritra Have Nothing in Achetana

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Darśana, jñāna, and cāritra have nothing (*kiṃcit nāsti*) in insentient viṣayas, in karma, in the body. So why does the ātmā cause harm in those domains?

---

### G370–G372 — Jīva's Guṇas Not in Para-Dravyas

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
**G370:** The jīva's *guṇas* (qualities) are not in para-dravyas. Therefore the samyag-dṛṣṭi has no rāga (attraction) toward sense-objects.

**G371:** Rāga, dveṣa, moha are the jīva's *own ananya-pariṇāmas* — they do not exist in śabda (sound) and other viṣayas (objects). They arise from the jīva's own modification, not from the external object.

**G372:** No other dravya can produce another dravya's guṇa. Therefore all dravyas arise by their own svabhāva (*svabhāvataḥ*).

**COMMENTARY:**
This is one of the most powerful doctrines in the Samaysaar: Rāga is *your* modification — not something the object did to you. The beautiful object did not cause your desire. Your soul modified into desire — using the object as *nimitta*. This cuts the root of blame, projection, and attachment.

---

### G373–G381 — The Six-Sense Analogy

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
**G373:** Pudgala transforms as nindā (criticism) and stuti (praise) words. The ajñānī becomes angry or pleased, thinking "this was said to me."

**G374:** The guṇa of pudgala-śabda belongs to pudgala — not to you.

**G375–G381:** Śabda (sound) doesn't say "grab me." Rūpa (form) doesn't say "grab me." Gandha (smell), rasa (taste), sparśa (touch), guṇa, dravya — none of them say "grab me." The ātmā doesn't *go out* to grasp them. The ātmā *illuminates* them; it is the jñeyas that appear — the ātmā doesn't move.

---

### G382 — The Mūḍha Still Doesn't Attain Upaśama

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Despite all this teaching, the *mūḍha* (deluded) still does not attain *upaśama* (inner calm). The delusion is deep-rooted.

---

## PART 6 — Niścaya-Cāritra: The Three Kalpas (G383–G389 + Kalaśas)

### G383–G386 — The Three Kalpas Defined

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
**G383:** *Pratikramaṇa* = past karma *mamtva-tyāga* (release of ownership of past karma).

**G384:** *Pratyākhyāna* = future karma *bhāva-nirvṛtti* (cessation of intention toward future karma).

**G385:** *Ālocana* = present *udaya-doṣa* recognition — consciously seeing the present karma rising.

**G386:** Nitya (constant) pratikramaṇa + ālocana + pratyākhyāna = true cāritra.

**COMMENTARY:**
These are not ritual recitations performed once a day. By *niścaya*, these three are a continuous inner attitude: "I was never the owner of past karma" (pratikramaṇa), "I am witnessing what is rising now" (ālocana), "I have no intention toward future karma" (pratyākhyāna). Together they constitute the soul's authentic conduct.

---

### G387–G389 — Three Forms of Vedana That Bind Again

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
**G387:** Experiencing (*vedana*) karmaphala as own-*rūpa* (as one's own nature) → binds 8-types *duḥkha-seeds* again.

**G388:** Experiencing thinking "I am the one who did this karmaphala" → binds again.

**G389:** Experiencing and *becoming* sukha or duḥkha (pleasure or pain) → binds again.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
There are three traps: (1) "This pain is what I am," (2) "I am the author of this karma," (3) "I am happy/I am miserable." Each of these reinforces bondage even while experiencing karma's fruit. The release is: "karma is rising; I am the witness."

---

### The Pratyākhyāna-Kalpa — 148 Karma-Type Declarations (Kalaśas 225–229)

The text provides three complete kalpas with 49 bhaṅgas each (3 yoga × 3 karaṇa + 4 extra combinations = 49) across three time periods:

**Pratikramaṇa-Kalpa** (past tense — "What I did/caused/approved by manas/vāc/kāya × 3-karaṇa = mithyā for me")

**Ālocana-Kalpa** (present tense — "I am not doing / causing / approving...")

**Pratyākhyāna-Kalpa** (future tense — "I will not do / cause / approve...")

The Pratyākhyāna-Kalpa covers all 148 karma types:
- Jñānāvaraṇa (5 types): mati, śruta, avadhi, manaḥparyaya, kevalajñāna
- Darśanāvaraṇa (9 types): cakṣu, acakṣu, avadhi, kevala, nidrā × 5 variants
- Vedanīya (2): śāta, aśāta
- Mohanīya (28): samyaktva, mithyātva, miśra; kasāya × 16; nokasāya × 9
- Āyu (4): naraka, tiryañca, manuṣya, deva
- Nāma (103 types): gati-nāma, jāti-nāma, śarīra-nāma, śarīra-bandha-nāma, śarīra-saṃghāta, śarīra-saṃsthāna, varṇa-nāma, gandha-nāma, rasa-nāma, sparśa-nāma, pūrvi-nāma, anupūrvi-nāma, gotra, vigraha...
- Gotra (2): uccagotra, nīcagotra
- Antarāya (5): dāna, lābha, bhoga, upabhoga, vīrya

For each: "**Nāhaṃ X bhuje, caitanyātmānamātmānameva sañcetaye**" — "I do not experience the fruit of X karma; I consciously experience only the consciousness-natured ātmā."

**BHĀVANĀ COMMENTARY:**
When the jīva is samyag-dṛṣṭi-jñānī, it already has jñāna-śraddhā: "I am free from all karma and karma-phala." But when pūrvabaddha karma comes into udaya, the contemplation of this karma-cetanā-tyāga (for 3-kāla × 49 bhaṅgas) and karmaphala-bhoga-tyāga — leaves only caitanya-svarūpa ātmā to be experienced. When the jīva reaches apramatta daśā, meditates with upayoga only in caitanyamātra ātmā, it generates kevalajñāna. Then: the fruit of this bhāvanā is jñāna-cetanā free from karma-cetanā and karmaphala-cetanā. The ātmā remains as jñāna-cetanā-rūpa, absorbed in paramānanda, eternally.

## PART 7 — Jñāna Distinct from All Dravyas (G390–G403)

### G390–G401 — Fourteen Propositions of Distinctness

Each gatha follows the same logical form:

**Pattern:** "X is not jñāna (*jñānaṃ na bhavati*) because X doesn't know anything (*kiṃcit na jānāti*). Therefore jñāna is different (*jñānam anyat*) and X is different (*X anyat*) — so Jinas say."

Applied to:
- **G390:** Śāstra (scripture) — achetana, cannot know
- **G391:** Śabda (sound/word) — pudgala paryāya, cannot know
- **G392:** Rūpa (form) — pudgala guṇa, cannot know
- **G393:** Varṇa (colour) — pudgala guṇa, cannot know
- **G394:** Gandha (smell) — pudgala guṇa, cannot know
- **G395:** Rasa (taste) — pudgala guṇa, cannot know
- **G396:** Sparśa (touch) — pudgala guṇa, cannot know
- **G397:** Karma (karmic matter) — achetana, cannot know
- **G398:** Dharma (dharmāstikāya) — achetana, cannot know
- **G399:** Adharma (adharmāstikāya) — achetana, cannot know
- **G400:** Kāla (time) — achetana, cannot know
- **G401:** Ākāśa (space) — achetana, cannot know

### G402 — Adhyavasāna Is Not Jñāna

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
*Adhyavasāna* (karmic determination / passion-driven impulse) is not jñāna, because adhyavasāna is *achetana* (insentient). Therefore jñāna is different and adhyavasāna is different.

**COMMENTARY (Tīkā):**
Jñāna and jīva are *avyatiriktam* (non-separate). Jñāna and śāstra are *vyatiriktam*. Jñāna and śabda are vyatiriktam. Jñāna and karma are vyatiriktam... Jñāna and adhyavasāna are vyatiriktam. Only jīva is cetana — therefore jñāna and jīva are avyatiriktam. The tiny remaining doubt — "is jñāna perhaps slightly separate from jīva?" — should not be entertained.

### G404 — Jñāna IS Samyagdṛṣṭi, Saṃyama, Sūtra, Dharma, Pravrajyā

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
> णाणं सम्मदिदिं दु संजमं सुत्तंगपुव्वगयं।
> धम्मादम्मं च तहा पव्वज्जं अभुवंति बुहा।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The *budha* (wise, jñānī) regard jñāna itself as *samyagdṛṣṭi* (right faith), as *saṃyama* (self-restraint), as *sūtras* and *pūrvas* (sacred scripture), as *dharma* and *adharma* (puṇya and pāpa), and as *pravrajyā* (initiation into renunciation).

**COMMENTARY:**
The jñānī does not see samyagdṛṣṭi as a set of beliefs held. The jñānī sees samyagdṛṣṭi as the jñāna itself — the knowing faculty as right seeing. Saṃyama is not a list of restraints — it is the natural non-grasping of jñāna. Pravrajyā is not wearing a garb — it is the inner renunciation that is jñāna's own nature.

## PART 8 — Jñāna Has No Body; Liṅga Is Not Mokṣa-Mārga (G405–G415)

### G405–G407 — Ātmā Is Amūrta; Pure Soul Grasps Nothing

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
**G405:** The ātmā is *amūrta* (formless/immaterial). Āhāra (nourishment/intake) is *mūrta* (material), being *pudgalamaya*. Therefore ātmā cannot be *āhāraka* (one who takes food/intake).

**G406:** Para-dravya cannot be grasped or released by the soul. Neither *prāyogika* (applied) nor *vaiśasika* (natural) modification of the soul reaches para-dravya.

**G407:** Therefore the *viśuddha-cetā* (pure-consciousness soul) neither grasps anything nor releases anything of jīva-ajīva dravyas.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
The soul doesn't "take in" experience the way food is taken in. The soul illuminates — nothing is captured, nothing is stored in the soul from outside. What appears to be stored is karma — a pudgala phenomenon.

---

### G408–G411 — Liṅga Is Not the Path; Darśana-Jñāna-Cāritra Is

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
**G408:** The deluded, grasping the *pāṣaṇḍī* (heterodox ascetic) garb or *gṛhī* (householder) garb in many forms, declare "this liṅga is the path of mokṣa."

**G409:** But liṅga is not the path of mokṣa. Arhants are *nirmmama* (beyond mamakāra) regarding the body; they leave the liṅga and serve darśana-jñāna-cāritra.

**G410:** Neither pāṣaṇḍī-liṅga nor gṛhī-liṅga is mokṣa-mārga. Darśana-jñāna-cāritra is the mokṣa-mārga — so Jinas say.

**G411:** Therefore, abandoning whatever liṅga is held — monk's garb (*sāgara*) or householder's (*gṛhastha*) — engage the ātmā in darśana-jñāna-cāritra, which is the path of mokṣa.

**COMMENTARY:**
This does not abolish external vows or the monastery. The Tīkā clarifies: vyavahāra-naya calls both muni-liṅga and śrāvaka-liṅga the path of mokṣa (as sadhanā containers). Niścaya-naya says: no liṅga is mokṣa-mārga — only darśana-jñāna-cāritra is. The upadeśa here is for those who cling to the garb as though the garb itself liberates. The vow-keeping is valid; the mamakāra in the garb is what must be released.

---

### G412 — The Final Instruction

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
> मोक्खपहे अप्पाणं ठवेहि तं चेव झाहि तं चेय।
> तत्थेव विहर णिच्चं मा विहरसु अण्णदव्वेसु।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Establish the ātmā on the *mokṣa-path*; meditate on that (*darśana-jñāna-cāritra*); contemplate it; dwell there always (*nitya*); do not dwell (*viharsu*) in other dravyas.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Four imperatives: establish, meditate, experience, dwell. One prohibition: don't dwell in other dravyas. This is the entire path in two lines.

---

### G413 — Those with Mamatā in Liṅgas Have Not Known Samayasāra

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Those who carry *mamatā* (ownership) in pāṣaṇḍī liṅgas or gṛhī liṅgas of many types — they have not known *samayasāra* (the essence of the soul).

**COMMENTARY:**
The one who wears the muni garb but secretly thinks "this garb is what liberates me" — has not known samayasāra. The one who performs rituals but thinks "this ritual purifies the soul" — has not known samayasāra. Samayasāra is the *pure soul itself*. It is known by jñāna alone, not by any external marker.

---

### G414 — Vyavahāra-Naya vs. Niścaya-Naya on Liṅga

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
*Vyavahāra-naya* calls both liṅgas (muni and śrāvaka) the path of mokṣa. *Niścaya-naya* does not consider any liṅga as mokṣa-mārga.

**COMMENTARY:**
These are not contradictions — they are two levels of the same truth. Vyavahāra: yes, the muni-discipline is a mokṣa-mārga instrument. Niścaya: the discipline works only because of the darśana-jñāna-cāritra operating within it. The garb is the container; darśana-jñāna-cāritra is the content. Only the content liberates.

---

### G415 — The Colophon Gatha: The Promise of This Text

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
> जो समयपाहुडिमणं पढिदूणं अत्थतच्चदो णादुं।
> अत्थे ठाही चेदा सो होही उत्तमं सोक्खं।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
> यः समयप्राभृतमिदं पठित्वा अर्थतत्त्वतो ज्ञात्वा।
> अर्थे स्थास्यति चेतयिता स भविष्यत्युत्तमं सौख्यम्।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The *cetayitā* (conscious soul — the *bhavya jīva*) who reads this *Samayaprābhṛta*, knows its meaning and *tattvas* (principles), and *establishes* (sthāyate) itself in that meaning — will attain the highest happiness (*uttamaṃ saukhyam*).

**COMMENTARY (Tīkā):**
The Samayaprābhṛta is called "*śabdabrahma*" — equivalent to the universal creative Word — because it illuminates all things as they are. The soul that reads this scripture, grasps its paramārtha (the *caitanya-prakāśarūpa* ātmā), and establishes itself in that paramārtha — in that complete *pūrṇavijñāna-ghana-paramabrahma* — will, in the immediacy of that experience, become radiant with one *cetanya-rasa*, naturally arrive at *uttama saukhya* (supreme happiness = paramānanda), and remain in that state eternally.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
If you read this text, understand it truly, and *live* in that understanding — you will attain the highest joy. Not someday. The moment the soul establishes itself in its own true nature as jñāyaka, that is the beginning of *paramānanda*.

## CLOSING COLOPHON

**SANSKRIT:**
> इति श्रीमदमृतचन्द्रसूरिविरचितायां समयसारख्याख्यामात्मख्यातौ सर्वविशुद्धज्ञानप्ररूपकः नवमोऽङ्कः।।

**ENGLISH:**
Thus in the *Ātmakhyāti* — the commentary on the *Samayasāra* composed by the venerable Śrī Amṛtacandrasūri — the Ninth Act, **revealer of Sarvavishuddhajñāna**, is concluded.

## CLOSING SARVAYĀ VERSE (Hindi)

> सर्वविशुद्धज्ञानरूप सदा चिदानंद करता न भोगता न परद्रव्यभावको,
> मूरत अमूरत जे आनद्रव्य लोकमांहि ते भी ज्ञानरूप नहीं न्यारे न अभावको;
> यहै जानि ज्ञानी जीव आपकूं भजे सदीव ज्ञानरूप सुखतूप आन न लगावको,
> कर्म-कर्मफलरूप चेतनाकूं दूरि टारि ज्ञानचेतना अभ्यास करे शुद्ध भावको।

**ENGLISH:**
The ātmā, whose form is *sarvavishuddhajñāna*, forever experiences pure consciousness (*cidānanda*) — not experiencing any para-dravya-bhāva. Even the mūrta (material) and amūrta (immaterial) other dravyas of the world — they are also not jñāna-rūpa; they are not its own, and not absent (they exist, but elsewhere). Knowing this, the jñānī jīva worships its own self as jñāna-rūpa always, attaching nothing else. Removing karma-cetanā and karmaphala-cetanā far away — practicing jñāna-cetanā with pure bhāva.

## FIVE KEYS OF ADHIKAR 9

1. **Jñāyaka is jñāyaka alone** — The seṭikā is the seṭikā; the knower is the knower. The soul is not defined by what it knows, or by the karma surrounding it.

2. **Rāga is your own pariṇāma** — No external object causes rāga or dveṣa. The object is nimitta; the pariṇāma is yours. This cuts all projection and blame at the root.

3. **Niścaya-cāritra is the three kalpas** — Pratikramaṇa (past mamtva-tyāga), ālocana (present witness), pratyākhyāna (future intention-release) — performed constantly, not ritually.

4. **Jñāna is avyatiriktam from jñāyaka** — Knowledge and the knower are not two things. The soul is not a knower who "has" knowledge. The soul is knowing itself (G403).

5. **Darśana-jñāna-cāritra is the only mokṣa-mārga** — Not the garb, not the ritual, not the lineage. The ātmā that establishes itself in its own darśana-jñāna-cāritra — that ātmā attains *uttama saukhya* (G415).
