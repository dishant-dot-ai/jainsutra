# Samaysaar — Adhikar 4: Āsrava (Influx of Karma)
## Translation: Gathas 164–180

**Scripture:** Samayasāra (समयसार)
**Author:** Ācārya Kundakunda
**Commentary (Ātmakhyāti Tīkā):** Ācārya Amṛtacandra
**Chapter:** Adhikar 4 — Āsrava (आस्रव अधिकार / Influx of Karma)
**Theme:** What āsrava actually is, how it operates at the level of the soul's own transformations, and crucially — why the jñānī (samyag-dṛṣṭi) does not bind new karma despite living within a body surrounded by previously bound karma.

---

## THEMATIC OVERVIEW

Adhikar 4 is Samaysaar's most technically precise chapter on the mechanism of karma-influx. Kundakunda opens by defining āsrava not as external invasion but as the soul's own transformations (ananya-pariṇāma) — mithyātva, aviramana, kaṣāya, yoga, saṃjñā. The soul is the author of its own bondage through rāga-dveṣa-moha. Then comes the central puzzle and its resolution: the jñānī (samyag-dṛṣṭi) lives surrounded by previously bound pratyayas (karma-factors) that continue to operate — yet is declared abandha (non-binding). Why? Because new karma requires two conditions: drva-āsrava (material factors) AND bhāva-āsrava (rāga-dveṣa-moha). The jñānī lacks the second. Without the bhāva-āsrava, the drva-pratyayas cannot bind new karma. The analogy is vivid: a young girl exists in a household but does not attract attachment (like previously bound karma in the jñānī) — once grown into a young woman in the right conditions, she does (like karma binding when rāga is present). The Adhikar closes with the digestive fire analogy: previously bound pratyayas in the jñānī who slips from shuddhanaya do bind karma — just as food transforms through digestive fire into flesh and blood when the body receives it.

## PART 1: What Is Āsrava? — The Soul's Own Transformations

### GATHA 164

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
मिच्छत्तं अविरमणं कसायजोगा य सण्णसण्णा दु।
बहुविहभया जीवे तस्सेव अणण्णपरिणामा।।१६४।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
मिथ्यात्वमविरमणं कषायोगैश्च संज्ञासंज्ञास्तु।
बहुविधभेदा जीवे तस्यैवानन्यपरिणामाः।।१६४।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Mithyātva, aviramana (non-restraint), kaṣāya-yoga, and saṃjñā-asaṃjñā — these many types of āsrava, with their many divisions, are the soul's own ananya-pariṇāma (not-other transformations — inseparable from the soul itself).

**COMMENTARY:**
The opening verse immediately reframes what āsrava is. It is not external force invading the soul from outside. It is the soul's own transformation — *ananya-pariṇāma* means "transformation that is not-other-than-the-soul." The five types of āsrava are:
1. **Mithyātva** — wrong perception/orientation
2. **Aviramana** — non-restraint, failure to withdraw from harm
3. **Kaṣāya** — the four passions (anger, pride, deceit, greed)
4. **Yoga** — the activity of mind, speech, body (here grouped with kaṣāya)
5. **Saṃjñā-asaṃjñā** — intentional (saṃjñā) and unintentional (asaṃjñā) aspects

These are the soul's own states. The Ātmakhyāti notes: these āsravas have the characteristics of consciousness (cidābhāsa) even though they are ignorance-based — they are not inert; they are the soul's own transformation in its defiled condition. The implication is profound: since āsrava is the soul's own state, it is also within the soul's power to change.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Āsrava — mithyātva, non-restraint, passions, yoga — these many types are the soul's own transformations. Karma doesn't invade from outside. The soul generates its own bondage from within.

### GATHA 165

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
णाणावरणादीयस्स ते दु कम्मस्स कारणं होंति।
तेसिं पि होिद जीवो य रागदोसादिभावकरो।।१६५।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
ज्ञानावरणादीयस्य ते तु कर्मणः कारणं भवन्ति।
तेषामपि भवति जीवश्च रागद्वेषादिभावकरः।।१६५।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
These (āsravas) are the cause of jñānāvaraṇa and other karmas. And the cause of those (āsravas) is the soul itself — the maker of rāga-dveṣa and other bhāvas.

**COMMENTARY:**
A causal chain is stated with precision:
- Soul generates rāga-dveṣa-moha (bhāva-āsrava)
- Rāga-dveṣa-moha causes the material karma-influx (drva-āsrava → jñānāvaraṇa-ādi karmas)
- Those karmas in turn condition the soul's future states

The soul is the originating cause. This is not determinism — it is the structure of self-created bondage. Understanding this chain completely is what enables its dissolution. The Ātmakhyāti elaborates: rāga-dveṣa-moha are the āsravas in their true sense. They are ajñāna-maya (ignorance-formed) and are cidābhāsa (appearing as consciousness but not pure consciousness). The pudgala-karma (material karma) merely follows the soul's own state.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
These āsravas cause jñānāvaraṇa and other karmas. And the cause of those āsravas is the soul itself — by making rāga, dveṣa, and other bhāvas.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
If you are the author of your own bondage — not through external forces but through your own inner states — what does that mean for the path to freedom?

## PART 2: The Samyag-Dṛṣṭi Has No Āsrava-Bandha

### GATHA 166

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
णित्थि दु आसवबंधो सम्मादिट्ठिस्स आसविणरोहो।
संते पुव्विणिबद्धे जाणिद सो ते अबंधंतो।।१६६।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
नास्ति त्वास्रवबन्धः सम्यग्दृष्टेराास्रविनिरोधः।
सन्ति पूर्वनिबद्धानि जानाति स तान्यबध्नन्।।१६६।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
For the samyag-dṛṣṭi (right-perceiving soul), there is no āsrava-bandha (influx-bondage) — there is āsrava-nirodha (cessation of influx). He knows the previously bound karmas as existing — but without binding new ones.

**COMMENTARY:**
This verse introduces the central paradox of the Adhikar: the samyag-dṛṣṭi still has previously bound karma operating (santi pūrva-nibaddhāni — previously bound ones still exist); yet is declared to have no āsrava-bandha. How? Because āsrava-bandha (new influx-bondage) requires bhāva-āsrava (rāga-dveṣa-moha), which the samyag-dṛṣṭi does not possess in the mithyātva-based form. What the samyag-dṛṣṭi has is āsrava-nirodha — cessation, stoppage of new influx. The previously bound karma continues to operate (they see it, know it) — but they are not binding more.

The phrase "jānāti tān abadhnan" — "he knows those while not binding" — is stunning: the jñānī observes the operation of their own prior karma without adding to it. Knowing without bonding is the jñānī's characteristic mode of being.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
For the samyag-dṛṣṭi, there is no new āsrava-bondage — there is cessation of āsrava. He knows the previously bound karmas as present — but binds no new ones.

## PART 3: The Law — Rāgī Bhāva Binds, Jñāna Bhāva Doesn't

### GATHA 167

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
भावो रागादिजुदो जीवेण कदो दु बंधगो भणिदो।
रागादिविप्पमुक्को अबंधगो जाणगो णवरि।।१६७।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
भावो रागादियुतो जीवेन कृतस्तु बन्धको भणितः।
रागादिविप्रमुक्तोऽबन्धको ज्ञायकः केवलम्।।१६७।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The bhāva (inner state) joined with rāgādi, done by the soul, is declared the binder (bandha). The bhāva free from rāgādi is non-binding — and is the pure knower only (jñāyaka kevalam).

**COMMENTARY:**
The operative principle of the entire Adhikar crystallized in one verse. Bondage is determined by the quality of the inner bhāva, not by the outer action. Two souls may perform the same external act; one with rāgādi (attachment/aversion), one without. Only the first creates new bondage. The second — whose bhāva is free from rāga — is declared *jñāyaka kevalam*: purely the knower. The knowing itself is non-binding; attachment to what is known is binding.

The Ātmakhyāti uses the iron-magnet analogy: a needle mixed with iron-dust (rāgādi) is attracted to the magnet (karma). The same needle, free of iron-dust, rests in its own nature — it is not attracted. The jñānī's bhāva, free of rāgādi, rests in its nature as pure knowing. Karma has no purchase on pure knowing.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
The inner bhāva mixed with rāgādi binds karma. The inner bhāva free from rāgādi does not bind — it is purely the knower.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
Is your awareness mixed with attraction/aversion — or is it simply knowing? The difference between the two is the difference between bondage and freedom.

## PART 4: When Karma-Bhāva Falls, It Does Not Return

### GATHA 168

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
पक्के फलम्हि पिडिए जह ण फलं बज्झए पुणो विंटे।
जीवस्स कम्मभावे पिडिए ण पुणोदयमुवेिद।।१६८।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
पक्वे फले पतिते यथा न फलं बध्यते पुनर्वृन्ततैः।
जीवस्य कर्मभावे पतिते न पुनरुदयमुपैति।।१६८।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Just as a ripe fruit, once fallen, does not again attach to the branch — so when the karma-bhāva falls from the soul's experience, it does not again arise in new form.

**COMMENTARY:**
The ripe fruit analogy speaks to the natural, effortless character of karma's departure when jñāna is present. A ripe fruit falls because ripeness and the branch-connection cannot coexist — gravity simply does its work. No one tears the fruit off; it falls when the conditions are complete. Similarly, when the karma-bhāva (the bhāva that was conditioned by karma) fully passes through its experience, it does not regenerate. The jñānī who knows this does not cling to the departing karma's bhāva, nor does it re-attach.

The Ātmakhyāti notes: once jñāna truly dawns, even if rāgādi occur (due to residual cāritra-moha), they are known without being owned. The soul does not manufacture a new identity from them. They do not re-seed themselves. This is the mechanism by which the jñānī's karmas gradually exhaust — not through violent suppression but through non-renewal.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
A ripe fruit falls and doesn't reattach to the branch. Similarly, when the karma-bhāva falls away from the soul's experience, it doesn't arise again in new form.

## PART 5: Previously Bound Pratyayas — Like Clods of Earth

### GATHA 169

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
पुढवीपिंडसमाणा पुव्विणिबद्धा दु पच्चया तस्स।
कम्मसरीरेण दु ते बद्धा सव्वे वि णाणिस्स।।१६९।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
पृथ्वीपिण्डसमानाः पूर्वनिबद्धास्तु प्रत्ययास्तस्य।
कर्मशरीरेण तु ते बद्धाः सर्वेऽपि ज्ञानिनः।।१६९।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
For the jñānī, all the previously bound pratyayas (āsrava-factors) are like clods of earth — they are all bound only to the karma-body (karmaśarīra), not to the jīva.

**COMMENTARY:**
The previously bound karma-factors (pratyayas) are *pṛthvī-piṇḍa-samānā* — like clods of earth. Earth is inert, external, physically there — but it is not the self. They are bound to the karma-body (the subtle material body of karma), not to the pure jīva itself. The jñānī knows this distinction with lived certainty, not just conceptually. Previously accumulated karma remains present — but it belongs to the karma-body, not to the consciousness.

The Ātmakhyāti elaborates: these previously bound pratyayas, bound in ajñāna (prior ignorance), are pudgala (material) transformations — achetan (non-sentient). They are therefore naturally distinct from the jñānī's jīva (pure consciousness). Being materially bound, they will eventually dissolve by their own nature. The jñānī simply does not add to them.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
For the jñānī, all previously bound pratyayas are like clods of earth — they are bound only to the karma-body, not to the jīva itself.

## PART 6: The Paradox — Why Jñānī Is Abandha Despite Ongoing Karma

### GATHA 170

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
चउविह अणेयभेयं बंधंते णाणदंसणगुणेिहं।
समए समए जम्हा तेण अबंधो िति णाणी दु।।१७०।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
चतुर्विधा अनेकभेदं बध्नन्ति ज्ञानदर्शनगुणाभ्याम्।
समये समये यस्मात् तेनाबन्ध इति ज्ञानी तु।।१७०।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Because the four-types of [drva-āsrava] bind many kinds of karma through the jñāna-darśana-guṇas at every moment — for this very reason, the jñānī is declared abandha (non-bound).

**COMMENTARY:**
This is the logical pivot of the Adhikar. The drva-pratyayas (previously bound material āsrava factors) do bind karma at the material level every moment — this is admitted. But the reason the jñānī is still declared abandha is precisely this: the material factors bind through the *jñāna-darśana-guṇa-pariṇāma* — through the transformation of the knowing-seeing quality — not through rāgādi. When the cause is jñāna-guṇa (lowest state) rather than rāga-dveṣa-moha, the binding is of a fundamentally different quality. The Ātmakhyāti notes: the jñānī is nirasrava in terms of bhāva-āsrava (the real āsrava). The material binding continues due to residual drva-pratyayas, but this is not considered genuine bondage from the paramārtha perspective.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Because the four types of drva-āsrava bind many types of karma through jñāna-darśana-guṇas every moment — for this very reason, the jñānī is declared non-bound.

### GATHA 171

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
जम्हा दु जहण्णादो णाणगुणादो पुणो वि परिणमिदे।
अण्णत्तं णाणगुणो तेण दु सो बंधगो भणिदो।।१७१।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
यस्मातु जघन्यात् ज्ञानगुणात् पुनरपि परिणमते।
अन्यत्वं ज्ञानगुणः तेन तु स बन्धको भणितः।।१७१।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Because from the lowest state of jñāna-guṇa, the jñāna-guṇa transforms again to another form — for this reason it is declared the cause of binding.

**COMMENTARY:**
The jñāna-guṇa in its lowest state (jaghanya — the minimum possible knowing-capacity of the samyag-dṛṣṭi who has not yet attained full cāritra) still transforms — flickers between one state and another. This minimal flickering is not stable, continuous pure knowing. Because it transforms to another form (anyatva — differentness), there is still some degree of residual material binding. The Ātmakhyāti: the kṣāyopaśamika jñāna (knowledge arising from partial suppression-destruction of knowledge-covering karma) stays on one object only for an instant (antarmuhūrta), then moves to another. This impermanence of focus means the knowing-guṇa is still in "lowest state" mode — and its transformation produces some bondage through the mechanism of unavoidable rāga that accompanies the pre-full-cāritra state.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Because from the lowest state of jñāna-guṇa, the jñāna-guṇa transforms again to another form — for this reason it is declared a binding cause.

### GATHA 172

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
दंसणणाणचरितं जं परिणमदे जहण्णभावेण।
णाणी तेण दु बज्झिद पोग्गलकम्मेण विविहेण।।१७२।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
दर्शनज्ञानचारित्रं यत्परिणमते जघन्यभावेन।
ज्ञानी तेन तु बध्यते पुद्गलकर्मणा विविधेन।।१७२।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
When darśana (perception), jñāna (knowledge), and cāritra (conduct) transform in their lowest states — the jñānī is bound by various pudgala-karma through that.

**COMMENTARY:**
The three jewels — darśana, jñāna, cāritra — in their lowest states (jaghanya-bhāva) still produce some pudgala karma binding. The key insight from the Ātmakhyāti: this binding is minimal, of a kind that does not extend samsāra in a meaningful way (not generating the major karma categories that keep samsāra running). It is residual, inevitable given the jñānī's stage — and it dissolves rapidly because the soul's overall trajectory is toward purity. The instruction that follows: keep pressing toward the fullest expression of darśana, jñāna, and cāritra — away from their lowest states. The closer one approaches full cāritra (complete yathākhyāta-cāritra), the less this residual binding occurs.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
When darśana, jñāna, and cāritra transform in their lowest states — the jñānī is bound by various pudgala-karma through that.

## PART 7: The Samyag-Dṛṣṭi and the Previously Bound

### GATHA 173

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
सव्वे पुव्विणिबद्धा दु पच्चया अत्थि सम्मदिट्ठिस्स।
उवओगपाओगं बंधंते कम्मभावेण।।१७३।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
सर्वे पूर्वनिबद्धास्तु प्रत्ययाः सन्ति सम्यग्दृष्टेः।
उपयोगप्रायोग्यं बध्नन्ति कर्मभावेन।।१७३।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
All previously bound pratyayas are present in the samyag-dṛṣṭi. Through upayoga-prayogya (what is fit for upayoga — application/usage), they bind through karma-bhāva.

**COMMENTARY:**
The samyag-dṛṣṭi carries all previously bound karma-factors — they did not vanish at the moment of right perception. What changed is the quality of their operation. Through upayoga-prayogya — the pratyayas that come into the field of upayoga (consciousness-application) — they produce binding through karma-bhāva. This is where G174-176 elaborate: the previously-dormant pratyayas become active (upabhogya — capable of being experienced) under the right conditions, particularly when the soul's upayoga is not purely established in its own svabhāva.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
All previously bound pratyayas exist in the samyag-dṛṣṭi. Through what comes into the sphere of upayoga, they bind through karma-bhāva.

### GATHA 174

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
होदूण णिरुवभोज्जा तह बंधिद जह हवंति उवभोज्जा।
सत्तट्ठिविहा भूदा णाणावरणादिभावेिहं।।१७४।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
भूत्वा निरुपभोग्यानि तथा बध्नाति यथा भवन्त्युपभोग्यानि।
सप्ताष्टविधानि भूतानि ज्ञानावरणादिभावैः।।१७४।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Having been non-enjoyable (nirupabhogya — dormant), they bind in such a way that they become enjoyable (upabhogya — active/ripening) — these seven-to-eight types of jñānāvaraṇa and other bhāvas.

**COMMENTARY:**
The previously bound karma-pratyayas exist in two states: nirupabhogya (dormant, not-yet-ripened, not currently active in binding) and upabhogya (active, ripened, capable of influencing the soul's experience). When dormant pratyayas mature into the active state, they bind the corresponding seven to eight types of karma (jñānāvaraṇa, darśanāvaraṇa, vedanīya, mohanīya, āyu, nāma, gotra, antarāya — the eight main karma-types). The metaphor of G175 will illustrate this transformation.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Having been dormant, they bind in such a way that they become active — these seven-to-eight types (jñānāvaraṇa etc.) of karma-bhāvas.

### GATHA 175

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
संता दु णिरुवभोज्जा बाला इत्थी जहेह पुिरसस्स।
बंधिद ते उवभोज्जे तरुणी इत्थी जह णरस्स।।१७५।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
सन्ति तु निरुपभोग्यानि बाला स्त्री यथेह पुरुषस्य।
बध्नाति तानि उपभोग्यानि तरुणी स्त्री यथा नरस्य।।१७५।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
They exist as non-enjoyable (dormant) — like a young girl in a man's household. They bind as enjoyable (active) — like a young woman does to a man.

**COMMENTARY:**
The analogy is vivid: a young girl in a household is present but creates no romantic attachment (nirpabhogya). The same person, grown into a young woman, can bind the man with attachment (upabhogya). The previously bound pratyayas are like the girl — present in the jñānī's karmic inheritance but not currently ripening into active bondage. When conditions change (when the soul slips from shuddhanaya into rāgādi), those same pratyayas become like the young woman — active, capable of binding new karma.

The Ātmakhyāti notes: this is the mechanism by which even the samyag-dṛṣṭi binds some karma in the transitional states between clear knowing and full cāritra. The pratyayas are always present; whether they bind depends on the soul's bhāva-state.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Dormant pratyayas are like a young girl in a man's household — present but not binding. Active pratyayas are like a young woman — they bind through the experience they create.

---

### GATHA 176

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
एदेण कारणेण दु सम्मादिट्ठी अबंधगो भणिद्रो।
आसवभावाभावे ण पच्चया बंधगा भणिदा।।१७६।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
एतेन कारणेण तु सम्यग्दृष्टिरबन्धको भणितः।
आसवभावाभावे न प्रत्यया बन्धका भणिताः।।१७६।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
For this reason, the samyag-dṛṣṭi is declared non-binding (abandha). In the absence of āsrava-bhāva, the pratyayas are not declared binding agents.

**COMMENTARY:**
The resolution: the samyag-dṛṣṭi is abandha because āsrava-bhāva (rāga-dveṣa-moha as actual operative states) is absent from the samyag-dṛṣṭi's experience. Without āsrava-bhāva, the drva-pratyayas (material karma-factors) cannot produce new bondage on their own. They require both material factors AND the soul's rāga-bhāva working together. The samyag-dṛṣṭi provides the first (old karma continues to operate) but not the second (no new rāga-bhāva in the mithyātva sense). Therefore: abandha.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
For this reason the samyag-dṛṣṭi is declared non-binding. Without āsrava-bhāva, the pratyayas are not declared binding agents.

**CONTEMPLATE:**
The soul is surrounded by the results of its past. But the past only binds the future when you add present rāga to it. What happens when you stop adding?

## PART 8: Rāga-Dveṣa-Moha Absent in Samyag-Dṛṣṭi

### GATHA 177

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
रागो दोसो मोहो य आसवा णित्थि सम्मदिट्ठिस्स।
तम्हा आसवभावेण विणा हेदू ण पच्चया होंति।।१७७।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
रागो द्वेषश्च मोहश्च आस्रवा न सन्ति सम्यग्दृष्टेः।
तस्मादास्रवभावेन विना हेतवो न प्रत्यया भवन्ति।।१७७।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Rāga, dveṣa, and moha — these āsravas do not exist in the samyag-dṛṣṭi. Therefore, without āsrava-bhāva, the pratyayas do not become binding causes (hetu).

**COMMENTARY:**
The direct statement: the three root āsravas — rāga (attachment), dveṣa (aversion), moha (delusion) — are absent from the samyag-dṛṣṭi's conscious experience. This does not mean the samyag-dṛṣṭi never has any rāga in the loose sense — it means that mithyātva-based rāga (the deep delusion of mistaking the non-self for self, with its attendant passionate possessiveness) is gone. The Ātmakhyāti's lengthy commentary here clarifies: rāga-dveṣa-moha that accompanies mithyātva is what is meant. The residual rāga from cāritra-moha (which even the avirati-samyag-dṛṣṭi has) is not the āsrava referred to here — it is not strong enough to be the binding cause in the principal sense.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Rāga, dveṣa, and moha — the real āsravas — do not exist in the samyag-dṛṣṭi. Therefore, without āsrava-bhāva, the pratyayas are not binding causes.

---

### GATHA 178

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
हेदू चदुव्वियप्पो अट्ठिवियप्पस्स कारणं भणिदं।
तेसिं पि य रागादी तेसिमभावे ण बज्झंति।।१७८।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
हेतुश्चतुर्विकल्पः अष्टविकल्पस्य कारणं भणितम्।
तेषामपि च रागादयः तेषामभावे न बध्यन्ते।।१७८।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
The four types of hetu (causes — mithyātva, aviramana, kaṣāya, yoga) are declared the cause of the eight types (of karma). And the cause of those hetus is rāgādi — in their absence (in the samyag-dṛṣṭi), no binding takes place.

**COMMENTARY:**
The causal chain is completed systematically:
- 8 types of karma ← caused by 4 types of hetu (mithyātva, aviramana, kaṣāya, yoga)
- 4 types of hetu ← caused by rāgādi (rāga, dveṣa, moha)
- rāgādi ← absent in samyag-dṛṣṭi
- Therefore: no binding in samyag-dṛṣṭi

The logical structure is clean. Remove the root cause; the chain collapses. Samyak-dṛṣṭi removes rāgādi (in the mithyātva-based form) as a structural consequence of right perception — not through willpower but through the very nature of seeing correctly.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
The four cause-types are the cause of eight types of karma. The cause of those four is rāgādi. In their absence (samyag-dṛṣṭi has no rāgādi), no karma is bound.

## PART 9: The Digestive Fire — When the Jñānī Slips

### GATHA 179

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
जह पुिरसेणाहारो गिहिदो परिणमिद सो अणेयविहं।
मंसवसारुहिरादी भावे उदरिग्गसंजुतो।।१७९।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
यथा पुरुषेणाहारो गृहीतः परिणमति सोऽनेकविधम्।
मांसवसारुधिरादीन् भावान् उदराग्निसंयुक्तः।।१७९।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Just as food taken by a person, combined with the digestive fire, transforms into many forms — flesh, fat, blood, and others —

**COMMENTARY:**
Gatha 179 begins the closing analogy. Food in itself is neutral — it is neither flesh nor blood. Combined with the body's digestive fire, it transforms into multiple forms. This sets up the parallel in G180.

---

### GATHA 180

**PRAKRIT TEXT:**
तह णाणिस्स दु पुव्वं जे बद्धा पच्चया बहुवियप्पं।
बज्झंते कम्मं ते णयपरिहीणा दु ते जीवा।।१८०।।

**SANSKRIT CHHAYA:**
तथा ज्ञानिनस्तु पूर्वं ये बद्धाः प्रत्यया बहुविकल्पम्।
बध्नन्ति कर्म ते नयपरिहीणास्तु ते जीवाः।।१८०।।

**LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION:**
Similarly, for the jñānī, the previously bound pratyayas bind karma in many forms — for those souls (jīvas) who have slipped away from right naya (shuddhanaya).

**COMMENTARY:**
The analogy is complete: just as food transforms into flesh and blood through the digestive fire — so the previously bound pratyayas in the jñānī, when combined with the "fire" of wrong-naya (deviation from shuddhanaya — the pure standpoint), transform into new karma of many types. The condition for this binding is specified: *naya-parihīna* — bereft of right naya.

This verse answers the question raised throughout the Adhikar: if the jñānī's previously bound pratyayas don't bind, what makes them bind again? Answer: slipping from shuddhanaya. The moment the jñānī's consciousness falls from the pure self-standpoint into rāgādi territory, the dormant pratyayas activate — like food activating through digestive fire.

The practical implication: remain established in shuddhanaya (the pure, direct self-knowing standpoint). Every degree of departure from it is a degree of vulnerability to the previously bound pratyayas becoming active binders. The Adhikar's closing instruction is the same as its opening diagnosis: the soul's own states determine its bondage or freedom.

**SIMPLY PUT:**
Similarly, the previously bound pratyayas of the jñānī bind karma in many forms — for those souls who have slipped away from right naya (shuddhanaya).

**CONTEMPLATE:**
What does it mean to "slip from shuddhanaya"? Can you notice the moment when pure knowing slides into something that starts selecting, preferring, rejecting — and return to just knowing?

---

## CLOSING

**The exit verse (end of Adhikar 4):**

इति आस्रवो निष्क्रान्तः।

*Thus āsrava has departed.*

The theatrical metaphor continues: āsrava entered the stage in its combat role — intoxicated with great pride, ready to battle. But jñāna (described in the opening mangal of Adhikar 4 as the incomparable bow-wielder) defeats it. Once seen clearly — as the soul's own transformations, not an external force — āsrava loses its mystique and its power to deceive. It exits the stage.
