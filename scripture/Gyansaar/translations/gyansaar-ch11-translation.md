# Gyansaar — Chapter 11: Nilepata (Non-attachment / Unstainedness)
## Shlokas ॥१॥८१॥ through ॥८॥८८॥

---

### Overview
Chapter 11 explores **nilepata** — the capacity of the jnana-siddha soul to remain unstained even while living in the soot-house of samsara. Eight shlokas systematically demonstrate how the atma, by right understanding of its own nature, passes through the world like the sky through clouds: in contact but never colored.

**Four Movements:**
1. The Kajjal-Griha & The Non-Doer (1–2)
2. Sky-Analogy & Dream-Reality (3–4)
3. Abhiman Stains, Bhavana Purifies (5–6)
4. The Two Nayas & Jnana-Kriya Unity (7–8)

---

### Shloka 1 — ॥१॥८१॥
**Sanskrit:**
संसारे निवसन् स्वायसज्ज: कज्जलवेश्मनि |
लिप्यते निखिलो लोक: ज्ञानसिद्धो न लिप्यते ॥१॥८१॥

**Translation:**
Living in the sansar — the kajjal-griha (house of soot) — the self-absorbed world gets karmically stained. But the jnana-siddha (one perfected in knowledge) does not get stained.

**Vivechan:**
The sansar is a kajjal-griha. Its walls are coated in soot; its floor is soot; its air is soot. Wherever you touch — soot. Wherever you go — soot. The feet get blackened, the hands, the chest, the back. Everyone inside becomes black. The only way to be in the kajjal-griha and remain unstained is jnana — not avoidance of the world, but the application of **jnana-rasayan** (the alchemy of right knowledge). The jnana-siddha passes through the same soot-house as everyone else. But the jnana-rasayan forms an invisible layer that prevents the soot from adhering. Without it, bhav-bhraman continues indefinitely.

### Shloka 2 — ॥२॥८२॥
**Sanskrit:**
नाहं पुद्गलभावानां, कर्ता कारयिताऽपि च |
नानुमन्ताऽपि चेत्यात्मज्ञानवान् लिप्यते कथम् ॥२॥८२॥

**Translation:**
"I am not the doer, the causer, nor the approver of paudgalik bhavas" — such an atma-jnani: how can they be stained?

**Vivechan:**
This is the operative jnana-rasayan practice. Three levels of non-identification: (1) **Karta** — "I am not the creator/doer of pudgal-bhavas." (2) **Kaarayita** — "I am not the one who causes others to create pudgal-bhavas." (3) **Anumanta** — "I am not even the approver/endorser of pudgal-bhavas." This triple renunciation of kartritva must be not an idea but a **living saturation**. The atma must be soaked in this contemplation — not once, but continuously, like a cloth soaked in kasturi until it permanently carries the fragrance. The jivatma who runs on false kartritva ("Main khata hoon... main kamata hoon... main bhogopabhog karta hoon...") creates endless karma-bandhan. This very mithya-abhiman is the glue that makes soot stick.

### Shloka 3 — ॥३॥८३॥
**Sanskrit:**
लिप्यते पुद्गलस्कन्धो, न लिप्ये पुद्गलैरहम् |
चित्रघोषांजनेनैव, ध्यायन्निति न लिप्यते ॥३॥८३॥

**Translation:**
Pudgal-skandha (matter-aggregates) stains itself. "I am not stained by pudgal." — One who meditates with this chitra-ghosh-anjana (the collyrium of pure contemplation) does not get stained.

**Vivechan:**
Upaadhyayji Maharaj's teaching of **pudgal-vigyan** — the science of understanding pudgal as radically other than the atma. The key image: **anjana** (collyrium/eye-medicine placed in the eye) — even applied directly to the organ of sight, it does not stain the sky seen through that eye. The sky with clouds of vivid colors (red, orange, blue) is never itself colored. The quote: *"Jis tarah anjana se vividh varna yukt aakash lipt nahi hota, thik usi tarah pudgalsamudaya se chetanya lipt nahi banta."* If this chintan is maintained actively during pudgal-parihhog (using, eating, experiencing pudgal), the atma does not get stained even in the act of engagement. The staining is not in the contact — it is in the identification.

### Shloka 4 — ॥४॥८४॥
**Sanskrit:**
लिप्त होते पुद्गल सभी, पुद्गलों से भी नहीं |
भ्रमल रथो न भाकाश, हूं शाश्वत सत्य यही ||
लिप्ता ज्ञान सपात-प्रतिपाताय केवलम् |
निते पंताणमात्रस्य, क्रिया सर्वेषियुस्मते ॥४॥८४॥

**Translation:**
All pudgal stains pudgal — but not me. Like the sky that is not stained by the moving chariot or clouds — I am the eternal truth. Jnana alone is the purifying agent. The appropriate kriyas for each stage must be continuously performed.

**Vivechan:**
The relationship between atma and pudgal is **svayog-svabhav** (natural coexistence through circumstance) — not identity. They inhabit the same space without becoming the same substance. The sky does not become the chariot or the cloud. This is the permanent nature of chetanya: in contact with pudgal from beginningless time, but categorically distinct. The jnana-siddha knows this — not as theory but as living recognition. Simultaneously: the appropriate kriyas for one's current bhoomika (spiritual level) must always be performed. The recognition of atma's nilepata does not eliminate the need for appropriate sadhana — it transforms the quality of that sadhana.

### Shloka 5 — ॥५॥८५॥
**Sanskrit:**
तपःश्रुतादिना मत्त: क्रियावानपि लिप्यते |
भावनाज्ञानसंपन्नो, निष्क्रियोऽपि न लिप्यते ॥५॥८५॥

**Translation:**
One intoxicated with pride of tapa, shruta (learning), and kriyas — even while performing kriyas — becomes karmically stained. But one endowed with bhavana-jnana, even if performing fewer kriyas, does not get stained.

**Vivechan:**
The devastating paradox of spiritual pride: "Main tapasvi hoon... main vidwan hoon... main vidyavan hoon... main kriyavan hoon." This kartritva-abhiman (pride of spiritual accomplishment) is itself a source of karma-bandhan, regardless of how much tapa or learning prompted it. Upaadhyayji's Prashmarati quotes Umaswati: *"Labdhva sarvam madhuran tenaiva madah katham karya?"* — having received everything from those very practices, how can one make them a source of pride? And the devastating verdict: *"Kevalmunmaad: svahridayasya sansaravriddhishcha"* — pride only creates the expansion of samsara. The antidote: four saturation-bhavanas: (1) comparing oneself to insects/worms vs the atma's infinite potential, (2) tapa-jnana help cross bhavsagar but shouldn't become identity, (3) after shruta-jnana mithyabhiman can still creep in, (4) bhavana-jnana is the required foundation for all aavashyakadi niyamas.

### Shloka 6 — ॥६॥८६॥
**Sanskrit:**
अलिप्तो निश्चयेनात्मा, सिप्तश्च व्यवहारत: |
शुद्ध्यत्यलिप्ततया ज्ञानी, क्रियावान् लिप्ततया रशा ॥६॥८६॥

**Translation:**
By Nishchaya Naya (absolute standpoint), the atma is completely unstained. By Vyavahar Naya (conventional standpoint), it is stained (karma-bound). The jnani who holds nilepata-drishti becomes truly shuddhata. The kriyavan with lipta-drishti gets cleansed through shuddhi-kriyas.

**Vivechan:**
The dual naya framework operating in concert. The jnani takes the Nishchaya marg — a complete meditation on the atma's pure svaroop: *"Main apne shuddha svabhava mein ajnani nahi... poornaroopen jnani hoon... purnadashi hoon... akrodhi hoon... nirabhamani hoon... mayarahit hoon... nirlobhi hoon... nirmohi hoon... anant veeryashali hoon... anami aur agurulaghu hoon... anahari aur avedi hoon. Mera svabhav mein na raat hai na nind hai na vikata, na roop hai, na ranga. Mera svaroop satchidanandamay hai."* Through this continuous contemplation, jnanijana become genuinely shuddhata. While from the Vyavahar side: "Main japna/ashuddhya pravrittiyo ke karana karma-vandhano se jakda hua hoon. Lekin ab satpravritiyo ko apne jivan mein sthapit karunga" — and engaging fully in kriyas of purification. Both paths lead to the same atma-shuddhi. Neither alone is sufficient.

### Shloka 7 — ॥७॥८७॥
**Sanskrit:**
ज्ञान-निषासमावेशा सद्देशोमीलने हयो |
भूमिकामेदतत्त्वज्ञ, भवेदेवेकं शुद्धता ॥७॥८७॥

**Translation:**
When jnana-drishti and kriya-drishti open together — both unlock simultaneously — jnana and kriya achieve unity. The one who knows the tattva of bhoomika-distinction attains shuddhata.

**Vivechan:**
The YogiRaj story: a man drowning in a pond, thrashing desperately. YogiRaj wades in, drags him to safety hand-in-hand through deep water. After the rescue: "When I put you in the water, why were you thrashing so desperately?" "For air — another moment and I would have died." "Is your longing for Parmatma-darshan as intense as that?" The teaching: jnana or kriya — give primacy according to your bhoomika. Up to the 6th gunasthana (pramatt-charitra), kriya has primacy. But even there, jnana-drishti is essential. In dhyanavastha, jnana has primacy, but neglecting aavashyaka kriyas still breaks shuddhi. Neither extreme works: ekanta kriya-jadtva (only kriya, no jnana) cannot produce atma-shuddhi; ekanta jnana-jadtva (only jnana, no kriya) equally cannot. Both nadiyas must open simultaneously.

### Shloka 8 — ॥८॥८८॥ *(Hero Shloka)*
**Sanskrit:**
सत्ज्ञान यदनुष्ठान, न लिप्त दोषपंकत |
शुद्धशुद्धस्वभावाय, तस्मै भगवते नम ॥८॥८८॥

**Translation:**
Sat-jnana accompanied by kriya-anushthan is not stained by dosha-panka (the mire of faults). To that Bhagavan — whose nature is shuddhashuddhsvabhav (both pure and the one who purifies) — my namaskara.

**Vivechan:**
The chapter culminates in a namaskara. Jnana-yukt kriyanushthan — kriya performed with full jnana of its nature, purpose, and proper method — does not get stained by dosh-panka. It naturally reveals the atma's shuddhbuddha svaroop. Dosh-yukt kriyas (performed with moha, abhiman, garv, kashaay, upasarg-bhiruta, vishay-akarshan) do not reveal the pure self — they feed mithyabhiman instead. The formula is precise: **dosha-rahit + samyag jnana-yukt + vidhipurvak anushthan** = atma's shuddhbuddha svaroop is revealed by itself. For karma-nirlep, jnana-kriya's vivekpurna tadatamya (wise, complete union) is essential. The chapter ends with pranama to Bhagavan — whose very svabhav is shuddhata, and whose grace is the purifying force behind all sadhana.

---

### Chapter Summary
The kajjal-griha cannot be avoided — but it can be navigated without becoming black. Eight shlokas build the complete map: jnana-rasayan is the protective layer (1) → triple non-identification as doer/causer/approver (2) → sky-analogy: contact without staining (3) → atma is eternal sky, svayog-svabhav with pudgal (4) → spiritual pride stains more than worldly action (5) → the two nayas — nishchaya and vyavahar — operating in concert (6) → jnana-drishti + kriya-drishti opening together by bhoomika (7) → sat-jnana + dosha-rahit anushthan reveals shuddhbuddha svaroop: namaskara to Bhagavan (8).
