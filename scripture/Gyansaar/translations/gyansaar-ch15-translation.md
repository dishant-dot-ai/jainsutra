# Gyansaar — Chapter 15: Vivek (Discernment)
## Shlokas ॥१॥११३॥ through ॥८॥१२०॥

---

### Overview
Chapter 15 explores **vivek** — discernment — as the bheda-jnāna that distinguishes the jīva from karma, from pudgal, from all that is not-ātmā. This is not the ordinary worldly vivek (common sense) but the razor-sharp tātvika vivek: knowing the jīva as utterly distinct from everything that has attached itself to it since anādi kāl. Eight shlokas build from the hansavṛtti of separating jīva from karma (1), through the extreme rarity of bheda-jnāna (2), the timira-roga analogy (3), the sphatikaratna-dhatura analogy (5), the ṣaṭ-kāraka ātmaika dṛṣṭi (7), to the hero shloka: saṃyama-śastra sharpened on viveka-sān (8).

**Four Movements:**
1. Hansavṛtti & The Rarity of True Bheda-Jnāna (1–2)
2. Timira-Roga & Karma-Āropana on the Pure Ātmā (3–4)
3. Dhatura Analogy & Jīva's Distinctness from All Five Dravyas (5–6)
4. Ṣaṭ-Kāraka Ātmaika Dṛṣṭi & The Saṃyama-Śastra (7–8)

---

### Shloka 1 — ॥१॥११३॥
**Sanskrit:**
कस जीव च समिसटं सयवा खीरनीरसद् ।
कस जीव व समिसट मोलसो मुनिहसो विवेचवाद् ।।१।।११३।।

**Translation:**
Just as the haṃsa (swan) separates milk from water — so the muni-haṃsa separates the jīva from karma through viveka.

**Vivechan:**
Jīva and ajīva have been mixed since anādikāl — like milk and water. The karma-jīva mixing has kept the jīva wandering in saṃsāra without respite. When the jīva attains viveka-jnāna — the knowledge that distinguishes jīva from karma precisely — the entire accumulated confusion can begin to be dismantled. The Samayasāra confirms: "bahuminnikī khaḍu khaḍo vaśasaṁ... paramāṇumisiv" — even at the level of paramāṇus, the bheda must be known and maintained. The muni who attains this bheda-jnāna experiences pūrṇānanda, rāgādi doṣas are upaśamified, and the citta becomes prasanna-praphullit.

Key recognition: the ātmā is "poorna viśuddha" — utterly pure — right now. The impurities seen in it are not ātmā's own; they are karmajanya bhāvas projected onto it. The one who recognizes this begins the actual liberation process.

### Shloka 2 — ॥२॥११४॥
**Sanskrit:**
देहात्माघविवेकोऽयं सर्वेदा सुलभो भवे ।
भवकोट्यापि तद्भेद-विवेकस्त्वति दुलभ: ।।२।।११४॥

**Translation:**
The mere "vāsanā" (habitual inclination) of body-ātmā's non-discrimination is easily available everywhere. But the true bheda-viveka — even after crores of lifetimes — is supremely rare.

**Vivechan:**
Living in body-ātmā non-discrimination is the default state of saṃsāra. It is "sulabha" — easily available — it requires no effort. What is supremely rare: knowing that a separate "ātmapadārtha" (self-substance) exists, distinct from the body. Most beings don't even know that such a distinction exists, let alone experience it.

The Samayasāra quote: "Egalastu yalamo śāvariśṇa sulamo vibhassata" — "The story of kāma-bhoga — who hasn't heard it? Who hasn't experienced it? It's extremely familiar. But the ātmā's ekākinī uniqueness — who has made it familiar?" The bheda-jnāna of the viśuddha ātmā is supremely rare because the prerequisites are demanding: viṣaya-vairāgya (dispassion toward sense objects), kaṣāya-upaśama (calming of the passions), nine-tattva śraddhā, and then the ātmā's natural anurāga toward its own svarūpa arising. Only then does bheda-jnāna become possible — and even then, it is alabhya (extraordinary) to maintain.

### Shloka 3 — ॥३॥११५॥
**Sanskrit:**
शुद्धेऽपि ध्योम्नि तिमिराद् रेखाभिमिश्रता यथा ।
विकारैर्मिश्रता भाति तथास्त्वन्यविवेकत: ।।३।।११५।।

**Translation:**
Just as in the clear sky, due to the timira-roga (eye-disease), colored streaks appear — so too in the pure ātmā, due to aviveka, the vikaras appear intermixed.

**Vivechan:**
The sky is utterly clear and pure. But to the eye afflicted with timira-roga, the sky appears full of colored lines and shapes — nīla, pīta, vichitradigvikaras. The vivekavān knows: these are not in the sky; they are in the afflicted vision. The avivekī says: "See, the sky has streaks, it is vikarātmaka!"

So too with the ātmā. Pure ātmā, in the avivekī's view, appears full of vikaras — janma, jarā, mṛtyu, rāga, dveṣa, moha. The vivekī knows: "These are not the ātmā's own vikaras. These are karmajanya bhāvas projected by the afflicted aviveka-dṛṣṭi. The ātmā is always shuddha, always chinmātra, always distinct." The Adhyātmasāra confirms: "Ātmajnāna-phalaṁ dhyānam ātmajnāna ca muktidaṁ" — ātmajnāna's phala is dhyāna; ātmajnāna itself is mokṣa-giving. Bheda-jnāna = ātmajnāna — the cure for the timira-roga.

### Shloka 4 — ॥४॥११६॥
**Sanskrit:**
यथा योधैः कुतं युद्ध स्वामिन्येवोपचर्यते ।
शुद्धात्मन्यविवेकेन कमस्कन्धोजितं तथा ।।४।।११६।।

**Translation:**
Just as a war fought by soldiers is credited to the king — so too, due to aviveka, the karmaskandhajaya's phala (fruit of karma's victory) is attributed to the pure ātmā.

**Vivechan:**
Soldiers fight — but "the king won" or "the king lost," says the people. The king receives the credit (or blame) for what the soldiers did. Similarly: karmapudgal creates puṇya and pāpa through its own activity — but aviveka credits the pure ātmā with "ātmā did puṇya" and "ātmā did pāpa." The Adhyātmasāra quotes confirm: "janmādiko'pi nimittaṁ pariṇāmo hi karmaṇām | na ca karma tūlato bheda syādātmanyavikaraṇi ||" — birth, old age, death, etc. are all karmajanya pariṇāmas; they do not belong to the avikārī ātmā. But the jnānabhrashṭa attributes karmajanya vikṛti to the ātmā — like seeing a crystal as red due to a red cloth nearby. The sphatikaratna appears red because of the red background — not because it is red. Similarly, the ātmā appears to have janmādi-vikṛti because of karma's proximity — not because it is vikārī. This mithyā āropana is the root of bhava-bhrāmaṇa.

### Shloka 5 — ॥५॥११७॥ (numbered ॥५॥११९॥ in text variant)
**Sanskrit:**
इष्टकाद्यपि हि स्वर्णं पीतोश्मतो वयेक्षते ।
आत्माऽपेदऽसत्तह्रद् देहादादविवेकिन: ।।५।।११७।।

**Translation:**
Just as the one who has drunk dhatura-juice sees even bricks and stones as gold — the avivekī also sees the ātmā in the śarīra-indriyādi (body and senses).

**Vivechan:**
Dhatura-rasa creates viparītatā in the dṛṣṭi — whatever the person sees, they see as gold everywhere. Avidyā and aviveka have a similar effect, no less than dhatura: the avivekī sees ātmā in the body, in the indriyas, in the mind — identifies with them, treats them as the ātmā itself. The recognition needed: ātmā's distinctness from all five dravyas:
- Pudgalāstikāya's dharma = mūrtatā (form-ness); ātmā's guṇa = jnāna → ātmā is bhinna from pudgala
- Dharmāstikāya's dharma = gatihetuatā; ātmā's guṇa = jnāna → bhinna
- Adharmāstikāya's dharma = sthitihetuatā; ātmā's guṇa = jnāna → bhinna
- Ākāśāstikāya's dharma = avakāśa; ātmā's guṇa = jnāna → bhinna

Indriya, bala, śvāsocchvāsa, āyuṣya — these are dravya-prāṇa, pudgal's paryāya — utterly distinct from ātmā. "Jīvo jīvita na prāṇovirnā tere jīvita" — the ātmā lives not because of these dravya-prāṇas. This is the dhatura-roga's cure: seeing each dravya in its own distinct svabhāva.

### Shloka 6 — ॥६॥११८॥
**Sanskrit:**
इच्छन् न परमान् भावान् विवेकाड्रे पतत्यष् ।
परम भावमनिच्छन् नाविवेके निमज्जति ।।६।।११८।।

**Translation:**
The one who does not seek the paramottama bhāvas falls from the peak of viveka. The one who seeks paramabhāva never sinks into aviveka.

**Vivechan:**
Seeking śuddha-caitanyabhāva — viśuddha ātmabhāva — lifts the jīva to viveka's supreme peak. Neglecting śuddha-caitanyabhāva throws it into the deep valley where aviveka's rākṣasī jaws devour it.

Viveka-giriraj's summit = apramattabhāva. At that summit, the apramattu ātmā receives rare siddhis and labdhis — but the viśuddha-ātmabhāva-yukta jīva is utterly udāsīna (indifferent) toward these. Praśamarati quotes (Umāsvātijī): "sātādi stotra-guṇa prāvyādi viśuddhi... — sākala praśamaratim śule na bhajati satyāṃ muniḥ sammam ||" — "In this world, other jīvas would struggle enormously to obtain such rare siddhi-labdhi. But the muni who has attained praśamarati cannot be moved by them." And: "yā sattvaguṇaratiṁ... nāghati sahasrabhāgaṁ koṭiśata..." — all devas' combined sampat cannot equal one-thousandth of the muni's adhyātma-sampat.

The fourteen viveka-lakṣaṇas listed: dhyāna-nimagnatā, prabodhitā, dharma-praśāntatā, nirabhimāna, sāgara-rahita nirmalatā, labdhābhijatma, śatru-mitra samadhṛṣṭi, ātmā-rāma, duḥkha-sukhā ke prati samadṛṣṭi, svādhyāya-pramattatā, uttama dharmadhyāna-viśuddhi, buddhi-gata viśuddhi, vaidya śarīra-viśuddhi, leśyā-viśuddhi.

### Shloka 7 — ॥७॥११९॥
**Sanskrit:**
आत्मन्येवात्मन. कुर्यात् य: पट्कारकसंगितम् ।
अवाविवेकजवरस्पास्य. वेद्यम्यं जडमज्जनात् ।।७।।११३।।

**Translation:**
The one who establishes the ṣaṭ-kāraka (six-case) relationship entirely within the ātmā — for them, the fever of aviveka arising from immersion in jaḍa-pudgal cannot afflict them.

**Vivechan:**
The six kārakas of grammar applied entirely to the ātmā:
1. **Kartā** (agent): Ātmā does jnāna-darśana — ātmā is kartā
2. **Karma** (object): Ātmā's pāta-sahita pariṇāma is ātmā's ādheyasthāna — ātmā is karma
3. **Karaṇa** (instrument): Ātmā is the upakāraṇa for jnāna-kriyā through upabhoga — ātmā is karaṇa
4. **Sampradāna** (recipient): Ātmā is the dhāmapātra for śuddha pariṇāma — ātmā is sampradāna
5. **Apādāna** (source): From pūrva paryāyas' vinaṣṭatā and new paryāyas' udāraṇa — ātmā is apādāna
6. **Adhikaraṇa** (locus): The kṣetra of all śuddha-paryāyas is ātmā's pradeśa — ātmā is adhikaraṇa

When all six relationships are established with ātmā — an ātmādvaita world is created. Kart ā is ātmā, karma is ātmā, karaṇa is ātmā... wherever one looks — ātmā. This is ātmānanda's fullness. When the saṃbandha with pudgala is severed and all six kārakas find their home in ātmā — aviveka has no root, viṣamatā has no basis. This is viveka: the complete reorientation of all relationships toward the ātmā.

### Shloka 8 — ॥८॥१२०॥ *(Hero Shloka)*
**Sanskrit:**
संयमास्त्रं विवेकेन धाण्यनोनोरोजितं मुने: ।
घृतिघारोल्वणं कर्मनात्रुच्छेदक्षमं भवेत् ।।८।।१२०।।

**Translation:**
The muni's saṃyama-śastra (weapon of restraint), sharpened on viveka-sān (whetstone of discernment) and made fierce with dhṛti-dhāra (the edge of endurance) — becomes capable of utterly destroying the karma-enemy.

**Vivechan:**
Three instruments for karma-kṣaya: **saṃyama** (restraint), **santoṣa** (contentment), **viveka** (discernment). The metaphor: a weapon needs a whetstone to be sharp enough to cut the enemy. Here, saṃyama is the śastra; viveka is the sān (whetstone); dhṛti (endurance) is the razor-edge.

The Khapaka Muni story: a royal servant was flaying the muni's skin with great cruelty. The muni held dhṛti and sharpened the saṃyama-śastra on viveka-sān. While the body's skin was being stripped, the muni was engaged in stripping karma's skin with the saṃyama-weapon. Result: in an instant, countless karmas were dissolved and the ātmā became free from pudgala-niyantaṇas. No disturbance, no asaṃyama, no āvṛtti-bhāvanā — while blood flowed and unbearable vedanā was present. This was possible only through viveka-śakti: knowing utterly that śarīra and ātmā are distinct, and therefore śarīra's vikṛtis cannot touch ātmā's bhāva.

Other examples: Rāyamaśūrijī Muni's 500 disciples who were beaten; Śukramāra Muni thrown into molten metal; all maintained saṃyama through bheda-jnāna alone.

The call to constant practice: "Yadi bhedajnāna kā abhyāsa, cintana, magan, manana aur prayoga jīvan meṁ nirantara cālaū rahegā, tabhī mṛtyu ke samaya vah (bhedajnāna) hamārī taraph kāma karegā."

---

### Chapter Summary
Vivek is not practical wisdom but the bheda-jnāna that knows jīva as utterly distinct from karma, from pudgal, from all that is not-ātmā. Eight shlokas: the hansavṛtti of separating jīva from karma (1) → bheda-jnāna's extreme rarity — denser than crores of lifetimes of kāmabhoga (2) → timira-roga analogy: vikaras in ātmā are like colored streaks in clear sky — seen only by afflicted vision (3) → karma's actions erroneously attributed to the pure ātmā like soldiers' war attributed to king (4) → dhatura-dṛṣṭi: avivekī sees ātmā in body like dhatura-drinker sees gold in bricks; ātmā distinct from all five dravyas (5) → seeking paramabhāva = viveka's summit; apramattabhāva as viveka-giriraj's peak with fourteen lakṣaṇas (6) → ṣaṭ-kāraka all applied to ātmā alone: the ātmādvaita dṛṣṭi that leaves no room for aviveka (7) → saṃyama-śastra sharpened on viveka-sān: Khapaka Muni as the proof (8).
