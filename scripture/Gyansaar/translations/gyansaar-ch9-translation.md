### Chapter Introduction (Title Page — PDF p.113)

**Chapter Name:** ९. क्रिया (Kriya — Right Action)

The introduction opens with a provocative question: "If religious actions are not performed, what is the loss?" — a question asked for years, but nobody asks: "If paap-kriyas are not performed, what is the loss?" Because paap-kriyas are liked by everyone. If dharma is liked, dharmic kriyas must be liked too. If moksha is desired, the kriyas necessary for it must be desired. The author attempts here to show the absolute necessity and indispensability of kriya in life for moksha.

For the indriyas-conqueror — five qualities are presented: 1. Jnani, 2. Kriyapar, 3. Shant (upasham), 4. Bhavit-atma, 5. Jit-endriya.

### Shloka 1 — ॥१॥६५॥

**Sanskrit:**
ज्ञानी क्रियापर ज्ञा लो, भावितात्मा तितीर्षय ।
स्वयं तीर्णों भवसागरे, परांस्तारयितु क्षम ॥१॥६५॥

**Translation:**
Know: one who is jnani AND kriyapar AND bhavantima (one whose soul is pervaded with dharma-bhaav) — such a one crosses the bhavsagar themselves and has the capacity to carry others across too.

**Anvayartha:**
Jnani: one who crosses bhavsagar, knowing its fearsome nature — without the jnanakari's help (from Arihanta, Sadguru), crossing is not possible. Kriyapar: Jinesha Bhagavan has shown the kriyas for crossing bhavsagar — alertness to these at all times is essential. Shant: shanti/samata/upasham — even if jnana is present, even if kriya is present, without upasham it is impossible to cross. Bhavit-atma: the soul pervaded with jnana-darshan-charitra — like kasturi-fragrant cloth spreads its scent everywhere, jnana-darshana-charitra's fragrance continuously spreads. Jit-endriya: the jivatma seeking to cross bhavsagar must keep its indriyas in control — uncontrolled indriyas throw the jivatma from the boat into the sea.

**Vivechan:**
The highest purpose of human life is to cross the bhavsagar. If this bhavana has become awakened in every pore of the jivatma, then jnana and kriya will come together without delay. From time immemorial, it is a natural law: when the desire for something is born, its correct recognition, the upay for obtaining it, and the purushartha to do so — all naturally arise. Whoever has awakened the desire for the incomparable param-sansati, what effort will they not make for attaining jnana?

**Simply Put:**
Five qualities cross the bhavsagar together — not one alone. Jnana without kriya is a map without walking. Kriya without jnana is walking blind. Upasham without both is stillness without direction. All five together, in one person — that is the one who can cross and carry others.

**Contemplate:**
Which of the five qualities is most developed in you? Which is most missing? What would integrating all five look like?

**Concept Tags:** Jnani · Kriyapar · Bhavit-atma · Jit-endriya · Bhavsagar · Panchaguna

### Shloka 2 — ॥२॥६६॥

**Sanskrit:**
क्रियाविरहिता मार्गः, ज्ञानमात्रमनत्यकम् ।
गति विना पत्ज्ञोऽपि, नाप्नोति पुरुषीस्तिताम् ॥२॥६६॥

**Translation:**
A path without kriya — mere jnana alone without action — cannot arrive anywhere. Even one who knows the directions perfectly cannot reach the desired destination without movement.

**Anvayartha:**
Like knowing the road to Delhi from every angle — every route, every shortcut — but not boarding the train, not buying the ticket, not taking even a single step: you will never arrive. Knowledge of the path is not the path. Jnana without kriya is like a lamp that has been prepared but never lit.

**Vivechan:**
Do you know the path to moksha? What good is that knowledge? What is there to boast about? Rajmarg tum jaante ho — but the railpath's jankari is also yours. You know the train's timetable. But you made no effort to prepare the ticket, board the train, endure the journey. So you will never reach Delhi. Only those who started, walked, bore the heat and cold of the path — they reach. Jnana of the path alone on the basis of jnana — without kriya, without motion — gati (progress) will never happen. The mohamarg ke liye — for those who want to renounce moha — if dharmapath ka jnana ho lekin kriya na karo, then after death all that jnana on one side, the paap-kriyas will overwhelm. Jnana without kriya is actually a danger.

Paramabhakti, pratikraman, samayik, sutra-svadhyaya, dhyan, gurubhakti, glan-seva, pratilekhana, tapa-tyagadi — these are all kriyas. Without these, the jivatma that wanders in aatma-vishuddhi seeking cannot go anywhere.

**Simply Put:**
You know the road. You know every turn. But you haven't moved. Jnana without kriya — perfectly knowing and never walking — delivers nothing. The one who walks even with less knowledge reaches; the one who knows everything but never walks does not.

**Contemplate:**
What do you know intellectually about the spiritual path that you are not yet living? What is the gap between your jnana and your kriya?

**Concept Tags:** Kriya · Jnana · Moksha-marg · Delhi train analogy · Purushartha

### Shloka 3 — ॥३॥६७॥

**Sanskrit:**
स्वाध्यूक्षला क्रिया काले, ज्ञानपूर्ण ऽपेक्षलते ।
प्रबोधि स्वप्रकाशोऽपि, सैलयोपवयोदिकम् ॥३॥६७॥

**Translation:**
The kriya done in the right time (svaadhyaya-kala), with jnana-purnatva as its foundation, becomes self-illuminating (svaprakaash) — like the lamp that is already prakaash-svarup does not neglect the act of filling with ghee.

**Anvayartha:**
Jab tak siddha-prapt na ho aur sadhak dasha vidyaman hai, tab tak kriya-avashyakata hoti hai. Svabhav ko pushta karne ke liye, nimrul avastha mein bhi bhin-bhin kriyon ki avashyakata hoti hai. Kevaljanion mahapurushon ko bhi kevalajanion ko kevaljanibhi kriya-avashyakata rahati hai.

For crossing bhavsagar, five qualities are needed: 1. Jnani, 2. Kriyapar, 3. Shant, 4. Bhavit-atma, 5. Jit-endriya. The svabhav-pushing kriya has the goal of guna-vruddhi. Each of the three stages (deshvirat, sarvvirat, samnirvrittam-sadhu) has specific kriyas: deshvirat must follow 67 types of vyavhar-niyama purified. Sarvvirat jiva follows panchachara (jnanachara etc) in the outer-inner manner. Samnirvrittam-sadhu must practice shukladhyana at all times.

**Simply Put:**
Even the lamp that is light itself needs to be filled with ghee. Even the soul that is already prakaash-svarup needs kriya — the act of self-maintenance, self-renewal. Kriya is not a sign of incompleteness; it is the act of remaining complete.

**Contemplate:**
What is your daily "ghee-filling" practice? What kriya do you do that keeps your inner light burning?

**Concept Tags:** Svaadhyaya · Kriya-kala · Svaprakaash · Lamp analogy · Guna-vruddhi

### Shloka 4 — ॥४॥६८॥

**Sanskrit:**
भी आलस्य, वेठ, अविविध अथवा उदासीनता न हो ।
बल्कि सटैठ प्रदस्य उत्साह और असीम उल्लास होना चाहिये ॥४॥६८॥

**Translation:**
No laziness, no boredom, no indifference, no carelessness should arise. On the contrary — unwavering enthusiasm and boundless joy should characterize all kriya.

**Anvayartha:**
The three stages — deshvirat (sravak, 67 types of vyavhar), sarvvirat (full renunciant, panchachara), samnirvrittam (shukladhyan sadhu) — in all three the kriyas differ. But the spirit animating all three must be the same: not mechanical compliance, but eager loving purushartha. The difference is not in kriya-type but in the bhaav with which kriya is done. Jnana, darshan, tap, charitra, all achar — their antardashaavalokana is required.

**Vivechan:**
Shanti (samata, upasham) is an absolute necessity. Even if jnana is present, even if kriya is present — if krodh and rog arise in the mind, jnana and kriya become lifeless and dead. The boat roaming in bhavsagar stops — becomes stuck, sinks. The rushing river current of avyarogeho breaks the anchor line. If we have not removed the fearsome sea-creatures of krodh, rog, irshya from our boat, they will make holes in the vessel — and give us jal-samadhi. This truth is what Upadhyayaji Maharaj states: the jivatma seeking to cross bhavsagar must be shant-prashant, kshamasheela, and param upasham-yukta.

**Simply Put:**
Kriya without bhaav is dead ritual. Bhaav without kriya is dream without action. The union of the two — with enthusiasm and without laziness — is the living spiritual practice.

**Contemplate:**
Which of your spiritual practices have become mechanical? How can you reinject enthusiasm and awe into them?

**Concept Tags:** Utsah · Ullasa · Shant · Upasham · Kriya-bhaav union

### Shloka 5 — ॥५॥६९॥

**Sanskrit:**
तत्राष्टमे गुणस्थाने, शुक्लसद्ध्यानमादिमम् ।
ध्यालुं प्रक्रमते साधु राढ्यसंहृननान्वित ॥५॥६९॥

**Translation:**
At the eighth gunasthana (the stage of apurvakarana), the saadhu begins the first shukladhyana, armed with the first vajra-rishabha-naraach body-structure — this is the apex of kriya.

**Anvayartha:**
At the eighth gunasthana, the first vajra-rishabha-naraach-sanyoga saadhu begins shukladhyana. The purpose is clear: reaching purnajnani-state requires kriya at every stage. Even the soul that has burned ghati-karmas and become purnajna must perform yoginirodha (yoga-cessation) and samudghaat (body-projection). For reaching purnata's highest peak, necessary kriya must be done at every stage.

**Vivechan:**
Even from the logic angle, the importance of kriya can be understood. From time immemorial, jivnatma has been drowning in paap-kriyas and wandering in samsara. Our paap-kriyas are the root cause of bhav-bhraman. To stop bhav-bhraman, first its causes must be resolved, destroyed. Paap-kriyas' antidote is dharmic-kriyas. Even light (bijli) — which is itself prakaash-svarup — needs 'switch on' and powerhouse connection. Similarly even the most elevated soul needs kriyas for self-expression.

**Simply Put:**
Even the Arihant needed kriya to reach Arihantship. No stage is exempt from the necessity of appropriate action. The higher the stage, the more refined the kriya — but kriya itself never becomes unnecessary until the very end.

**Contemplate:**
Are you at a stage where you are using "I'm beyond kriya" as an excuse not to practice? What is the appropriate kriya for your current stage?

**Concept Tags:** Gunasthana · Shukladhyana · Sarvvarjit · Kriya at every level · Vajra-naraach

### Shloka 6 — ॥६॥७०॥

**Sanskrit:**
बाह्यभांग पुरश्कृत्य, ये क्रिया व्यमहारत ।
वत्ने कवलक्षेय विगते तु तुष्टिकालिम ॥६॥७०॥

**Translation:**
Those who perform kriyas only for external display and worldly honor — without inner bhaav — derive no true benefit. Only those with the inner spirit of moksha-bhavna gain true contentment from their kriyas.

**Anvayartha:**
Bhaav bhi apni visheshata hai. Agar pratisama sava ki suraksha na ho, to raha nahi chalti. Aise shubha (bhaavs), but if sadhvani na karo to shubha-bhaavs ka bahir fasion nahi layenge. Atirik shubha, lekin chanchala bhaavs ka parivartana nahi dikh jata hai. Yahan shubha bhaav means — janakari ki prashansa-ninda adi bhaav nah ho. Ghara ka namo-nishaan nahi — swachcha, nirabha akash! Poornima ki dhavala rajani aur solah kalaaon se purna-vikasit chandra. The moon was always there — it is the clouds that covered it. Jab antim bhaav tab hota hai jab gadri baa mein dharti par girkar phuton mein fasion lagate hain. The falling state — the time when we must again pick up and re-establish contact with sadguru.

**Vivechan:**
What are you doing kriyas for? If the goal is dharma-labha but not atma-vishuddhi, then something is missing. The shubha-bhaav ki sachchi suraksha karna hamara param kartavya hai. When prashant bhavana yadi sadhvani na ho, then the kriyas done with shubha bhaav may sprout on the surface but the kshan bhav will not be able to sustain.

Three examples from history: (1) Raja Harishchandra — for protecting satya, he renounced everything: राजसी ठाट-ठाट, vaibhav, vilaas. (2) Mahakarunia Maharaj Kumar Meghavar — for protecting ahimsa-bhaav, he saved the life of a pigeon. (3) Santil — for protecting the sanvatsam-bhaav of that fast, Nirupamati Ravana endured the severest tests.

**Simply Put:**
Kriyas without bhaav are theater. What matters is: what is the soul behind the action? If the kriyas come from moksha-bhavna, they build the inner treasure. If they come from praise-seeking, they build only a reputation.

**Contemplate:**
What is driving your spiritual practice right now? Recognition? Habit? Or genuine moksha-bhavna?

**Concept Tags:** Bhaav · Moksha-bhavna · Parishudddhi · External vs. inner kriya · Harishchandra

### Shloka 7 — ॥७॥७१॥

**Sanskrit:**
गुणवद्बहुमानादिनित्यस्मृत्या च सतिक्रया ।
ज्ञात न पातवेदु भावमज्ञात जनधेशपि ॥७॥७१॥

**Translation:**
Through constant remembrance (nitya-smriti) of the virtuous (gunavad) with great reverence (bahuman) — the kriya becomes alive. This shubha-bhaav protects the unknowing soul from falling, even the most ignorant.

**Anvayartha:**
Five shubha-bhaav protectors that guard the kriya's spirit:
1. **Vrat-smarana** — When ashubbha bhaav attacks our shubha bhaavs, we must constantly recall the vrat/pratigya we took. The soul becomes ajey-shakti. When kaamonnmatta sundari attacked Bhanakria Muni, the munivar said with shant-chitta: "मन-वचन-काया से प्रहित, लिया व्रत नहीं भंग करूँ | अविचल रहूँ ध्रुव सा निज तप में पुन. संसार का न मोह धरूँ"
2. **Gunashali-samman** — The gunashali: soldiers armed with shubha-bhavanaon ke shastr. Towards them: agaadha shraddha, param bhakti, apaar priti-bhaav in sankatakal — they run to help us and protect our atmashakti.
3. **Paap-jugupsa** — For the paaps we have already renounced, we must never allow any attraction to develop. The moha ki supta bhavana must never arise. We must always have ghrina toward them.
4. **Parinaam-alochan** — We must continuously contemplate the results of paap-kriya and dharma-kriya. 'Dukha papat, sukha dharmat' — this sutra must remain in smriti.
5. **Tirthankara-bhakti** — Devadhistev Tirthankara Bhagavan ka namasmarana, darshan-pujana, and ceaseless smaran-chintan of their upkaaras.

**Simply Put:**
Five guards protect the inner bhaav: vrat-remembrance, reverence for the virtuous, disgust for past sins, contemplating consequences, and bhakti toward the Tirthankara. With these five at every station, the journey stays on track.

**Contemplate:**
Which of the five guards is most active in your life right now? Which one have you neglected?

**Concept Tags:** Vrat-smarana · Gunashali · Paap-jugupsa · Parinaam-alochan · Tirthankara-bhakti

### Shloka 8 — ॥८॥७२॥

**Sanskrit:**
वषोद्युक्ठानतोऽसागनियासागतिमगति ।
सेय ज्ञान क्रियाऽस्मुमिरान-दिपच्छला ॥८॥७२॥

**Translation:**
When Jinesha-deva's love-bhakti pervades the jivatma fully, the soul is drenched in the sugandhit water of his vachana — and an adbhut vishuddh virya (pure spiritual energy) surges up. Through this virya, the capacity to understand the Priyatam Parmatma's gahan vachana in yatharth rupa becomes available — and commitment to live accordingly becomes firm.

**Anvayartha:**
Jivnatma ke sadhana ka samay kewal ek dhyey, ek hi dhyey aur ek hi sarana rahni chahiye: 'guru-vruddhi.' Pravesh shuddha-bhava ka lakshya / dhyey aur guru-bhavana saath ek maan dharatmuna ki abhimuddhita ho honi chahiye. Vyapaari ek dhyey: ghan-vruddhi. Dhan batorne ke liye, sampatti ikathhee karne ke liye vah har sambhav marga apnata hai, upay aur yojanaon ko karyanvit karta hai. Bhale hi uska prayas, apnaya marga apaar kasht aur ayak parishram wala kyon na ho? Lekin vah lakshya-purti ke liye sada-sarvada sachet, sajag aur sacchet rahta hai.

Four key items for this asang-anushthan:
1. Paramatma Jineshvar Dev ke prati ananya-priti
2. Paramatma Jineshvar Dev ke prati shraddha-bhakti
3. Paramatma Jineshvar Dev ke vachanon ka sarvangin jnana
4. Ukt jnana ke madhyam se jinavachananusar jivan-pavana karne hetu mahapurushartha

When priti-bhakti toward Parmatma Jineshvar arises, the physical/paudgalik sambandh breaks. Shabd-rupa-rasa-gandha ke bandhan toot lagne lagte hain. Mohandhkamandha jivon ka aadar-satkar karne ki pravritti band ho jati hai.

**Simply Put:**
The final key to kriya: love the Jina. Not as ritual, not as duty — but as your deepest longing. When that love becomes total, every kriya becomes natural and self-sustaining. The energy of bhakti carries the jivnatma across.

**Contemplate:**
Does your practice include genuine priti toward Jineshvar — or only formal respect? What would it look like to truly love the Jina?

**Concept Tags:** Jinesha-bhakti · Asang-anushthan · Vishuddh-virya · Ananya-priti · Mahapurushartha

### Chapter Summary

**Key Theme:** Kriya is not ritual — it is the activated expression of jnana and bhavana. The chapter systematically refutes jnana-alone as sufficient. Five qualities together cross the bhavsagar (Shloka 1); jnana without kriya never arrives (Shloka 2); kriya must be timely and self-illuminating (Shloka 3); enthusiasm not laziness must animate it (Shloka 4); even the highest gunasthan requires appropriate kriya (Shloka 5); bhaav must animate all kriya (Shloka 6); five guards protect shubha-bhaav (Shloka 7); and Jina-bhakti is the supreme energy source (Shloka 8).

**Chapter Arc — Four Movements:**
1. Five Qualities for Crossing (Shlokas 1–2): Jnani + Kriyapar + Shant + Bhavit-atma + Jit-endriya; jnana without kriya = knowing the road but never walking
2. The Spirit of Kriya (Shlokas 3–4): Right time, right bhaav, enthusiasm — the lamp needs ghee even if it is prakaash-svarup
3. Kriya at Every Level (Shlokas 5–6): Even the 8th gunasthana requires shukladhyana; external kriya without inner bhaav = empty theater
4. The Five Guards and Jina-Love (Shlokas 7–8): Vrat-smarana, gunashali, paap-jugupsa, parinaam-alochan, Tirthankara-bhakti; and the supreme energizer — ananya-priti toward Jineshvar
