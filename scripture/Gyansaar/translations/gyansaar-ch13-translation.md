# Gyansaar — Chapter 13: Maun (Silence)
## Shlokas ॥१॥९७॥ through ॥८॥१०४॥

---

### Overview
Chapter 13 explores **maun** — silence — in its complete, three-dimensional form. Not the silence of a closed mouth, but the silence of manas, vachan, and kāya turned toward the ātma. Eight shlokas map the full architecture of authentic maun, from its root in jagat-tattva-jnāna to the jyotiṣmayī dīpa whose very nature is light.

**Four Movements:**
1. Jagat-Tattva-Jnāna & The Muni's True Svabhāv (1–2)
2. Chāritra as Ātma-Anugaman & The Two Nayas (3–4)
3. When Jnāna-Śraddhā Produces No Shuddhi (5–6)
4. The Three Mauns & The Jyotiṣmayī Dīpa (7–8)

---

### Shloka 1 — ॥१॥९७॥
**Sanskrit:**
मन्यते यो जगत्तत्त्वं स मुनिः परिकीर्तित |
सम्यक्त्वमेव तन्मौनं सम्यक्त्वमेव वा ॥१॥९७॥

**Translation:**
The one who knows jagat-tattva (the true nature of the world/reality) is called a muni. That very samyaktva is maun — and that very samyaktva IS maun.

**Vivechan:**
Maun-mārga is the path of the muni-jīvan's ārādhanā. Maun must be understood through the ātma's svabhāv — it is the muni's jīvantā svarūp. The 'muni' etymology: "manyate jagattattvaṁ so muniḥ" — the one who knows jagat-tattva is the muni. Among the anant svarūps of the muni, one svarūp is considered through the 'evambhūt' naya perspective: the one who knows jagat-tattva. The evambhūt naya identifies a vastu by its characteristic svarūp in action — here, the muni is the one actively engaged in knowing. Jnāna-jnāna-doṭa (the jnāna of jnāna itself): jagat-tattva-jnāna = samyaktva = śramaṇatva. The equation: Jaga-tattva kā jnān = samyaktva; samyaktva = śramaṇatva; therefore jagat-tattva kā jnān = śramaṇatva.

### Shloka 2 — ॥२॥९८॥
**Sanskrit:**
आत्मोडस्त्रमेयेव मञ्चशुद्ध जानात्मात्मानमात्मना |
सेय रत्नत्रये शान्तिरध्याचारैरता मुने ॥२॥९८॥

**Translation:**
The ātmā knows the pure ātmā by the ātmā through the ātmā itself. This is the ratnatraya (jnāna-darśan-chāritra) in its state of śānti and abheda-parijñāti — this is the muni's authentic path.

**Vivechan:**
Ātmasya svābhāvin vedanā requires ātmā's svābhāvika jnāna. This 'abheda-parijñāti' (unbroken self-recognition) is the ādhārbhūt upāy: ātmā (moha-tyāg kar) → ātmā ko ('sarvajñānamay') → ātmā dvārā (śrutajnān) → ātmā mein (sarva-guṇa-paryāyamay jāne). The Samayprābhṛt confirms: "Jo hi śuṇṇāabhigacchhaï appāṇaṁ tu kevalaṁ śuddhaṁ | taṁ suakkevalimisinṇo bhaṇṇati logappadīvayarā ||" — Those who know the pure ātmā through śrutajnān, the best śrutakevalī call them the luminaries of the world. When this firm conviction becomes established — 'Main sādhy-sādhak aur siddha-svarūp hūṁ' — jnāna-darśan and chāritrādi guṇos se yukt hūṁ — aisī jnāna-dṛṣṭi appaneāp prakaṭ ho jātī hai. It is the ratnatraya's abheda-parijñāti. In it alone is the authentic anubhav of ātmasukha's anupam saṃvedanā.

### Shloka 3 — ॥३॥९९॥
**Sanskrit:**
चारित्रमात्मचरणाद् ज्ञान वा दर्शन मुने |
शुद्धजानमये साध्ये नियालाभात् क्रियानये ॥३॥९९॥

**Translation:**
Chāritra is ātma-anugaman (following the ātmā). The sādhya for the muni is shuddhajnāna. By the jñānanaya standpoint: after ātmajnāna, appropriate kriyā must integrate into jīvan.

**Vivechan:**
Ātmā ke sāmnay mein chāritra karnā — that is chāritra. The muni's sādhya is always chāritra. The svarūp of this chāritra through shuddhajnāna aur nishchayanaya: chāritra = ātmā mein anugaman karnā = paudgalik māyā se nivrut honā = ātma-svarup mein ramaṇā karnā = ātmā, jo ki chetanāsvarūp hai, us mein ḍūb jānā = ātmā ke ātmasvabhāv mein ramyhatā. The satsya yahi hai: ātmajnān mein sthirtā yahī chāritra hai — aur chāritra kā matalab ātma-jnān mein ramaṇā. Jnān aur chāritra mein abhed hai. The equation (jnānanaya formula): jnānasya phalaṁ virati; viratiphalaṁ āśravanirodha; saṁvaraphalaṁ tapobalaṁ; tapaso nirjarāphalaṁ iṣṭam. Shraman ke liye jo chāritra sādhya hai, vah jnānasvarūp nahīṁ, balki jnān ke phalasvarūp hai. "Jo bhī karnā hai ātmā ke liye kar. He jīva, mana-vachan aur kāyā kā viniyog ātmā mein hī kar de."

### Shloka 4 — ॥४॥१००॥
**Sanskrit:**
यत प्रवृत्तिने मणौ, लभ्यते वानतत्फलम् |
अतात्विकी सरिण्ज्ञप्ति-सरिण्ज्ञश्रद्धा च सा यथा ॥४॥१००॥

**Translation:**
The pravṛtti (action) of the muni in which no ātmik phala is obtained — that is atātvikī (inauthentic). Similarly, the seriousness of jnāna and the śraddhā must be of the right kind.

**Vivechan:**
Jis tarah mani (gem) kī śuddhatā kē bal par ratna manana, "Yeh ratna hai? Saśkī koi prāptī ratne kī pravśī karnegā?" — vāstav mein ratna kā koi phala jis muni-murtā mein detā hai, us mein koī bhī phala milne wāla nahi hotā. Vāstave ratna hai — aur bhayankara viśay kā sarvottam nāsh karnā hai. Dharmī muni yadi kisī jauhrī ke hāth-becā jāe, to lākhoṁ kī mātra pūṭ honge — lekin śarīr kā puṣṭā nahīṁ māntā. Real yog śraddha is here — mani aur śraddhā — do bātāeṁ (1) maun śraddha kā pāvana kar n jāne yathā abdhyabhūt mani yahāṁ batāyā gaya hai. Yug śuddha ātmasvabhāv kī nijhaktanā sādhanā wālā ātmasvabhāv kā sahi prashnā kā uttar hamāre hindubar hatyā hai — toh humhāra dharm aur humāre chāchāraṇa mein viśuddh ātmā ko ānandita honā chāhie — karmoṁ kī gaharāī khelasiyān nahīṁ, nā hī karmā kā bhīśit prabhāv.

### Shloka 5 — ॥५॥१०१॥
**Sanskrit:**
तथा यतो न शुद्धात्मस्वभावाऽऽस्शरणं भवेत् |
फलं दोषनिवृत्तिवां, न तद् ज्ञानं न दर्शनम् ॥५॥१०१॥

**Translation:**
That from which no resort to śuddhātma-svabhāv arises — from which no phala of doṣa-nivṛtti (cessation of faults) comes — that is neither jnāna nor darśan in the true sense.

**Vivechan:**
Jnān aur śraddhā kā māpan na jāne yathā: abdhyabhūt mani yahāṁ batāyā gaya hai. Yug śuddha ātmasvabhāv kī nijhaktanā sādhanā — ātmasvabhāv kā sahi prashnā. Jīvātmā kā ātmajnān evaṁ ātmaśraddhā prāyaḥ mānsik, vāchik aur kāyik ācharaṇa ko prabhāvit kartī hai. "Maiṁ viśuddha ātmā hūṁ.... satchidānadasvarūp hūṁ." Pariṇāmsvarūp uske manoratha kalpanāeṁ, spruhāeṁ kāmanāeṁ aur anant abhilāpāeṁ pāṁdgalik bhāvoṁ se parāṇmukha ban jātī haiṁ. Uski vāṇī tatvabharvoṁ kī nindā-praśansā se nivṛtt ho, ātmabhāv kī agama-agochara rahasyavārtāoṁ ko prakaṭ karne kā sarvottam sādhan ban jātī hai. Uska indriya-vyāpār śabda, rūpa, rasa, gandh aur sparśa ke sukha-duḥkha se nivṛtt ho ātmābhivyakti ke puruṣārtha mein līn ho jātā hai.

### Shloka 6 — ॥६॥१०२॥
**Sanskrit:**
यथा शोफस्य पुष्टत्वं यथा वा वध्यमण्डनम् |
तथा ज्ञानभवोन्मादमात्मतृप्तो मुनिर्भवेत् ॥६॥१०२॥

**Translation:**
Just as the puffiness of a swollen body is not true health — and just as the ornamentation of a condemned man is not true beauty — so too should the muni be free from jnāna-bhavonmāda (pride of having attained jnāna-states). The ātmatrupt muni is the true form.

**Vivechan:**
Kamazor sāman me dekhne mein śobhā aur phul-phūlā santusht lagta — but vāstav mein nothing. This is śarīra kā puṣṭa māntā nahi hai, rokaṁ se parīpūṇa māntā hai. Ṭhīk aisī tarah karmānmidya se, puṇyavaśam ne ātmā ke prāti muni kā yah śuddha dṛṣṭi hotī hai ki "yah bhāratamik puṣṭatā nahi hai, balki karm janm bhavbar māphī hai." Jab śarīr ke muni mamata rasne sahane mein 'mūjhan' rūp laghatā hai ṭhīk isi tarah ātmā par mamata prātīt hotā hai, usake liye purā saner ho — aur aisī kāyik puṣṭatā mānaṁ māntā. The contrast story: ancient king (prāchīn mahāparāmśaṁ mein aisī paraṁpara thī ki jismabdhān bar bacā na karna ho), who gave pouṇya-mālāeṁ (garland-flower) decorating victim — dol, turhī dhāre — jashodhep ke sīch ūṁchre śobhāyamāna nibhālatī jātī. Aisī samay vyavasthama ko dhāre manuṣya ko śāmar dhāre bāt-nudar thā śāmar mein — kyā tum jagabāra dhūr tham mein praśant hotā? Nahīṁ, bilkul nahīṁ. Śrāmar jay jagabāra dhāre murhī ghoṣa usake lie mṛtyughoṣa se kama nahīṁ hota. Vah ākulvyākul aur ādhīr hotā hai. The ātmatrupt muni — immersed in ātma-dhyān — is compared to Ramachandra who remained unmoved even when Siteindra created a magnificent world of music and dance. Ātmatrupta muni's one target: nirmal, niṣkalaṅka, paraṁ chetanya svarūp — niranjanāirākār aisā ātmadravya hotā hai.

### Shloka 7 — ॥७॥१०३॥
**Sanskrit:**
सुलभ वाग्मनुच्चार मौनमेकेड्रियेड्वपि |
पुद्गलेषु धर्मप्रवृत्तिषु योगीना मौनमुसमम् ॥७॥१०३॥

**Translation:**
Even a one-sensed being (ekēndriya jīva) can achieve maun as mere non-speaking. But the true maun of yogīs is the maun of manas, vachan, and kāyā turned away from pudgal-pravṛtti.

**Vivechan:**
Maun kī paribhāṣā sirf yahāṁ tak hī seemit dhyānpanī nahi, śabdocchār bhī nahīṁ karnā. Ām "maun" śabda is arth mein pracalit hai. Log samajhte haiṁ ki muṁh se na bolnā matalab maun — aur dharmādhīr se log aisā hī maun karte dikhayi dete haiṁ. Lekin yahāṁ par aise maun kī mahattā nahi batāī gaī hai. Sad sādhāraṇa taur par manuṣya kī bhūmikā ko maun kī upayukta paribhāṣā dī gaī hai. Three levels: (1) Man kā maun — mānasik maun. Dharmā se bhin aise manosamvegopoyak padārthoṁ kā chintan manan nahīṁ karnā. Svapna meiṁ bhī unkā vicār nahīṁ karnā. Is man kā maun bharoṣit mānsik maun baṭṭā hai. Hiṃsā, corī, durachar, parivesha, krodh, māna, māyā, lobhajanya, mohjanya aur lobhjanya bāt jahān par na lānā — mānī vachan kā maun. Vāchik maun kahlātā hai. Pāudgalik bhāva kī nindā aur praśamsā na karnā vāchik maun hai. (3) Kāyā se pudgal-bhāvopayak pravṛtti kā paritiāg karnā — yah kāyā kā maun kahlātā hai. Inī tarah mana, vachan, kāyā ke maun ko hī yathārtha maun kī sañjā dī gaī hai. The positive form: nirtar apne mana mein ātmabhāvopayak vicāroṁ kā sancār karna — kṣamā, namratā, vinaya, vivek, sralatā evaṁ nirlabhmatā ke bhāvoṁ mein sadā-sarvadā khoe rahnā. Ahiṃsā, satya, achauya, brahmacharya, aparigraha ke manorath racānā. ātmā ke vāstavik svarūp kā dhyān dharanā ādi kriyāeṁ mānasik maun hī hai. Vāṇī se ātmabhāvopayak kathā... śāstrābhyās aur śāstra-parīśīlan karnā, paramātma-stuti mein satata lage rahnā... ye kārya vāchik maun ke hī dyotaka hai. Kāyā ke mādhyam se ātmabhāv kī aur prerit aur protsāhit kartī pravṛttiyāṁ karnā — kāyik maun hai.

### Shloka 8 — ॥८॥१०४॥ *(Hero Shloka)*
**Sanskrit:**
ज्योतिर्मयीव दीपस्य क्रिया सर्वोऽपि चिन्मयी |
यत्सं-मन्यस्वभावाऽस्य तस्य मौनमनुत्तरम् ॥८॥१०४॥

**Translation:**
Just as the nature of a lamp is jyotiṣmayī (luminous) — and its every kriyā (action) is light itself — so too: the muni whose svarūp is chinmaya (consciousness-full) has maun that is anuttara (unsurpassed, supreme).

**Vivechan:**
The lamp example: the lamp's nature is light — and every action of the lamp is only light. The lamp does not "decide" to give light; it simply expresses its svarūp. Its light as prākāśanmay becomes prākāśa. Ṭhīk usī tarah yogī/muni durgun se ke yoga pudgal-bhāv (māyā karmavāso) se nivaṭta hote hain, dikhāī padtā hai — lekin actually they are always chinmaya. Phir bhī prakat sthiti mein vah śantāgam muni śrāddha ārādhana par muni kā yah kriyā śodhyamaye hī chatanya-gamaya hotī hai. Their āhāra-kriyā, parodeṣa-kriyā, sabhī jnānamay hotī hai. The distinction — jnānadṛṣṭi (knowledge-vision) vs mohadṛṣṭi (delusion-vision): When jnānadṛṣṭi kā dvār khultā hai — pudgal ke manapassanda rūpa, raṅg, gandh, sparśādi kā vyāpāra ātmā mein rāga-dveṣa aur moha-māyā ko paidā karne mein asamarth ho jāte haiṁ. Jab jnānadṛṣṭi kā dvār band rahtā tha — pudgalbhāv jīvan mein rāga-dveṣa paidā karte the. Lekin jnānadṛṣṭi kā dvār khultā hī vah pudgal-bhāv hote hue bhī rāga-dveṣa aur moha paidā karne mein sarvandhā asamarth bante haiṁ. This is the lamp's light as the sign and signal of jnānadṛṣṭi's dvār opening. The conclusion: jnānadṛṣṭi se yukt aur pudgal-parānmukh svabhāv vālī ātmā kā maun anuttara hotā hai.

---

### Chapter Summary
Maun is not the silence of a closed mouth — it is the silence of three yogas turned toward the ātmā. Eight shlokas build the complete map: jagat-tattva-jnāna = true munihood (1) → ātmā knows ātmā by ātmā — ratnatraya's abheda-parijñāti (2) → chāritra = ātma-anugaman; jnāna's phala is kriyā (3) → atātvikī pravṛtti produces no ātmik phala (4) → that which yields no doṣa-nivṛtti is neither jnāna nor darśan (5) → the ātmatrupt muni free from jnāna-bhavonmāda — like Ramachandra unmoved by Siteindra's music (6) → three dimensions of maun: mānasik, vāchik, kāyik (7) → the jyotiṣmayī lamp: the muni whose svarūp is chinmaya has maun that is anuttara (8).
