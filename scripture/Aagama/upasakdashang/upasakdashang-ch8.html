Upasakdashang · Chapter 8

Lay Follower Mahashatakश्रमणोपासक महाशतक

Detachment amid domestic indulgence — the silent strength of Dharma-Dhyana

Mahashatak was one of the wealthiest householders of Rajgrihi — a bronze merchant with twenty-four crore gold coins and thousands of cattle. Yet his story is not about wealth; it is about the rare inner discipline of maintaining spiritual practice within a household pulled in opposite directions. His wife Revati continued to eat meat and drink alcohol even as he observed strict non-violence. Rather than arguing or judging, Mahashatak responded with absolute silence and deeper meditation. This chapter teaches that the greatest spiritual victory is not found in dramatic heroism, but in the unshakeable equanimity of Dharma-Dhyana — and that even the greatest masters, like Gautam Swami himself, will come to support a sincere practitioner at the end.

"Mahashatak remained absorbed in silent, righteous meditation — undisturbed by the world around him."

About This Chapter

Śramaṇopāsaka Mahashatak

Chapter 8 of the Upasakdashang (उपासकदशांग) is the story of Mahashatak, the wealthiest lay devotee in the text, whose home contained one of the sharpest contrasts in the entire Upasakdashang: a husband in strict spiritual practice and a wife, Revati, who continued consuming meat and all six types of liquor. The chapter introduces the concept of "spiritual asymmetry" — what does one do when those closest to you live by entirely different values?

Jain PrincipleDharma-Dhyāna · Righteous Meditation

The Jain teaching that the highest response to worldly provocation is inner silence — not argument, not condemnation, but the active state of righteous meditation (dharma-dhyāna). Mahashatak demonstrates this: spiritual victory through equanimity rather than dramatic confrontation.

Mahashatak's answer is silence — not passive resignation, but the active inner state of Dharma-Dhyana (righteous meditation). He does not argue, condemn, or abandon his path. When he undertakes the Saṃlehanā (the conscious final fast), Bhagavan Gautam — Mahavira's greatest disciple — personally visits to share the teachings and offer support. Mahashatak then departs in peace, reborn in the luminous Arunabha palace in the Saudharma heaven. His story proves that individual sincerity is its own complete path.

Domestic Tension Sutra 8.14
Revati Contrast Household Reality

तए णं सा रेवई गाहावइणी तोहिं गोणमंसेहि य... सुरं च महुं च मेरगं च मज्जं च... परिभुंजेमाणी विहरइ ॥८.१४॥

Translation

CautionHousehold Life as Spiritual Arena

The Upasakdashang teaches that a household with conflicting values is itself a spiritual crucible. Avoiding condemnation, maintaining one's own practice without forcing it on others, and responding to opposition with equanimity rather than reaction — this is the Jain householder's discipline.

Then Revati, the householder's wife, continued to indulge in various meats, fried foods, and six types of liquors — tasting, savoring, and consuming them.

Commentary: This creates a unique "spiritual asymmetry" in the household. While Mahashatak is practicing strict non-violence and abstinence, his wife Revati remains immersed in meat and alcohol. This represents a real-world challenge: maintaining one's principles when those closest to you live by different ones.

"While Mahashatak followed a strict spiritual path, his wife Revati continued eating meat and drinking alcohol — creating tension in their home."

Righteous Meditation Sutra 8.17
Silence Equanimity Dharma-Dhyana

तए णं से महासयए समणोवासए... धम्मज्झाणोवगए तुसिणीए धम्मज्झाणोवगए विहरइ ॥८.१७॥

Translation

Then Mahashatak the lay devotee, without giving heed to worldly provocations, remained absorbed in silent, righteous meditation (Dharma-Dhyana).

Commentary: Mahashatak's response to his wife's lifestyle and questions is profound silence. Rather than arguing or judging, he remains focused on the internal work of the soul. This silence is not passive; it is an active state of spiritual maturity called Dharma-Dhyana.

"Instead of arguing, Mahashatak simply stayed in deep, peaceful meditation — his silence was his answer."

Protection of the Soul Sutra 8.31
Samlehana Gentle Speech Final Fast

णो खलु कप्पइ, गोयमा ! समणोवासयस्स... अमणुण्णेहिं वागरणेहिं वागरित्ता ॥८.३१॥

Translation

"O Gautam! It is not proper for a devotee undertaking the final fast (Saṃlehanā) to be addressed with unpleasant or discouraging words."

Commentary: Lord Mahavira establishes a rule of spiritual care. One who is consciously departing the body through fasting needs an environment of absolute peace. The spiritual community's duty is to protect the practitioner's state of mind from worldly disruption.

"Mahavira taught that when someone is undertaking their final fast, they should only be spoken to with kindness and respect."

Ancient Support Sutra 8.33
Gautam Swami Support Transmission

तए णं से भगवं गोयमे महासयगं समणोवासयं एवं वयासी— महासयया ! अज्ज तुम्भं अंतिए धम्मं परिकहेमि ॥८.३३॥

Translation

Then Bhagavan Gautam said to Mahashatak: "Mahashatak! Today I will explain the dharma to you (to support your final practice)."

Commentary: Mahavira's chief disciple, Gautam, personally visits Mahashatak to provide spiritual strength during his final fast. This shows the incredible value placed on the successful completion of a life of practice. The greatest masters support the humblest devotees.

"The great master Gautam personally came to share the teachings with Mahashatak and support him at the end."

Celestial Rebirth Sutra 8.38
Saudharma Arunabha Transition

तए णं तस्स महासयगस्स... सौधर्मकल्पमां अरुणाभ वैमानिकमां — देवरूपे उत्पन्न थया ॥८.३८॥

Translation

Then Mahashatak — having purified his karma through faithful practice — was reborn as a celestial being in the Arunabha palace in the Saudharma heaven.

Commentary: Mahashatak's journey ends in the highest celestial realm available to householders. The "Arunabha" palace suggests a rebirth full of spiritual light. His story proves that even in a house of conflicting values, one can attain the highest goals through individual sincerity.

"After a peaceful end, Mahashatak was reborn in the highest heaven — a fruit of his lifetime of steadfast practice."

॥ अध्ययन-८ संपूर्ण ॥
