Avashyak Sutra આવશ્યક સૂત્ર

The Six Daily Essentials. The complete framework that every Jain practitioner — monk, nun, layman, laywoman — performs each morning and each evening: equanimity, praise, reverence, confession, bodily release, and renunciation. The daily work by which the soul is kept moving toward liberation.

Teachings By

Lord Mahāvīra

Compiled By

Arya Sudharmashvami

Translated By

Dishant Shah

Essentials

6 Total

Avashyak 01 arrow_outward

Sāmāyika

The first essential — equanimity. The Namaskar Mahamantra and the Karemi Bhante vow: the foundation of every Jain practice.
Avashyak 02 arrow_outward

Chaturviṃśatistava

The Logassa Sūtra — the seven-verse praise of the twenty-four Tirthankaras, from Rishabh to Mahavir.
Avashyak 03 arrow_outward

Vandanā

The Ichchhami Khamasamano — the disciple's four-part formula of reverence to the living Guru.
Avashyak 04 arrow_outward

Pratikramaṇa

The longest essential — turning back from every transgression of the day. Ten paths of confession ending in universal forgiveness.
Avashyak 05 arrow_outward

Kāyotsarga

Release of the body. The standing or seated meditation that lets the wound opened by Pratikramana finally close.
Avashyak 06 arrow_outward

Pratyakhyāna

The forward-looking renunciation. Ten time-bound vows from Navkarshi to Upvas — the polishing-stroke that completes the day.
