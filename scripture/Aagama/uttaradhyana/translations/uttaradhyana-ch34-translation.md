# Uttaradhyayana Sutra — Chapter 34
# Lesya (Soul Colorations)

**Total Sutras:** 61
**Theme:** This chapter presents the doctrine of lesya — the six soul colorations (krsna, nila, kapota, tejo, padma, and sukla) that reflect the purity or impurity of a soul's mental, vocal, and physical activities. Through vivid analogies of trees, tastes, smells, colors, and touch, the chapter demonstrates how these colorations determine the soul's destiny across the four realms of existence, their duration and intensity of karmic bondage, and ultimately how the progression from dark to luminous lesya marks the soul's journey toward liberation.

---

## SUTRA 34.1

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

लेसज्झयणं पवक्खामि, आणुपुव्वि जहक्कमं ।
छण्हंपि कम्मलेसाणं, अणुभावे सुणेह मे ॥३४.१॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Lesajjhayaṇaṃ pavakkhāmi, āṇupuvvi jahakkamaṃ |
Chaṇhaṃpi kammalesāṇaṃ, aṇubhāve suṇeha me || 34.1 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

I shall describe the chapter on lesya in sequence, in order; listen to me about the effects of the six karma-lesyas.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **lesajjhayaṇaṃ** — the chapter on lesya (soul colorations)
- **pavakkhāmi** — I shall describe
- **āṇupuvvi** — in sequence / in order (anupurvi)
- **jahakkamaṃ** — as per their order
- **chaṇhaṃpi** — of the six also
- **kammalesāṇaṃ** — of the karma-lesyas
- **aṇubhāve** — the effects / influence
- **suṇeha me** — listen to me

### 5. COMMENTARY

This opening sutra introduces the subject of the entire chapter — the six lesyas (soul colorations). The speaker announces the intention to describe them systematically in their proper sequence. The word *kammalesanam* (karma-lesyas) indicates that these colorations are intimately connected with karmic matter. The six lesyas — krsna (black), nila (blue), kapota (grey), tejo (fiery/red), padma (lotus-pink), and sukla (white) — represent varying degrees of the soul's purity and impurity.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: The chapter begins by announcing it will explain the six soul colorations that reflect how pure or impure a soul's activities are.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Am I aware of the coloring my own thoughts, words, and actions give to my soul at this very moment?

णामाइं वण्ण-रस-गंध, फास-परिणामलक्खणं ।
ठाणं ठिइं गइं चाउं, लेसाणं तु सुणेह मे ॥३४.२॥

- **gaṃdha** — smell
- **phāsa** — touch

This sutra outlines the eleven aspects through which the lesyas will be described: (1) name, (2) color, (3) taste, (4) smell, (5) touch, (6) characteristic transformations (parinam), (7) station/realm, (8) duration, (9) destiny, (10) movement/gati, and (11) lifespan. This systematic framework ensures a comprehensive understanding of each lesya from every possible angle.

## SUTRA 34.3

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

किण्हा णीला य काऊ य, तेऊ पम्हा तहेव य ।
सुक्कलेसा य छट्ठा य, णामाइं तु जहक्कमं ॥३४.३॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Kiṇhā ṇīlā ya kāū ya, teū pamhā taheva ya |
Sukkalesā ya chaṭṭhā ya, ṇāmāiṃ tu jahakkamaṃ || 34.3 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Black, blue, grey, fiery, lotus-pink, and white — the sixth — these are the names of the lesyas in their order.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **kiṇhā** — black (krsna lesya)
- **ṇīlā** — blue (nila lesya)
- **kāū** — grey/dove-colored (kapota lesya)
- **teū** — fiery/red (tejo lesya)
- **pamhā** — lotus-pink (padma lesya)
- **sukkalesā** — white lesya (sukla lesya)
- **chaṭṭhā** — the sixth
- **ṇāmāiṃ** — names
- **jahakkamaṃ** — in order

### 5. COMMENTARY

This sutra names the six lesyas in their traditional sequence from the most impure to the most pure. The first three — krsna (black), nila (blue), and kapota (grey) — are the inauspicious (ashubha) lesyas associated with sinful and harmful activities. The latter three — tejo (fiery), padma (lotus-pink), and sukla (white) — are the auspicious (shubha) lesyas associated with virtuous conduct. This ordering reflects the progressive purification of the soul.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: The six soul colorations are named in order from darkest to lightest — black, blue, grey, fiery-red, lotus-pink, and white.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

If my soul's coloration were visible right now, would it be closer to the dark end or the luminous end of this spectrum?

## SUTRA 34.4

Resembling a dark rain-cloud, similar to a buffalo's horn, like collyrium (kajal) in appearance — such is the color of the black lesya.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **ṇiddhasaṃkāsā** — resembling in appearance
- **gavalarḍuga** — buffalo's horn
- **saṇṇibhā** — similar to
- **khaṃjaṇa** — collyrium / kajal / antimony

This sutra describes the color of the black lesya through three vivid analogies: it is dark like a rain-cloud, like a buffalo's horn, and like collyrium (kajal) applied to the eyes. एयाहि तिहि वि जीवो, सुग्गइं उववज्जइ ॥३४.५७॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Teū pamhā sukkā, tiṇṇi vi eyāo dhammalesāo |
Eyāhi tihi vi jīvo, suggaiṃ uvavajjai || 34.57 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Fiery, lotus-pink, and white — these three are the righteous lesyas; by these three, the soul takes birth in a good destiny.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **teū** — fiery (tejo)
- **pamhā** — lotus-pink (padma)
- **sukkā** — white (sukla)
- **tiṇṇi** — three
- **eyāo** — these
- **dhammalesāo** — righteous lesyas (dharma-lesya)
- **jīvo** — soul
- **suggaiṃ** — good destiny (sugati)
- **uvavajjai** — is born into

### 5. COMMENTARY

In direct contrast to the previous sutra, this one categorizes the three bright lesyas — tejo, padma, and sukla — as "dharma lesyas" (righteous colorations). Souls with these colorations are born into favorable destinies — human and celestial realms. This pairing of sutras 56 and 57 provides the essential moral teaching of the chapter: inner purity leads to good births, inner impurity to suffering.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: The three bright soul colorations — fiery, lotus-pink, and white — lead the soul to birth in higher, more favorable existences.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Am I actively cultivating the bright soul colorations through my daily choices, knowing they directly shape my future existence?

## SUTRA 34.58

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

लेस्साहि सव्वाहिं, पढमे समयम्मि परिणयाहिं तु ।
ण हु कस्सइ उववाओ, परे भवे होइ जीवस्स ॥३४.५८॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Lessāhi savvāhiṃ, paḍhame samayammi pariṇayāhiṃ tu |
Ṇa hu kassai uvavāo, pare bhave hoi jīvassa || 34.58 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

With all the lesyas in their first moment of transformation — for none is there birth in any other existence without these (lesyas of the soul).

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **lessāhi** — with lesyas
- **savvāhiṃ** — all
- **paḍhame** — first
- **samayammi** — in the moment (samaya)
- **pariṇayāhiṃ** — transformed / characterized by
- **ṇa hu** — not indeed
- **kassai** — for anyone
- **uvavāo** — birth
- **pare bhave** — in the next existence
- **jīvassa** — of the soul

### 5. COMMENTARY

This sutra makes a profound statement: every birth in the cycle of existence involves a lesya. No soul is born without a lesya coloring it at the very first moment of its new existence. The lesya present at the first moment (prathama samaya) of a new birth determines the fundamental character of that existence. This underscores that lesya is an inseparable aspect of the embodied soul's experience.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: Every single birth involves a soul coloration — no being is ever born without one of the six lesyas marking its soul.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

If my lesya at the moment of death determines my next birth, how crucial is it to maintain purity in my final moments?

## SUTRA 34.59

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

लेस्साहि सव्वाहिं, चरिमे समयम्मि परिणयाहिं तु ।
ण हु कस्सइ उववाओ, परे भवे होइ जीवस्स ॥३४.५९॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Lessāhi savvāhiṃ, carime samayammi pariṇayāhiṃ tu |
Ṇa hu kassai uvavāo, pare bhave hoi jīvassa || 34.59 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

With all the lesyas in their final moment of transformation — for none is there birth in any other existence without these (lesyas of the soul).

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **lessāhi** — with lesyas
- **savvāhiṃ** — all
- **carime** — final / last
- **samayammi** — in the moment
- **pariṇayāhiṃ** — transformed / characterized by
- **ṇa hu** — not indeed
- **kassai** — for anyone
- **uvavāo** — birth
- **pare bhave** — in the next existence
- **jīvassa** — of the soul

### 5. COMMENTARY

Complementing the previous sutra, this one states that the lesya at the last moment (carama samaya) of an existence also determines the next birth. The soul's coloration at the moment of death is what it carries into its next existence. This teaching has profound practical implications: the spiritual state at the time of death is of utmost importance, as it directly determines the nature of the next birth.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: The soul coloration at the final moment of life determines the soul's next birth — making the spiritual state at death critically important.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Am I living in a way that, if this moment were my last, my soul's coloration would lead to a favorable next existence?

## SUTRA 34.60

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

अंतोमुहुत्तम्मि गए, अंतोमुहुत्तम्मि सेसए चेव ।
लेस्साहिं परिणयाहिं तु, जीवा गच्छंति परलोयं ॥३४.६०॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Aṃtomuhuttammi gae, aṃtomuhuttammi sesae ceva |
Lessāhiṃ pariṇayāhiṃ tu, jīvā gacchaṃti paraloyaṃ || 34.60 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Having passed within an antarmuhurta, and with an antarmuhurta remaining — souls, characterized by their lesyas, proceed to the next world.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **aṃtomuhuttammi** — within an antarmuhurta (less than 48 minutes)
- **gae** — having passed / elapsed
- **sesae** — remaining
- **ceva** — indeed
- **lessāhiṃ** — with lesyas
- **pariṇayāhiṃ** — characterized by / transformed
- **jīvā** — souls
- **gacchaṃti** — go / proceed
- **paraloyaṃ** — to the next world (paraloka)

### 5. COMMENTARY

This sutra describes the actual process of transition between lives. Within the span of an antarmuhurta (less than 48 minutes), the soul departs one existence and enters another. Throughout this transition, the soul carries its lesya coloration, which accompanies it into the next world. The lesya is thus the soul's constant companion through the cycle of birth and death — it is the coloring that determines every new existence.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: When a soul transitions from one life to the next — which happens within about 48 minutes — it carries its soul coloration with it, determining its new birth.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

In the brief window between death and rebirth, my soul's coloration is all I carry — what am I doing right now to brighten it?

## SUTRA 34.61

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

तम्हा एयासिं लेसाणं, आणुभावे वियाणिया ।
अप्पसत्थाओ वज्जित्ता, पसत्थाओ अहिट्ठए मुणी ॥ ति बेमि ॥३४.६१॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Tamhā eyāsiṃ lesāṇaṃ, āṇubhāve viyāṇiyā |
Appasatthāo vajjittā, pasatthāo ahiṭṭhae muṇī || ti bemi || 34.61 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Therefore, having understood the effects of these lesyas, a wise person (muni) abandons the inauspicious ones and firmly establishes the auspicious ones — thus do I declare.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **tamhā** — therefore
- **eyāsiṃ** — of these
- **lesāṇaṃ** — lesyas
- **āṇubhāve** — the effects / influence
- **viyāṇiyā** — having understood / comprehended
- **appasatthāo** — the inauspicious ones (apashasta)
- **vajjittā** — having abandoned
- **pasatthāo** — the auspicious ones (prashasta)
- **ahiṭṭhae** — firmly establishes (adhishthita)
- **muṇī** — wise person / monk (muni)
- **ti bemi** — thus do I declare

### 5. COMMENTARY

This concluding sutra summarizes the entire chapter's teaching and provides its practical application. Having systematically described all six lesyas — their names, colors, tastes, smells, touches, transformations, stations, durations, and destinies — the speaker now draws the moral conclusion: a wise person, understanding these effects, abandons the three dark lesyas through purification of conduct and firmly establishes the three bright lesyas through righteous living, self-restraint, and spiritual practice. The phrase *ti bemi* (thus do I declare) is the traditional Jain marker for the end of a discourse, lending authority to the teaching.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: The chapter concludes by saying: understand the effects of these soul colorations, abandon the dark ones, and cultivate the bright ones — this is the path of wisdom.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Now that I understand how my actions color my soul and determine my destiny, what will I actually do differently starting today?
