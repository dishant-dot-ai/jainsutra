# Uttaradhyayana Sutra — Chapter 11
# Bahuśruta Pūjā (Honour of the Many-Learned)

**Chapter 11 of the Uttaradhyayana Sutra**
*Gujarati Source: Volume 1, printed pages 197–210 (PDF pages 267–280)*
*Total Sutras: 30*

---

## SUTRA 11.1

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

संजोगा विप्पमुक्कस्स, अणागारस्स भिक्खुणो ।
आयारं पाउक्करिस्सामि, आणुपुव्विं सुणेह मे ॥११.१॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

saṃjogā vippamukkassa, aṇāgārassa bhikkhuṇo |
āyāraṃ pāukkarissāmi, āṇupuvviṃ suṇeha me || 11.1 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

For the monk who is freed from worldly attachments and is homeless — I shall describe the proper conduct (ācāra) in sequence. Listen to me.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **saṃjogā** = from worldly attachments, connections (saṃyoga)
- **vippamukkassa** = freed, released (vipramukta)
- **aṇāgārassa** = homeless, without a house (anagāra)
- **bhikkhuṇo** = of the monk, the mendicant (bhikṣu)
- **āyāraṃ** = the conduct, the ācāra (proper monastic behaviour)
- **pāukkarissāmi** = I shall describe, I shall set forth (prakariṣyāmi)
- **āṇupuvviṃ** = in sequence, step by step (anupūrvī)
- **suṇeha me** = listen to me

### 5. COMMENTARY

The chapter opens by establishing its subject: the proper conduct (ācāra) of a monk who has renounced all worldly bonds. The word **bahuśruta** (many-learned) is the name of the ideal teacher — one who has heard, studied, and mastered the canonical teachings. This chapter will describe how such a teacher should be honoured, what qualities make a true bahuśruta, and through a magnificent series of similes (sutras 14–30), will compare the bahuśruta to the greatest things in the universe.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

I shall describe the proper conduct for the homeless monk freed from worldly attachments — listen to me in sequence.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

What does it mean to be truly "freed from worldly attachments"? Is it a physical condition or an inner one?

## SUTRA 11.2

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

जे यावि होइ णिव्विज्जे, थद्धे लुद्धे अणिग्गहे ।
अभिक्खणं उल्लवइ, सुयं लद्धुं ण मज्जइ ॥११.२॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

je yāvi hoi ṇivvijje, thaddhe luddhe aṇiggahe |
abhikkhaṇaṃ ullavai, suyaṃ laddhuṃ ṇa majjāi || 11.2 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Even one who is uneducated, stubborn, greedy, and without self-mastery — if that one hears the teaching constantly, having obtained śruta (learning), such a one does not remain intoxicated (by ignorance).

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **je yāvi** = even one who
- **ṇivvijje** = without learning, uneducated (nir-vidya)
- **thaddhe** = stubborn, rigid
- **luddhe** = greedy
- **aṇiggahe** = without self-mastery, unrestrained
- **abhikkhaṇaṃ** = constantly, frequently
- **ullavai** = hears, listens, approaches (the teaching)
- **suyaṃ** = śruta — the heard teaching, canonical learning
- **laddhuṃ** = having obtained
- **ṇa majjāi** = does not remain intoxicated, is not deluded

### 5. COMMENTARY

A powerful democratic statement: even the most unpromising student — stubborn, greedy, unrestrained — can be transformed by constant exposure to the teaching. The word **abhikkhaṇaṃ** (constantly) is the key: it is not one-time hearing but sustained, repeated exposure that transforms. The word **majjāi** (intoxicated) describes the default state of the unlearned soul: drunk on delusion, sense-pleasure, and pride. Learning is the antidote to this intoxication.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Even the most stubborn, greedy, and unrestrained person — if they hear the teaching constantly — will be freed from the intoxication of ignorance.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

No one is beyond the reach of the teaching. But it requires constant exposure, not a single dose. Am I returning to the teaching daily?

## SUTRAS 11.3–5: OBSTACLES TO LEARNING

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

अह पंचर्हि ठाणेर्हि, जेर्हि सिक्खा ण लब्भइ ।
थंभा कोहा पमाएणं, रोगेणा ऽऽलस्सएण य ॥११.३॥

एगया खत्तिओ होइ, तओ साइ बुक्कसो ।
तओ कीडपयंगो य, सिक्खासीले ति वुच्चइ ॥११.४॥

अकोहणे सच्चवरए, ण सिया अइलोलुए ।
अकोहणे विसीले, ण सिया अइलोलुए ॥११.५॥

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

(3) There are five obstacles by which learning (śikṣā) cannot be obtained: pride (stambha), anger (krodha), negligence (pramāda), disease (roga), and laziness (ālasya).
(4–5) These five qualities prevent a student from obtaining learning. The wise teacher recognises these obstacles and works to remove them in the student.

### 5. COMMENTARY

The five obstacles to learning (**śikṣā-bādhaka kāraṇo**):
1. **Stambha** (pride/stubbornness) — the inability to bow before knowledge
2. **Krodha** (anger) — emotional reactivity that blocks absorption
3. **Pramāda** (negligence/carelessness) — not treating learning as urgent
4. **Roga** (disease) — physical illness that prevents study
5. **Ālasya** (laziness) — the unwillingness to exert effort

These five are present in every learner to some degree. The text identifies them not to condemn the student but to provide a diagnostic: which of these five is blocking your learning right now?

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Five things block learning: pride, anger, negligence, disease, and laziness. Recognise which one is blocking you.

## SUTRAS 11.6–9: QUALITIES OF THE AVINĪTA (UNDISCIPLINED STUDENT)

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

अह चोद्दसर्हि ठाणेर्हि, वट्ठुमाणे उ संजए ।
अविणीए वुच्चइ सो उ, णिव्वाणं च ण गच्छइ ॥११.६॥

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

One who stands in fourteen faults is called avinīta (undisciplined) — and such a one does not attain nirvāṇa.

### 5. COMMENTARY

The Vivecana enumerates 14 marks of the undisciplined student (avinīta lakṣaṇa). These include: (1) being angry frequently, (2) holding grudges, (3) being jealous of fellow students, (4) speaking harshly to the teacher, (5) not following instructions, (6) not engaging in study time, (7) keeping company with worldly people, (8) being lazy in bodily duties, and more. The emphasis is clear: discipline is not optional for spiritual progress. Without vinaya (humility/discipline toward the teacher), nirvāṇa is unreachable.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

One who has fourteen faults of indiscipline is called undisciplined (avinīta) — and cannot reach nirvāṇa.

## SUTRAS 11.10–13: QUALITIES OF THE SUVINĪTA (WELL-DISCIPLINED STUDENT)

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

अह पण्णरसर्हि ठाणेर्हि, सुविणीए ति वुच्चइ ।
अप्पं च अहिक्खिवइ, पबंधं च ण कुव्वइ ॥११.१०–११॥

ण य पावपरिक्खेवी, ण य मित्तेसु कुप्पइ ।
कलह डमर अभिजाइए, बुद्धे अभिजाइए ॥११.१२–१३॥

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

(10–11) One who possesses fifteen qualities is called suvinīta (well-disciplined). Such a one does not belittle small teachings and does not create discord.
(12–13) Such a one does not surround themselves with sinful company, does not get angry with friends, does not engage in quarrels or disputes — and is recognised as wise.

### 5. COMMENTARY

The 15 marks of the well-disciplined student include: (1) not belittling small points, (2) not creating divisions, (3) not keeping bad company, (4) not getting angry at friends, (5) avoiding quarrels, (6) being recognised by the wise, (7) showing proper respect, (8) doing assigned tasks, (9) being truthful in confession, (10) not criticising other traditions, (11) maintaining bodily discipline, (12) practising contentment, (13) being patient in hardship, (14) serving the teacher, (15) showing gratitude. The suvinīta is the ideal student — humble, diligent, truthful, and grateful.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

The well-disciplined student has fifteen qualities: humility, diligence, truthfulness, patience, non-contentiousness, and gratitude toward the teacher.

## SUTRA 11.14: THE BAHUŚRUTA LIKE A JEWEL IN A SETTING

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

वसे गुरुकुले णिच्चं, जोगवं उवहाणवं ।
पियकरे पियंवाई, से सिक्खं लद्धुमरिहइ ॥११.१४॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

vase gurukule ṇiccaṃ, jogavaṃ uvahāṇavaṃ |
piyakare piyaṃvāī, se sikkhaṃ laddhum arihhai || 11.14 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

One who dwells constantly in the guru's home, alert and attentive, doing what is pleasing and speaking what is pleasant — such a one deserves to obtain the teaching.

### 5. COMMENTARY

From sutra 14 onward, the chapter shifts to a magnificent series of similes comparing the bahuśruta (the learned teacher) to the greatest things in the universe. Each sutra uses the formula "jaha se..." (just as that...) to draw an analogy. The student who lives in the guru's service, attentive and devoted, earns the right to receive the teaching. This is the classical gurukula relationship — not a transaction but a devotional apprenticeship.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

One who lives in the guru's home with alertness, attentiveness, pleasant action, and pleasant speech — such a one deserves to receive the teaching.

## SUTRAS 11.15–30: THE GREAT SIMILES OF THE BAHUŚRUTA

*The following sutras form one of the most beautiful poetic sequences in the Uttaradhyayana. Each compares the bahuśruta (learned teacher) to something great in the universe.*

### SUTRA 11.15 — LIKE MILK IN A CONCH SHELL

जहा संखम्मि पयं णिहियं, दुहओ वि विरायइ ।
एवं बहुस्सुए भिक्खू, धम्मो आवकहाए, गुरुकुलवासिए ति वुच्चइ ॥११.१५॥

"Just as milk placed in a conch shell shines beautifully from both sides — so the bahuśruta monk, through dharma and teacher-service, radiates on all sides."

### SUTRA 11.16 — LIKE A KING'S MINISTER

जहा से कम्बोयाणं, आइण्णे कंथए सिया ।
जवणे जवेण पवरे, एवं हवइ बहुस्सुए ॥११.१६॥

"Just as a trusted minister of the king of Kamboja (Cambodia) — reliable, wise, commanding — so is the bahuśruta among monks."

### SUTRA 11.17 — LIKE A FIERCE WARRIOR

जहा ऊ ऽइण्णसमारुढे, सूरे दढपरक्कमे ।
"Just as a mighty warrior firmly mounted on his steed, resolute in battle."

### SUTRA 11.18 — LIKE A SIXTY-YEAR-OLD BULL ELEPHANT

जहा करेणु परिकिण्णे, कुंजरे सट्ठिहायणे ।
बलवंते अप्पडिहए, एवं हवइ बहुस्सुए ॥११.१८॥

"Just as a sixty-year-old bull elephant, surrounded by females, mighty and unstoppable — so is the bahuśruta."

### SUTRA 11.19 — LIKE A SHARP-HORNED BULL

जहा से तिक्खसिंगे, जायखंधे विरायइ ।
"Just as a sharp-horned bull with a strong neck shines among the herd."

### SUTRA 11.20 — LIKE A SHARP-TUSKED LION

जहा से तिक्खदाढे, उदग्गे दुप्पहंसए ।
सीहे मियाणं पवरे, एवं हवइ बहुस्सुए ॥११.२०॥

"Just as the sharp-tusked, towering, unconquerable lion — the best among animals — so is the bahuśruta."

### SUTRA 11.21 — LIKE VĀSUDEVA

जहा से वासुदेवे, संख-चक्क-गदाधरे ।
अप्पडिहय-बले जोहे, एवं हवइ बहुस्सुए ॥११.२१॥

"Just as Vāsudeva — bearing conch, discus, and mace, of incomparable strength — so is the bahuśruta."

### SUTRA 11.22 — LIKE A CAKRAVARTĪ (UNIVERSAL EMPEROR)

जहा से चउरंते, चक्कवट्टी महिट्ठिए ।
चोद्दस रयणाहिवई, एवं हवइ बहुस्सुए ॥११.२२॥

"Just as a Cakravartī — lord of the four directions, possessor of the fourteen jewels, supremely powerful — so is the bahuśruta."

### SUTRA 11.23 — LIKE INDRA

जहा से सहस्सक्खे, वज्जपाणी पुरंदरे ।
सक्के देवाणमिंदे, एवं हवइ बहुस्सुए ॥११.२३॥

"Just as Indra — the thousand-eyed, thunderbolt-wielding, lord of gods — so is the bahuśruta."

### SUTRA 11.24 — LIKE THE SUN

जहा से तिमिर-विद्धंसे, उत्तिट्ठते दिवायरे ।
जलंते इव तेएण, एवं हवइ बहुस्सुए ॥११.२४॥

"Just as the rising sun — destroyer of darkness, blazing with radiance — so is the bahuśruta."

### SUTRA 11.25 — LIKE THE MOON

जहा से उडुवई चंदे, णक्खत्त-परिवारिए ।
पडिपुण्णे पुण्णमासीए, एवं हवइ बहुस्सुए ॥११.२५॥

"Just as the full moon — lord of stars, surrounded by constellations, complete on the full-moon night — so is the bahuśruta."

### SUTRA 11.26 — LIKE A WELL-GUARDED FORTRESS

जहा से सामाइयाणं, कोट्टागारे सुरक्खिए ।
णाणा-धण्ण-पडिपुण्णे, एवं हवइ बहुस्सुए ॥११.२६॥

"Just as a well-guarded treasury-fortress of kings — filled with all kinds of wealth, perfectly protected — so is the bahuśruta."

### SUTRA 11.27 — LIKE THE JAMBŪ TREE

जहा सा दुमाण पवरा, जम्बू णाम सुदंसणा ।
अणाठियस्स देवस्स, एवं हवइ बहुस्सुए ॥११.२७॥

"Just as the great Jambū tree — the most excellent among trees, beautiful to behold, worthy of the gods — so is the bahuśruta."

### SUTRA 11.28 — LIKE THE GREAT RIVER FLOWING TO THE OCEAN

जहा सा णईण पवरा, सलिला सागरंगमा ।
सीया णीलवंत पवहा, एवं हवइ बहुस्सुए ॥११.२८॥

"Just as the greatest of rivers — flowing steadily to the ocean — so is the bahuśruta."

### SUTRA 11.29 — LIKE MOUNT MERU

जहा से णाण पवरे, सुमहं मंदरे गिरी ।
णाणोसहि-पज्जलिए, एवं हवइ बहुस्सुए ॥११.२९॥

"Just as Mount Meru — supreme among mountains, blazing with many herbs and radiance — so is the bahuśruta."

### SUTRA 11.30 — LIKE THE OCEAN ITSELF

जहा से सयंभूरमणे, उदही अक्खओदए ।
णाणा रयण पडिपुण्णे, एवं हवइ बहुस्सुए ॥११.३०॥ – ति बेमि

"Just as the self-born ocean — inexhaustible, filled with innumerable jewels — so is the bahuśruta. Thus I say."

### COMMENTARY ON THE SIMILE SERIES (11.15–30)

Sixteen similes in ascending grandeur: milk in a conch, a king's minister, a warrior, a bull elephant, a sharp-horned bull, a lion, Vāsudeva, a Cakravartī, Indra, the sun, the full moon, a guarded fortress, the Jambū tree, the great river, Mount Meru, and finally the ocean itself. Each simile captures a different quality of the bahuśruta:

| Simile | Quality of the Bahuśruta |
|---|---|
| Milk in conch | Radiance and purity |
| King's minister | Trusted wisdom |
| Warrior | Fearless discipline |
| Bull elephant | Unstoppable strength |
| Sharp-horned bull | Commanding presence |
| Lion | Supremacy among beings |
| Vāsudeva | Equipped with all weapons of knowledge |
| Cakravartī | Universal sovereignty over learning |
| Indra | Lordship over all other teachers |
| Sun | Destroyer of the darkness of ignorance |
| Full moon | Complete, surrounded by students like stars |
| Fortress | Repository of all spiritual wealth |
| Jambū tree | Shade-giver, protector |
| Great river | Steady flow toward the ocean of liberation |
| Mount Meru | Central, supreme, radiant |
| Ocean | Inexhaustible, filled with jewels of knowledge |

The sequence ends with the ocean — the final, all-encompassing metaphor. The bahuśruta is not just great in one dimension but in every dimension: strength, beauty, radiance, depth, stability, generosity, and inexhaustibility.

### CONTEMPLATE

Which of these sixteen qualities of the learned teacher do I value most — and which am I cultivating in myself?
