**Chapter 3 of the Uttaradhyayana Sutra**
*Gujarati Source: Volume 1, printed pages 63–81 (PDF pages 133–151)*
*Total Sutras: 20*

## SUTRA 3.1

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

चत्तारि परमंगाणि, दुल्लहाणीह जंतुणो ।
माणुसत्तं सुई सद्धा, संजमम्मि य वीरियं ॥३.१॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

cattāri paramaṃgāṇi, dullahāṇīha jaṃtuṇo |
māṇusattaṃ suī saddhā, saṃjamammi ya vīriyaṃ || 3.1 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Four supreme factors are extremely rare for a living being: human birth, the hearing of dharma (śruti), faith (śraddhā), and energy in restraint (vīrya in saṃyama).

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **cattāri** = four
- **paramaṃgāṇi** = supreme/highest factors, the most excellent elements
- **dullahāṇi** = difficult to obtain, extremely rare
- **iha** = here, in this world
- **jaṃtuṇo** = for a living being, for a creature
- **māṇusattaṃ** = human birth, the state of being human (manuṣyatva)
- **suī** = hearing of dharma (śruti — the sacred teaching heard from a teacher)
- **saddhā** = faith (śraddhā — conviction in the truth of the teaching)
- **saṃjamammi** = in restraint, in the practice of saṃyama
- **vīriyaṃ** = energy, vigour, effort (vīrya)

### 5. COMMENTARY

This opening verse is the thesis of the entire chapter. The four rarities (caturaṅga) are: (1) **manuṣyatva** — human birth, which alone provides the opportunity for liberation; (2) **śruti** — actually hearing the dharma, which requires both a teacher and a receptive listener; (3) **śraddhā** — faith in what has been heard, without which hearing is mere noise; (4) **vīrya in saṃyama** — the energy to actually practise restraint, without which faith remains merely theoretical. Each is rare on its own; all four together in one life is extraordinarily rare. The chapter will demonstrate why each is precious and how a wise being uses them.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Four things are supremely rare for any living being: human birth, hearing the true teaching, faith in it, and the energy to practise restraint.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Do I have all four right now — human birth, access to the teaching, faith, and energy for practice? If so, how am I using this rare combination?

## SUTRA 3.2

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

समावण्णं संसारे, णाणा-गोत्तासु जाइसु ।
कम्मा-विहा कट्टु, पुत्तो विस्संभिया पया ॥३.२॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

samāvaṇṇaṃ saṃsāre, ṇāṇā-gottāsu jāisu |
kammā-vihā kaṭṭu, putto vissaṃbhiyā payā || 3.2 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Wandering in saṃsāra, born in various lineages and births — performing various kinds of karma — the soul, like a lost child, has been wandering on the path.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **samāvaṇṇaṃ** = having entered, wandering in
- **saṃsāre** = in the cycle of worldly existence
- **ṇāṇā-gottāsu** = in various lineages/families (gotra)
- **jāisu** = in births (jāti — different types of birth)
- **kammā-vihā** = various types of karma
- **kaṭṭu** = having done, having performed
- **putto** = like a child, like a son (innocent, unaware)
- **vissaṃbhiyā** = lost, trusting blindly (viśvasta — unsuspecting)
- **payā** = on the path, wandering along

### 5. COMMENTARY

The soul has wandered through infinite births — in every kind of lineage, every species, every realm. The word **putto** (child) evokes innocence and vulnerability: like a child who has wandered away from home and doesn't know the way back, the soul has been lost in saṃsāra, performing all kinds of karma without understanding the consequences. The word **vissaṃbhiyā** adds another dimension: the soul trusts blindly — it trusts that sense-pleasures will bring happiness, that this birth will be different, that more accumulation will bring security. This blind trust keeps the wandering going.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

The soul has wandered through saṃsāra, born in countless lineages and births, performing all kinds of karma — like a lost child trusting blindly on an endless path.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

How long have I been wandering? And is this the birth where I finally find my way home?

## SUTRA 3.3

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

एगया देवलोएसु, णरएसु वि एगया ।
एगया आसुरे काये, अहाकम्मेहिं गच्छइ ॥३.३॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

egayā devaloesu, ṇaraesu vi egayā |
egayā āsure kāye, ahākammehiṃ gacchai || 3.3 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Sometimes born in the celestial realms, sometimes in the hells, sometimes in demonic bodies — the soul goes wherever its karma takes it.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **egayā** = sometimes, at one time
- **devaloesu** = in the celestial worlds (deva-loka)
- **ṇaraesu** = in the hells (naraka)
- **vi** = also
- **āsure kāye** = in demonic/āsura bodies
- **ahākammehiṃ** = according to its karma (yathā-karma)
- **gacchai** = goes, is born, travels

### 5. COMMENTARY

The soul is not fixed in any one realm. Depending on its karma, it may be born as a deva (celestial being) in one life, fall to naraka (hell) in the next, and take an āsura (demonic) body after that. The word **ahākammehiṃ** — "according to its karma" — is the governing principle. There is no external being assigning these births; the soul's own accumulated karma determines where it goes. The range is total: from the highest heaven to the lowest hell. This is the Jain understanding of the soul's journey — radically self-determined, yet often blindly so.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Sometimes born as a god, sometimes in hell, sometimes in a demonic body — the soul goes wherever its karma sends it.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

The same soul that was once a god has also been in hell. What does that say about identity, status, and pride?

## SUTRA 3.4

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

एगया खत्तिओ होइ, तओ चंडाल बुक्कसो ।
तओ कीडपयंगो य, तओ कुंथू पिवीलिया ॥३.४॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

egayā khattiho hoi, tao caṃḍāla bukkaso |
tao kīḍapayaṃgo ya, tao kuṃthū pivīliyā || 3.4 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Sometimes a warrior-king (kṣatriya), then an outcaste (caṇḍāla), then an insect or moth, then a louse or ant — the soul takes all forms.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **egayā** = sometimes
- **khattiho** = a kṣatriya (warrior, king)
- **hoi** = becomes
- **tao** = then, after that
- **caṃḍāla** = a caṇḍāla (outcaste, untouchable in the old social order)
- **bukkaso** = a tribal person of low social standing
- **kīḍapayaṃgo** = an insect, a moth (kīṭa-pataṅga)
- **kuṃthū** = a louse, a tiny parasite
- **pivīliyā** = an ant (pipīlikā)

### 5. COMMENTARY

The extremity of the soul's journey: from the highest social status (kṣatriya — warrior-king) to the lowest (caṇḍāla, bukkasa) to tiny creatures (insects, lice, ants). The point is not merely that status changes — it is that the soul has been every one of these, including the most microscopic and seemingly insignificant beings. If the king knew he was once a louse, and the louse knew it was once a king — what would either feel about the other? This is the foundation of Jain universal compassion: every being you see has been what you are, and you have been what every being is.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Sometimes a king, then an outcaste, then a moth, then a louse — the soul passes through every form of life.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

If I have been every creature — the king and the ant, the god and the worm — who am I really? And how should I treat the beings around me?

## SUTRA 3.5

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

एवमवट्ट जोणीसु, पाणिणो विणिहम्मंति पाणिणो ।

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

evamavaṭṭa joṇīsu, pāṇiṇo viṇihammṃti pāṇiṇo |

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Thus do living beings revolve through various births — beings tossed about among beings.

### 5. COMMENTARY

A brief, devastating summary: the cycle is circular (**avaṭṭa** — revolving, turning). Beings (**pāṇiṇo**) are thrown around (**viṇihammṃti**) among other beings. There is no escape from within the cycle itself — only the four rarities (human birth, hearing dharma, faith, energy) can break the revolution.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

In this way, living beings keep revolving through countless births — tossed endlessly among other beings.

## SUTRA 3.6

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

कम्म-संगेहिं सम्मूढा, दुक्खिया बहुवेयणा ।
अमाणुसासु जोणीसु, विणिहम्मंति पाणिणो ॥३.६॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

kamma-saṃgehiṃ sammūḍhā, dukkhiyā bahuveyaṇā |
amāṇusāsu joṇīsu, viṇihammṃti pāṇiṇo || 3.6 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Deluded by the bonds of karma, suffering, experiencing much pain — living beings are cast into non-human births.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **kamma-saṃgehiṃ** = by the bonds/attachments of karma
- **sammūḍhā** = deluded, confused, bewildered
- **dukkhiyā** = suffering, afflicted
- **bahuveyaṇā** = experiencing many types of pain (vedanā)
- **amāṇusāsu** = in non-human (births)
- **joṇīsu** = in wombs/births (yoni)
- **viṇihammṃti** = are cast, are thrown
- **pāṇiṇo** = living beings

### 5. COMMENTARY

The reason for the endless cycling: **kamma-saṃgehiṃ sammūḍhā** — deluded by karmic bonds. Delusion (moha) is the root cause; karma is the mechanism; suffering is the result. The word **amāṇusāsu** — non-human births — covers the vast majority of all births: hell-beings, animals, plants, earth-bodies, water-bodies, fire-bodies, air-bodies. Human birth is the rare exception. Most of the soul's time in saṃsāra is spent in non-human forms where the opportunity for liberation does not exist.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Deluded by karma, suffering greatly, living beings are cast again and again into non-human births.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Most of my existence has been in non-human forms where I couldn't even understand this teaching. Now I can. What will I do with that?

## SUTRA 3.7

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

कम्माणं तु पहाणाए, आणुपुव्वी कयाइ उ ।
जीवा सोहिमणुप्पत्ता, आययंति मणुस्संयं ॥३.७॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

kammāṇaṃ tu pahāṇāe, āṇupuvvī kayāi u |
jīvā sohimaṇuppattā, āyayaṃti maṇussaṃyaṃ || 3.7 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Through the gradual wearing away of karma, following the natural sequence — souls endowed with a capacity for awakening attain human birth.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **kammāṇaṃ** = of karma
- **pahāṇāe** = through the wearing away, through the exhaustion
- **āṇupuvvī** = in sequence, gradually (anupūrvī)
- **kayāi** = having done, having undergone
- **jīvā** = souls
- **sohimaṇuppattā** = endowed with the capacity for awakening/purification (śodhi-manah-utpanna)
- **āyayaṃti** = attain, arrive at
- **maṇussaṃyaṃ** = human birth (manuṣyatva)

### 5. COMMENTARY

Human birth is not random — it comes through the gradual wearing away of obstructing karma. The word **sohimaṇuppattā** — endowed with the seed of awakening — is crucial. Not every soul that reaches human birth has this seed. The ones who do are the ones for whom the four rarities can potentially converge. This sutra establishes the first rarity: **manuṣyatva** (human birth) is not just rare — it is the culmination of an extremely long process of karmic exhaustion.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Through the gradual exhaustion of karma, souls with a capacity for awakening finally attain the rare human birth.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Human birth is not an accident — it's the result of an immensely long karmic process. What does that tell me about the value of this moment?

## SUTRA 3.8

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

माणुस्संमि आयाओ, जो धम्मं सोच्च सद्दहे ।
माणुसत्तं सुई सद्धा, संवुडे णिद्धुणे रयं ॥३.८॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

māṇussaṃmi āyāo, jo dhammaṃ socca saddahe |
māṇusattaṃ suī saddhā, saṃvuḍe ṇiddhuṇe rayaṃ || 3.8 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Having attained human birth, one who hears the dharma and develops faith — possessing human birth, hearing, and faith — becomes restrained and shakes off the dust of karma.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **māṇussaṃmi āyāo** = having come to / having attained human birth
- **jo** = who, the one who
- **dhammaṃ** = dharma, the teaching
- **socca** = having heard (śrutvā)
- **saddahe** = believes, develops faith (śraddhā)
- **saṃvuḍe** = becomes restrained (saṃvṛta — covered in self-discipline)
- **ṇiddhuṇe** = shakes off
- **rayaṃ** = the dust/impurity of karma (rajas — the stain of karma)

### 5. COMMENTARY

This verse shows how the four rarities work together: human birth → hearing dharma → faith → restraint → karmic purification. The sequence is not arbitrary — each step enables the next. Without human birth, you cannot hear dharma. Without hearing, you cannot develop faith. Without faith, you cannot sustain restraint. Without restraint, you cannot shake off karma. The word **ṇiddhuṇe rayaṃ** — "shakes off the dust" — is a vivid image: karma is like dust clinging to the soul. Restraint is the vigorous shaking that dislodges it.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Having gained human birth, one who hears dharma and develops faith becomes restrained — and shakes off the dust of karma.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Hearing, believing, practising, purifying — which step am I on? And what is keeping me from the next one?

## SUTRA 3.9

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

आहच्च सवणं लद्धुं, सद्धा परमदुल्लहा ।
सोच्चा णेयाउयं मग्गं, बहवे परिभस्सइ ॥३.९॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

āhacca savaṇaṃ laddhuṃ, saddhā paramadullahā |
soccā ṇeyāuyaṃ maggaṃ, bahave paribhassai || 3.9 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Even having obtained the hearing of dharma, faith is supremely difficult to obtain. Many, even after hearing the path that should be known, fall away.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **āhacca** = even, having reached, having obtained
- **savaṇaṃ** = hearing (of dharma)
- **laddhuṃ** = having obtained
- **saddhā** = faith (śraddhā)
- **paramadullahā** = supremely difficult to obtain, extremely rare
- **soccā** = having heard
- **ṇeyāuyaṃ** = that which should be known, the knowable
- **maggaṃ** = the path
- **bahave** = many (people)
- **paribhassai** = fall away, deviate, are lost

### 5. COMMENTARY

This sutra makes a sobering distinction: hearing is one thing, faith is another. **Paramadullahā** — supremely difficult — is applied to faith, not hearing. Many people hear the dharma; far fewer develop genuine conviction. And even among those who hear and intellectually understand, **bahave paribhassai** — many fall away. The path from hearing to faith involves a transformation that intellectual understanding alone cannot accomplish. This is why śraddhā is listed as a separate rarity — it is a distinct and harder attainment than mere hearing.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Even after hearing the dharma, faith is supremely rare. Many hear the path but fall away without developing true conviction.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Have I truly moved from hearing to faith — or am I still at the stage of intellectual interest without inner conviction?

## SUTRA 3.10

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

सुइं च लद्धुं सद्धं च, वीरियं लद्धुं संयमं ।
बहवे रोयमाणा वि, गो य पडिविज्जइ ॥३.१०॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

suiṃ ca laddhuṃ saddhaṃ ca, vīriyaṃ laddhuṃ saṃyamaṃ |
bahave royamāṇā vi, go ya paḍivijjai || 3.10 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Even having obtained hearing, faith, and energy for restraint — many, though weeping with desire, fall back like cattle returning to their pen.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **suiṃ** = hearing (śruti)
- **laddhuṃ** = having obtained
- **saddhaṃ** = faith
- **vīriyaṃ** = energy
- **saṃyamaṃ** = restraint
- **bahave** = many
- **royamāṇā vi** = even while weeping, lamenting
- **go ya** = like cattle
- **paḍivijjai** = fall back, return (to the old ways)

### 5. COMMENTARY

The most poignant verse so far. Even those who have all three — hearing, faith, and energy — can still fall back. The image is devastating: **go ya paḍivijjai** — like cattle returning to their pen. Cattle are led to pasture (freedom), but at evening they return to the pen (bondage) out of habit and comfort. So too, many practitioners who have genuinely begun the path return to their old habits — **royamāṇā vi** — even while weeping, knowing they are losing something precious. This is the tragedy of the fourth rarity: sustained vīrya (energy) in saṃyama is the rarest of all.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Even those who have hearing, faith, and energy for restraint — many fall back like cattle returning to their pen, weeping as they go.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Have I ever known the right path and returned to old habits anyway — weeping inwardly as I did? What would it take to not go back?

## SUTRA 3.11

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

माणुसत्तंमि आयाओ, जो धम्मं सोच्च सद्दहे ।
तवस्सी वीरियं लद्धुं, संवुडे णिद्धुणे रयं ॥३.११॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

māṇusattaṃmi āyāo, jo dhammaṃ socca saddahe |
tavassī vīriyaṃ laddhuṃ, saṃvuḍe ṇiddhuṇe rayaṃ || 3.11 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Having attained human birth, one who hears the dharma and develops faith — endowed with austerity and energy, restrained — shakes off the dust of karma.

### 5. COMMENTARY

This sutra reinforces sutra 3.8 but adds **tavassī** (endowed with tapas/austerity). The complete formula: human birth + hearing + faith + tapas + vīrya + saṃyama = shaking off karmic dust. Tapas is specified separately here because energy (vīrya) and austerity (tapas) are distinct: vīrya is the raw energy, tapas is its directed application through specific disciplines (fasting, meditation, endurance of hardship).

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

One who attains human birth, hears dharma, develops faith, and applies austerity and energy in restraint — shakes off the dust of karma.

## SUTRA 3.12

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

सोही उज्जुयभूयस्स, धम्मो चित्तुइ ।
णिव्वाणं परमं जाइ, चय-सित्तिव्व पावए ॥३.१२॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

sohī ujjuyabhūyassa, dhammo cittuï |
ṇivvāṇaṃ paramaṃ jāi, caya-sittivva pāvae || 3.12 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

For the one who is pure, upright, and straightforward — dharma purifies the consciousness. Such a one attains supreme nirvāṇa — like rain falling on cool ground.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **sohī** = pure, cleansed (śodhita)
- **ujjuyabhūyassa** = of one who is upright, straightforward (ṛju-bhūta)
- **dhammo** = dharma
- **cittuï** = purifies the mind/consciousness
- **ṇivvāṇaṃ** = nirvāṇa, liberation
- **paramaṃ** = supreme, highest
- **jāi** = goes to, attains
- **caya-sittivva** = like rain on cool/cleared ground (chāyā-sīta — cooled ground)
- **pāvae** = attains, reaches

### 5. COMMENTARY

The beautiful simile: dharma falls on the upright soul like rain on prepared, cool ground. Just as rain on cleared, cool earth soaks in and nourishes growth, dharma absorbed by a pure and straightforward consciousness leads to the supreme fruit: nirvāṇa. But rain on rocky, unprepared ground runs off without effect. The quality of the recipient matters as much as the quality of the teaching.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

For the pure and straightforward, dharma purifies the consciousness — and such a one attains supreme nirvāṇa, like rain soaking into cool, prepared earth.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Is my inner ground prepared — cool, cleared, receptive? Or does the rain of dharma run off the surface of my busyness and distraction?

## SUTRA 3.13

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

विगिंच कम्मुणो हेउं, जसं संचिणु खंतिए ।
पाढवं सरीरं हिच्चा, उडुं पक्कमइ दिसं ॥३.१३॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

vigiṃca kammuṇo heuṃ, jasaṃ saṃciṇu khaṃtie |
pāḍhavaṃ sarīraṃ hiccā, uḍuṃ pakkamai disaṃ || 3.13 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Cutting the root cause of karma, accumulating the glory of patience — leaving behind the gross body, the soul moves upward in the direction of liberation.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **vigiṃca** = cutting, severing
- **kammuṇo** = of karma
- **heuṃ** = the cause, the root
- **jasaṃ** = glory, fame (yaśas — the glory of spiritual accomplishment)
- **saṃciṇu** = accumulating, gathering
- **khaṃtie** = through patience, forbearance (kṣānti)
- **pāḍhavaṃ** = the gross, physical
- **sarīraṃ** = body
- **hiccā** = having left, having abandoned
- **uḍuṃ** = upward (ūrdhva)
- **pakkamai** = proceeds, moves
- **disaṃ** = in the direction (of liberation)

### 5. COMMENTARY

The mechanics of liberation in one verse: (1) cut the cause of karma — not just the effect, but the root; (2) accumulate the glory of kṣānti (patience) — the most fundamental spiritual virtue; (3) leave the physical body — the final shedding at death; (4) move upward — the soul freed from karma naturally rises to the top of the loka (siddha-śilā). The word **uḍuṃ** (upward) is both physical (the soul literally moves up in Jain cosmology) and metaphorical (the soul ascends from bondage to freedom).

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Cutting the root cause of karma, accumulating the glory of patience — the soul leaves the body behind and rises upward toward liberation.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Am I cutting the root of karma — or just trimming the branches? Is patience my practice, or just my wish?

## SUTRA 3.14

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

विसालिसेहिं सीलेहिं, जक्खा उत्तर-उत्तरा ।
महासुक्का व दिप्पंता, मण्णंता अपुणच्चवं ॥३.१४॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

visālisehiṃ sīlehiṃ, jakkhā uttara-uttarā |
mahāsukkā va dippantā, maṇṇaṃtā apuṇaccavaṃ || 3.14 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

By virtues of increasing excellence, celestial beings rise higher and higher — brilliantly radiant like great luminaries, believing their state to be permanent.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **visālisehiṃ** = by extensive, excellent (viśāla)
- **sīlehiṃ** = by virtues, moral practices (śīla)
- **jakkhā** = celestial beings, yakṣas (devas)
- **uttara-uttarā** = higher and higher, each surpassing the one before
- **mahāsukkā** = greatly luminous, supremely radiant
- **dippantā** = shining, blazing
- **maṇṇaṃtā** = believing, thinking, assuming
- **apuṇaccavaṃ** = that there will be no return, that their state is permanent (a-punar-cyava — non-returning)

### 5. COMMENTARY

Now the text shows the trajectory of a soul that has accumulated good karma but not liberation-karma: it rises to celestial realms, becoming increasingly radiant and powerful — but with one fatal flaw: **maṇṇaṃtā apuṇaccavaṃ** — they believe their state is permanent. Even the gods are deluded. Their celestial splendour blinds them to the fact that their merit will eventually run out and they will fall back into lower births. This is why human birth is listed as the first rarity, not celestial birth: the gods' delusion of permanence prevents liberation, while the human's awareness of suffering enables it.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

By great virtues, celestial beings rise ever higher — radiantly brilliant — but believing mistakenly that their state will last forever.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

What in my life am I treating as permanent when it is actually temporary? Am I intoxicated by the radiance of my current good fortune?

## SUTRAS 3.15–16: THE FALL FROM HEAVEN

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

अप्पिया देवकामाणं, कामरूव विउव्विणो ।
उडुं कप्पेसु चित्तुंति, पुव्वा वासया बहू ॥३.१५॥

तत्थ ठिच्चा जहाताणं, जक्खा आउक्खए चुया ।
उवेंति माणुसं जोणिं, से दसंगेऽभिजायइ ॥३.१६॥

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

(15) Having satisfied the desires of divine pleasures, assuming beautiful forms at will — they dwell in the celestial realms for many lifespans.
(16) Standing there for the duration of their merit, when the celestial beings' lifespan is exhausted, they fall — and are born into human birth, possessing the ten limbs of spiritual capacity.

### 5. COMMENTARY

The gods enjoy immense pleasures for immense periods — but when the karmic merit is exhausted (**āukkkhae** — lifespan exhausted), they fall. The word **cuyā** (fallen) is precise: it is a fall, not a graceful descent. The celestial being who thought their state was permanent is suddenly thrown back into the cycle. But here is the crucial point: a soul that falls from a high celestial birth and enters human birth arrives with **dasaṅga** — ten spiritual qualities/limbs. This soul has a particularly strong potential for awakening because of its accumulated celestial merit, now channelled into a human form where liberation is actually possible.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

After enjoying celestial pleasures for long periods, when their merit runs out, the gods fall — and are born as humans with strong spiritual potential.

## SUTRA 3.17

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

खेत्तं वत्थुं हिरण्णं च, पसवो दास पोरुसं ।
चत्तारि काम खंधाणि, तत्थ से उववज्जइ ॥३.१७॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

khettaṃ vatthuṃ hiraṇṇaṃ ca, pasavo dāsa porusaṃ |
cattāri kāma khaṃdhāṇi, tattha se uvavajjai || 3.17 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Fields, property, gold, cattle, servants, attendants — the four branches of worldly desire — all of these come to such a one upon human birth.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **khettaṃ** = fields, land
- **vatthuṃ** = property, dwelling
- **hiraṇṇaṃ** = gold, wealth
- **pasavo** = cattle, livestock
- **dāsa** = servants
- **porusaṃ** = attendants, human resources
- **cattāri kāma khaṃdhāṇi** = the four branches/categories of sensory desire (kāma-skandha)
- **uvavajjai** = are obtained, come to that soul

### 5. COMMENTARY

The soul born human after a celestial life arrives with all the material conditions for comfort: land, wealth, servants, livestock — the four classical categories of worldly prosperity. This is the result of merit, not luck. The danger, however, is clear: will this prosperity be used to deepen spiritual practice, or will it become a new form of bondage? The chapter's thesis is that even all these blessings are worthless without the four rarities. Wealth is simply the context; what you do with the four rarities is the content.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Such a soul is born into wealth — land, gold, cattle, servants — all the branches of worldly prosperity come to them naturally.

## SUTRA 3.18

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

मित्तवं णायवं होइ, उच्चागोए य वण्णवं ।
अप्पायंके महापण्णे, अभिजाए जसोबले ॥३.१८॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

mittavaṃ ṇāyavaṃ hoi, uccāgoe ya vaṇṇavaṃ |
appāyaṃke mahāpaṇṇe, abhijāe jasobale || 3.18 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Such a one is blessed with friends, relatives, high birth, good complexion, few ailments, great wisdom, noble lineage, and the power of fame.

### 5. COMMENTARY

The full catalogue of worldly blessings for a soul entering human birth with strong celestial merit: friendship, kinship, high social status, physical beauty, health, intelligence, noble birth, and fame. These are not merely desirable — they are the optimal conditions for spiritual practice. But again, the teaching is clear: these are conditions, not guarantees. Without the four rarities, all of this is simply the gilded cage of saṃsāra.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

They are born with friends, family, high status, beauty, health, wisdom, nobility, and fame — all the conditions for spiritual practice.

## SUTRA 3.19

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

भोच्चा माणुस्सए भोए, अप्पडिरूवे अहाउयं ।
पुव्वं विसुद्ध-सद्धम्मे, केवलं बोहि बुज्झया ॥३.१९॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

bhoccā māṇussae bhoe, appaḍirūve ahāuyaṃ |
puvvaṃ visuddha-saddhamme, kevalaṃ bohi bujjhayā || 3.19 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Having experienced unmatched human pleasures according to lifespan — established in the previously purified true dharma — such a one awakens to kevala-jñāna (omniscience).

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **bhoccā** = having experienced
- **māṇussae** = human
- **bhoe** = pleasures, enjoyments
- **appaḍirūve** = unmatched, unequalled
- **ahāuyaṃ** = according to lifespan (yathā-āyuṣya)
- **puvvaṃ** = previously, in former lives
- **visuddha-saddhamme** = in the purified true dharma (viśuddha-saddharma)
- **kevalaṃ** = kevala — absolute, complete, omniscient
- **bohi** = bodhi — awakening, enlightenment
- **bujjhayā** = awakens to, attains

### 5. COMMENTARY

The culmination: the soul that has used its human birth properly — experiencing life but grounded in the purified dharma from past lives — attains **kevala-jñāna**: complete omniscience. This is not the enlightenment of partial knowing; it is the total, simultaneous knowing of everything — past, present, and future, in all realms. **Kevala-bodhi** is the distinctive Jain term for this supreme awakening, reserved for those who have completely destroyed the four destructive karmas (knowledge-obscuring, vision-obscuring, deluding, and obstructing).

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Having lived a full human life grounded in purified dharma — such a one awakens to kevala-jñāna, complete omniscient knowledge.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Omniscience is not a supernatural gift — it is the natural state of the soul when all karma is removed. What is blocking mine?

## SUTRA 3.20

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

चउरंगं दुल्लहं णच्चा, संजमं पडिवज्जिया ।
तवसा धुयकम्मंसे, सिद्धे हवइ सासए ॥३.२०॥ – ति बेमि

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

cauraṃgaṃ dullahaṃ ṇaccā, saṃjamaṃ paḍivajjiyā |
tavasā dhuyakaṃmaṃse, siddhe havai sāsae || 3.20 || — ti bemi

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Knowing the four-fold rarity, having embraced restraint — by austerity having shaken off karma — such a one becomes siddha, eternally. Thus I say.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **cauraṃgaṃ** = the four-fold (caturaṅga — the four rarities)
- **dullahaṃ** = as rare, as difficult to obtain
- **ṇaccā** = knowing, recognising (jñātvā)
- **saṃjamaṃ** = restraint
- **paḍivajjiyā** = having embraced, having entered into
- **tavasā** = by austerity, through tapas
- **dhuyakaṃmaṃse** = having shaken off karma (dhūta-karma-aṃśa)
- **siddhe** = siddha — the liberated one
- **havai** = becomes
- **sāsae** = eternally, permanently
- **ti bemi** = thus I say (the canonical closing formula)

### 5. COMMENTARY

The perfect conclusion, closing the circle opened in sutra 1. Knowing the four rarities as rare → embracing restraint → burning karma through tapas → becoming siddha eternally. The word **sāsae** (eternally, śāśvata) is the final note: liberation is not temporary like celestial pleasure. It is permanent. Once a soul becomes siddha, it never returns to bondage. The closing formula **ti bemi** — "thus I say" — marks this as the teaching of Lord Mahāvīra himself, transmitted through the canonical tradition.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Recognising the four rarities, embracing restraint, burning off karma through austerity — such a one becomes siddha, liberated forever. Thus I say.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

The four rarities are in your hands right now — human birth, access to teaching, the capacity for faith, and the energy for practice. The question is not "can I?" — it is "will I?"
