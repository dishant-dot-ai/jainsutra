# Uttaradhyayana Sutra — Chapter 3: Chaturangiya (ચતુરંગીય)

**Translation by:** Dishant Shah
**Source:** Gujarati PDF commentary (Shri Uttaradhyayan Sutra-1)
**Chapter:** Third Adhyayana (ત્રીજું અધ્યયન)
**Total Sutras:** 20
**Theme:** The Four Rare Supreme Attributes — (1) Human birth (Manushyattva), (2) Hearing dharma (Shruti), (3) Faith (Shraddha), and (4) Effort in self-restraint (Virya). These four together are exceedingly rare and constitute the path to liberation.

---

## PART I — The Four Rare Supreme Attributes (Sutras 1–7)

---

### SUTRA 3.1

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

चत्तारि परमंगाणि, दुल्लहाणीह जंतुणो ।
माणुसत्तं सुई सद्धा, संजमम्मि य वीरियं ॥३.१॥

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

cattāri paramaṃgāṇi, dullahāṇīha jaṃtuṇo |
māṇusattaṃ suī saddhā, saṃjamammi ya vīriyaṃ || 3.1 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Four supreme attributes are exceedingly rare for a living being in this world: human birth, hearing the dharma, faith in it, and effort in self-restraint.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **इह** = in this world (samsara)
- **जंतुणो** = for a living being
- **माणुसत्तं** = human birth (manushyattva)
- **सुई** = hearing the dharma-shastra (shruti)
- **सद्धा** = faith in dharma (shraddha)
- **संजमम्मि** = in self-restraint (sanyam)
- **वीरियं** = effort, vigour (virya)
- **चत्तारि** = these four
- **परमंगाणि** = supreme attributes/limbs
- **दुल्लहाणीह** = are exceedingly rare to attain

**5. COMMENTARY**

These four are called "parama-anga" (supreme limbs) because they are the nearest and most essential causes of liberation. Among all the beings wandering in the endless cycle of samsara, obtaining even one of these is extraordinarily rare — to obtain all four simultaneously is the rarest of the rare. The word "parama" emphasizes that these are not ordinary qualities but the highest prerequisites for spiritual progress. Hearing dharma (shruti) here means exposure to the authentic teachings of the Tirthankara tradition, not mere worldly learning. Faith (shraddha) means genuine conviction in the truth of dharma, not blind belief. Virya means sustained spiritual effort, not mere physical strength.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: Out of all the beings in existence, getting four things at once is incredibly rare — being born human, getting to hear true spiritual teachings, actually believing in them, and having the willpower to live by them.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

Of these four rare gifts, which one do I take most for granted — and what would my life look like if I truly honored it?

### SUTRA 3.2

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

समावण्णाण संसारे, णाणा-गोत्तासु जाइसु ।
कम्मा णाणा-विहा कट्टु, पुत्तो विस्संभिया पया ॥३.२॥

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

samāvaṇṇāṇa saṃsāre, ṇāṇā-gottāsu jāisu |
kammā ṇāṇā-vihā kaṭṭu, putto vissaṃbhiyā payā || 3.2 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Having entered the cycle of samsara and taken birth in various lineages and species, living beings — performing diverse kinds of karma — have wandered extensively, having been separated from their true nature.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **संसारे** = in samsara (the cycle of existence)
- **समावण्णाण** = having entered, fallen into
- **णाणा-गोत्तासु** = in various lineages/clans (gotra)
- **जाइसु** = in births/species
- **कम्मा** = karmas, actions
- **णाणा-विहा** = of various kinds
- **कट्टु** = having performed
- **पुत्तो** = creatures, beings
- **विस्संभिया** = having touched/experienced extensively
- **पया** = the world, all directions of existence

**5. COMMENTARY**

This sutra sets the stage for understanding why the four attributes of Sutra 1 are so rare. Beings have been wandering through samsara since beginningless time, accumulating diverse karmas that propel them into countless species and lineages. The word "vishvambhiya" suggests that beings have traversed every corner of existence — there is no birth-form they have not experienced. This vast wandering makes the convergence of the four rare attributes all the more precious when it finally occurs.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: Every soul has been wandering through countless lives — born as gods, humans, animals, insects — doing all kinds of deeds, scattered across every possible form of existence since time without beginning.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

If my soul has already wandered through infinite births, what makes this particular human life different from all the ones before?

### SUTRA 3.3

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

एगया देवलोएसु, णरएसु वि एगया ।
एगया आसुरे काये, अहाकम्मेहिं गच्छइ ॥३.३॥

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

egayā devaloēsu, ṇaraēsu vi egayā |
egayā āsure kāye, ahākammehiṃ gacchai || 3.3 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Sometimes a being goes to the celestial realms, sometimes to the hells, and sometimes into demonic forms — all according to its karmas.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **अहाकम्मेहिं** = according to one's karmas
- **एगया** = sometimes, at one time
- **देवलोएसु** = in the realms of the gods (devaloka)
- **णरएसु** = in the hells (naraka)
- **वि** = also
- **आसुरे काये** = in the body of an asura (demonic form)
- **गच्छइ** = goes, is born

**5. COMMENTARY**

This sutra illustrates the soul's helpless wandering through the four major categories of existence (gati). Driven by karma, the same soul that once enjoyed celestial pleasures may find itself in hellish suffering, or take birth in a demonic form. The repetition of "egaya" (sometimes) emphasizes the randomness and unpredictability of karmic outcomes — the soul has no control over where it will go next. This instability is precisely what makes the human birth, where spiritual progress is possible, so precious.

📷 Visual Aid: A single soul shown in three panels — radiant in a celestial realm, suffering in a dark hell pit, and crouching in a demonic form — illustrating the same soul's helpless drift across realms driven by karma.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: The same soul bounces around — sometimes born as a god in heaven, sometimes suffering in hell, sometimes in a demon's body — wherever its past actions send it.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

If my next birth is entirely determined by my actions right now, what am I choosing?

### SUTRA 3.4

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

एगया खत्तिओ होइ, ताओ चंडाल बुक्कसो ।
ताओ कीडपयंगो य, ताओ कुंथू पिवीलिया ॥३.४॥

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

egayā khattiō hōi, tāō caṃḍāla bukkasō |
tāō kīḍapayaṃgō ya, tāō kuṃthū pivīliyā || 3.4 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Sometimes one is born a warrior-king, and from there a chandala or an outcast; from there an insect or a moth, and from there a louse or an ant.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **खत्तिओ** = kshatriya (warrior/king)
- **होइ** = becomes
- **ताओ** = from there, thereafter
- **चंडाल** = chandala (one of low social standing)
- **बुक्कसो** = bukkasa (one considered of lowest rank)
- **कीडपयंगो** = insect, worm
- **य** = and
- **कुंथू** = louse, tiny organism
- **पिवीलिया** = ant

**5. COMMENTARY**

This verse dramatically illustrates the extreme swings of karmic fortune even within the human and sub-human realms. A soul that was once a powerful kshatriya king can, in its very next birth, be born as a chandala — and from there sink further into insect life as a worm, louse, or ant. The use of "asura" and "deva" in the previous sutra showed macro-level wandering across realms; this sutra shows the micro-level volatility even within adjacent births. No worldly status is permanent; only spiritual progress carries forward.

📷 Visual Aid: A descending staircase showing a crowned warrior-king at the top, then a ragged outcast, then a worm, then a tiny ant at the bottom — illustrating the soul's dramatic karmic fall across births.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: A soul can go from being a powerful king in one life to a street sweeper in the next, then to a tiny worm, then to a louse — karma doesn't care about your status.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

How much of my identity is built on things — status, body, possessions — that will not follow me beyond this life?

### SUTRA 3.5

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

एवमावट्ट जोणीसु, पाणिणो कम्मकिव्विसा ।
एवमावट्टजोणीसु, पाणिणो कम्मकिव्विसा ॥३.५॥

⚠️ The second line of this sutra is partially obscured in the source PDF. The Prakrit above is based on the visible shabdarth. The full verse likely continues with a statement about beings not finding liberation in samsara. Verification from additional scholarly sources recommended.

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

evamāvaṭṭa jōṇīsu, pāṇiṇō kammakivvisā |
⚠️ [second line requires verification] || 3.5 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Thus revolving through various birth-forms, beings poisoned by karma do not find peace in samsara.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **एवमावट्ट** = thus revolving, rotating in this manner
- **जोणीसु** = in various birth-forms (yonis)
- **पाणिणो** = living beings
- **कम्मकिव्विसा** = poisoned/afflicted by karma

**5. COMMENTARY**

This sutra summarizes the relentless churning described in the previous verses. The powerful metaphor of "kamma-kivvisa" (karma-poison) compares karma to a venom that has infected the soul, keeping it trapped in an endless cycle of births. Just as a poisoned person cannot think clearly or act freely, a karma-afflicted soul cannot break free from samsara on its own. The word "avaṭṭa" (revolving) suggests not linear progression but circular repetition — the soul goes round and round without advancing, like a bullock tied to a grinding wheel.

📷 Visual Aid: A bullock blindfolded and tied to a grinding wheel, walking in an endless circle — representing the soul revolving through samsara, poisoned by karma, never advancing.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: Souls keep spinning through life after life — poisoned by their own past actions — unable to find any lasting peace or escape from the cycle.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

In what ways am I going in circles in my own life — repeating the same patterns, driven by the same habits — mistaking motion for progress?

### SUTRA 3.6

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

कम्म-संगेहिं सम्मूढा, दुक्खिया बहुवेयणा ।
अमाणुसासु जोणीसु, विणिहम्मंति पाणिणो ॥३.६॥

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

kamma-saṃgēhiṃ sammūḍhā, dukkhiyā bahuvēyaṇā |
amāṇusāsu jōṇīsu, viṇihammanti pāṇiṇō || 3.6 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Deluded by the attachment to karma, afflicted and experiencing manifold suffering, beings are cast into non-human birth-forms.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **कम्म-संगेहिं** = by attachment to karma, by karmic bondage
- **सम्मूढा** = deluded, confused
- **दुक्खिया** = afflicted, suffering
- **बहुवेयणा** = experiencing many types of suffering (vedana)
- **अमाणुसासु** = in non-human
- **जोणीसु** = birth-forms (yonis)
- **विणिहम्मंति** = are cast down, thrown into
- **पाणिणो** = living beings

**5. COMMENTARY**

The word "sammudha" (deluded) is key — it is not that beings choose suffering, but that they are so confused by karmic attachment that they cannot see clearly. This delusion leads to actions that further bind them, creating a vicious cycle. The phrase "bahu-veyana" (manifold suffering) reminds us that sub-human births carry not just one type of pain but many — physical, mental, and existential. Being "cast" (vinihammanti) into these births implies helplessness; the soul does not choose but is thrown by the force of its own accumulated karma.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: When souls are blinded by attachment to their actions, they suffer greatly and keep getting thrown into animal, plant, and other non-human lives where the suffering is even worse.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

What attachments in my life are so strong that they might be blinding me to consequences I cannot yet see?

### SUTRA 3.7

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

कम्माणं तु पहाणाए, आणुपुव्वी कयाइ उ ।
जीवा सोहिमणुप्पत्ता, आचयंति मणुस्सयं ॥३.७॥

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

kammāṇaṃ tu pahāṇāē, āṇupuvvī kayāi u |
jīvā sōhimaṇuppattā, ācayaṃti maṇussayaṃ || 3.7 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

When karmas gradually diminish and a sequence of purification occurs, souls attain a degree of inner clarity and thereby obtain human birth.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **कम्माणं** = of karmas
- **तु** = indeed, but
- **पहाणाए** = by the diminishing, by the shedding
- **आणुपुव्वी** = gradually, in sequence (anupurvi)
- **कयाइ** = sometimes, at some point
- **उ** = indeed
- **जीवा** = souls
- **सोहिमणुप्पत्ता** = having attained purity/clarity (shodhana + utpatti)
- **आचयंति** = obtain, reach
- **मणुस्सयं** = human birth (manushyattva)

**5. COMMENTARY**

After the bleak picture of sutras 2–6, this verse offers a ray of hope. Human birth is not random — it occurs when karmas gradually shed and the soul undergoes a process of purification. The word "anupurvi" (in sequence) is significant: liberation doesn't happen in a sudden leap but through a gradual unfolding. Even amidst the vast ocean of samsara, the soul's inherent tendency toward purity (shodhi) eventually surfaces. This is why Jain philosophy never considers any soul permanently lost — every soul has the potential to rise. Human birth is the fruit of this rising.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: When the weight of past karma slowly lightens and the soul becomes a little purer, it finally gets the chance to be born as a human being — the one birth from which real spiritual progress is possible.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

If my human birth is the result of ages of gradual purification, what responsibility does that place on how I use this life?

## PART II — Hearing Dharma and Faith (Sutras 8–9)

---

### SUTRA 3.8

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

⚠️ The Prakrit text for this sutra falls at a page boundary in the source PDF (pages 71–72) and is partially obscured. The following is reconstructed from the shabdarth (word-meanings) provided in the source and standard scholarly editions:

माणुसत्तं च लद्धं सुई, धम्मस्स चेव दुल्लहा ।
जं सोच्चा पडिवज्जंति, तवं खंतिमहिंसयं ॥३.८॥

(Prakrit reconstructed from standard sources)

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

māṇusattaṃ ca laddhaṃ suī, dhammassa cēva dullahā |
jaṃ soccā paḍivajjanti, tavaṃ khaṃtimahiṃsayaṃ || 3.8 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Having obtained human birth, hearing the dharma is itself exceedingly rare — for those who hear it and then adopt austerity, patience, and non-violence are rarer still.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **माणुसत्तं** = human birth
- **लद्धं** = having obtained
- **सुई** = hearing (shruti)
- **धम्मस्स** = of the dharma
- **दुल्लहा** = is rare, difficult to attain
- **जं** = that which
- **सोच्चा** = having heard
- **पडिवज्जंति** = they adopt, undertake
- **तवं** = austerity (tapa)
- **खंति** = patience, forbearance (kshanti)
- **महिंसयं** = non-violence (ahimsa)

**5. COMMENTARY**

Even after obtaining the rare human birth, hearing authentic dharma is itself another layer of rarity. The sutra then adds a third filter: among those who hear dharma, how many actually adopt its practices — austerity (tapa, the 12 types of self-discipline), patience (kshanti, enduring hardship without resentment), and non-violence (ahimsa, including abstention from falsehood, theft, sensuality, and possessiveness)? Each stage is a further winnowing. Millions are born human; fewer hear true dharma; fewer still practice what they hear.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: Being born human is rare enough, but actually hearing real spiritual teachings is even rarer — and among those who hear, only a handful actually put it into practice through self-discipline, patience, and non-violence.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

I have heard the dharma — but am I among those who merely hear, or among the rare few who actually live it?

### SUTRA 3.9

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

आहच्च सवणं लद्धुं, सद्धा परमदुल्लहा ।
सोच्चा णेयाउयं मग्गं, बहवे परिभस्सइ ॥३.९॥

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

āhacca savaṇaṃ laddhuṃ, saddhā paramadullahā |
soccā ṇeyāuyaṃ maggaṃ, bahavē paribhassai || 3.9 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Even after obtaining the hearing of dharma, faith is supremely rare to attain. Having heard the righteous path, many still fall away from it.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **आहच्च** = having obtained, even after
- **सवणं** = hearing (dharma-shravana)
- **लद्धुं** = having attained
- **सद्धा** = faith, conviction (shraddha)
- **परमदुल्लहा** = supremely rare, exceedingly difficult to attain
- **सोच्चा** = having heard
- **णेयाउयं** = righteous, proper (nyaya-yukta)
- **मग्गं** = the path (marga — the path of samyak-darshana, etc.)
- **बहवे** = many (people)
- **परिभस्सइ** = fall away from, deviate from, become corrupted

**5. COMMENTARY**

This is one of the most psychologically penetrating verses in the chapter. It acknowledges a painful truth: hearing dharma is not enough. Many people hear the teachings, intellectually understand the path, and yet fail to develop genuine shraddha (faith/conviction). The word "paribhassai" (fall away) suggests that without deep inner faith, knowledge alone is unstable — like a vessel without an anchor, the person drifts away from the path they once understood. Faith here is not blind belief but an unshakeable inner conviction (samyak-darshana) that transforms one's entire orientation toward life and liberation.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: Even among people who hear genuine spiritual truth, developing real faith — deep, unshakeable belief — is the rarest thing. Many hear the right path and still wander away from it.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

Have I truly internalized what I know to be true, or is my understanding still only intellectual — untested by real commitment?

## PART III — Effort in Self-Restraint (Sutra 10)

---

### SUTRA 3.10

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

सुइं च लद्धुं सद्धं च, वीरियं पुण दुल्लहं ।
बहवे रोयमाणा वि, णो तं पडिवज्जए ॥३.१०॥

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

suiṃ ca laddhuṃ saddhaṃ ca, vīriyaṃ puṇa dullahaṃ |
bahavē rōyamāṇā vi, ṇō taṃ paḍivajjaē || 3.10 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Even after obtaining hearing and faith, effort (in self-restraint) is yet more rare. Many desire it earnestly, yet they cannot adopt it.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **सुइं** = hearing of dharma (shruti)
- **च** = and
- **लद्धुं** = having obtained
- **सद्धं** = faith (shraddha)
- **वीरियं** = effort, vigour in self-restraint (virya)
- **पुण** = even more, still further
- **दुल्लहं** = rare, difficult
- **बहवे** = many (people)
- **रोयमाणा** = desiring, longing, weeping for it
- **वि** = even, although
- **णो** = not
- **तं** = that (self-restraint)
- **पडिवज्जए** = adopt, undertake, practice

**5. COMMENTARY**

This sutra completes the ascending hierarchy of rarity: human birth → hearing dharma → faith → and now the rarest of all, actually putting self-restraint into practice. The word "royamana" (weeping/longing) is striking — it describes people who genuinely want to practice dharma, who feel the pull toward renunciation, yet cannot bring themselves to take the step. This may be due to worldly attachments, family obligations, fear, or simply the overwhelming difficulty of sustained self-discipline. The Uttaradhyayana acknowledges this human struggle honestly, without judgment — recognizing that desire alone is not enough; virya (spiritual heroism) is required.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: The hardest part isn't hearing the truth or even believing it — it's actually living it. Many people desperately want to practice self-restraint but still can't manage to do it.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

Where in my life do I know exactly what I should do but still can't bring myself to do it — and what is truly holding me back?

## PART IV — The Fruit of Attaining the Four Attributes (Sutras 11–20)

---

### SUTRA 3.11

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

माणुसत्तंमि आयाओ, जो धम्मं सोच्च सद्दहे ।
तवस्सी वीरियं लद्धुं, संवुडे णिद्धुणो रयं ॥३.११॥

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

māṇusattaṃmi āyāō, jō dhammaṃ sōcca saddahē |
tavassī vīriyaṃ laddhuṃ, saṃvuḍē ṇiddhūṇō rayaṃ || 3.11 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

One who, having come into human birth, hears the dharma and develops faith — that one, becoming an ascetic and obtaining spiritual effort, becomes restrained and shakes off the dust of karma.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **माणुसत्तंमि** = in human existence (manushyattva)
- **आयाओ** = having arrived, having been born
- **जो** = who, that soul which
- **धम्मं** = the dharma
- **सोच्च** = having heard (shravana)
- **सद्दहे** = develops faith, believes (shraddha)
- **तवस्सी** = becoming an ascetic, practising austerity (tapasvi)
- **वीरियं** = effort, vigour (virya)
- **लद्धुं** = having obtained
- **संवुडे** = becomes restrained, practicing samvara (stopping new karma)
- **णिद्धुणो** = shakes off, destroys
- **रयं** = the dust of karma (raja)

**5. COMMENTARY**

This sutra marks the transition from describing rarity to describing the fruit. The soul that obtains all four rare attributes — human birth, hearing, faith, and effort — now begins the actual work of liberation. "Samvude" (restrained) refers to samvara, the stopping of new karmic influx. "Niddhuno rayam" (shaking off the dust of karma) refers to nirjara, the shedding of accumulated karma through austerity. Together, samvara and nirjara are the two active processes that lead to moksha.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: The person who is born human, hears the truth, believes it, and then actually commits to spiritual discipline — that person stops accumulating new karma and begins shaking off the old, like dusting off a garment.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

Am I only accumulating, or have I also begun the work of letting go?

### SUTRA 3.12

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

सोही उज्जुयभूयस्स, धम्मो चिट्ठइ ।
णिव्वाणं परमं जाइ, चयसित्तिप्प पावए ॥३.१२॥

⚠️ The first line of this sutra appears to be a half-verse (shorter meter) in the source PDF. The text above is extracted as visible; verification from additional scholarly editions is recommended.

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

sōhī ujjuyabhūyassa, dhammō ciṭṭhai |
ṇivvāṇaṃ paramaṃ jāi, cayasittippa pāvaē || 3.12 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Dharma remains stable only in one who is pure and upright in nature. Such a one attains the supreme state of nirvana — the eternal cessation of karmic bondage.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **सोही** = of the pure one, one with inner clarity
- **उज्जुयभूयस्स** = of one who is upright, straightforward by nature (ujju = straight, bhuta = nature)
- **धम्मो** = dharma
- **चिट्ठइ** = remains, stays stable, endures
- **णिव्वाणं** = nirvana, liberation
- **परमं** = supreme, ultimate
- **जाइ** = attains, reaches
- **चयसित्तिप्प** = through the cessation of karmic activity
- **पावए** = obtains, reaches

**5. COMMENTARY**

A critical insight: dharma does not remain stable in everyone equally. It endures only in the soul that is fundamentally "ujjuya-bhuta" — straight, honest, and simple in nature. Crookedness, deceit, and pretense are enemies of spiritual progress. The word "chitthai" (remains stable) suggests dharma is like a seed — it grows only in prepared soil. A crooked mind may hear dharma and even practice it temporarily, but it will not take root. The reward for such uprightness is "nivvana parama" — the supreme liberation, the final end of all karmic bondage.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: Real spiritual truth only sticks with honest, straightforward people. If you're genuinely sincere, you'll reach the highest liberation — but if you're crooked inside, the teachings won't hold.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

Am I truly straightforward in my spiritual life, or do I carry hidden crookedness — saying one thing while meaning another?

### SUTRA 3.13

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

विग्गिंच कम्मुणो हेउं, जसं संचिणु खंतिए ।
पाढवं सरीरं हिच्चा, उड्ढं पक्कमइ दिसं ॥३.१३॥

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

viggiṃca kammuṇō hēuṃ, jasaṃ saṃciṇu khaṃtiē |
pāḍhavaṃ sarīraṃ hiccā, uḍḍhaṃ pakkamai disaṃ || 3.13 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Cut off the causes of karma! Accumulate the glory of patience! Abandoning this physical body, one ascends to the highest realm.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **विग्गिंच** = cut off, sever, remove
- **कम्मुणो** = of karma
- **हेउं** = the cause, the root
- **जसं** = glory, fame, merit
- **संचिणु** = accumulate, gather
- **खंतिए** = through patience, forbearance (kshanti)
- **पाढवं** = physical, earthly (parthiva — of the earth)
- **सरीरं** = body (sharira)
- **हिच्चा** = leaving, abandoning
- **उड्ढं** = upward
- **पक्कमइ** = proceeds, goes forth
- **दिसं** = direction, realm (towards moksha or higher states)

**5. COMMENTARY**

This is a call to action — an imperative addressed directly to the spiritual aspirant. The two commands are complementary: "vigginca" (cut off!) targets the causes of new karma, while "sancinu" (accumulate!) targets the merit that comes from patient endurance. The promise is concrete: one who does this leaves behind the physical body and ascends upward (uddham) — a reference to the liberated soul rising to Siddha-loka, the highest point of the universe. The "physical body" (padhavam sariram) specifically means the gross audārika sharira, emphasizing that the soul's true nature is not this body.

📷 Visual Aid: A luminous soul rising upward from an abandoned physical body on the ground, ascending toward a radiant point at the apex of the universe representing Siddha-loka.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: Stop doing the things that create karma. Build your strength through patience. When you leave this body behind, your soul will rise to the highest place.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

What is one cause of karma in my life that I could cut off today — and what patience am I being called to develop?

### SUTRA 3.14

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

विसालिसेहिं सीलेहिं, जक्खा उत्तर-उत्तरा ।
महासुक्का व दिप्पंता, मण्णंता अपुणच्चवं ॥३.१४॥

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

visālisēhiṃ sīlēhiṃ, jakkhā uttara-uttarā |
mahāsukkā va dippantā, maṇṇantā apuṇaccavaṃ || 3.14 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Through diverse virtuous conduct, one ascends to progressively higher celestial states — shining like a great radiant light, believing oneself to be beyond rebirth.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **विसालिसेहिं** = through diverse, manifold
- **सीलेहिं** = virtuous conduct, moral discipline (shila)
- **जक्खा** = yaksha (celestial being, deva)
- **उत्तर-उत्तरा** = progressively higher, each one higher than the last
- **महासुक्का** = greatly radiant, of great purity (maha-shukla)
- **व** = like, as if
- **दिप्पंता** = shining, blazing with light
- **मण्णंता** = thinking, believing
- **अपुणच्चवं** = that there will be no further fall, no rebirth (apunarbhava)

**5. COMMENTARY**

Through the practice of various virtues and moral disciplines (shila), the soul ascends to celestial states, becoming a radiant deva. The phrase "uttara-uttara" (higher and higher) indicates the hierarchy of celestial realms — from the Saudharma heaven upward through the 16 heavens, the Graiveyaka, and the Anuttara realms. The beings there shine brilliantly and even believe they are beyond rebirth (apunachavam). Yet this belief is mistaken — even celestial life is impermanent. Only through the complete path of the four attributes does one reach true permanence.

📷 Visual Aid: A stacked diagram of progressively higher and more luminous celestial realms, with glowing divine beings at each tier, each one brighter than the last — illustrating the soul's ascent through the hierarchy of heavens.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: Through good conduct, souls rise to higher and higher heavenly realms, glowing with brilliance. They even start thinking they'll never fall from there — but they're wrong, because even heavenly life doesn't last forever.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

Where in my own life do I mistake a temporary high point for permanent achievement?

### SUTRA 3.15

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

अप्पिया देवकामाणं, कामरूव विउव्विणो ।
उड्ढं कप्पेसु चिट्ठंति, पुव्वा वासया बहू ॥३.१५॥

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

appiyā dēvakāmāṇaṃ, kāmarūva viuvviṇō |
uḍḍhaṃ kappēsu ciṭṭhaṃti, puvvā vāsayā bahū || 3.15 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Enjoying the desires of the gods, assuming forms at will, they dwell in the upper celestial realms for vast periods of time.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **अप्पिया** = having obtained, enjoying
- **देवकामाणं** = the desires/pleasures of the gods (deva-kama)
- **कामरूव** = assuming desired forms at will
- **विउव्विणो** = transforming, creating through supernatural power (vikriya)
- **उड्ढं** = upward, in the upper realms
- **कप्पेसु** = in the kalpas (celestial abodes/heavens)
- **चिट्ठंति** = they dwell, remain
- **पुव्वा** = purva (a vast unit of time)
- **वासया** = years
- **बहू** = many, for a very long time

**5. COMMENTARY**

This sutra describes the celestial life in more detail. Devas enjoy pleasures unimaginable to humans, can assume any form they wish through their supernatural power (vikriya), and dwell in the upper kalpas (heavenly realms) for immeasurably long periods — measured in purvas, where one purva equals 70,56,00,00,00,00,00,000 years. Yet even this astronomically long celestial life is finite. The sutra builds toward the crucial point that will come in the next verse: even this glorious existence must end.

📷 Visual Aid: A radiant divine being in a celestial palace, surrounded by beautiful forms shifting and morphing — illustrating the deva's supernatural power to assume any desired form while enjoying the pleasures of the upper heavens.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: In heaven, beings enjoy every pleasure imaginable, can shapeshift at will, and live for unimaginably long periods — but even that eventually runs out.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

If even heavenly pleasure lasting trillions of years must end, what does that tell me about the things I chase in this short human life?

### SUTRA 3.16

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

तत्थ ठिच्चा जहाताणं, जक्खा आउक्खए चुया ।
उवेंति मणुएसु जोणिं, दसंगेहिभिजायइ ॥३.१६॥

⚠️ The second line of this sutra is partially difficult to read in the source PDF. The above is based on available shabdarth context. Verification recommended.

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

tattha ṭhiccā jahātāṇaṃ, jakkhā āukkhaē cuyā |
uvēṃti maṇuēsu jōṇiṃ, dasaṃgēhibhijāyai || 3.16 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Having dwelt there, when the gods' lifespan expires, they fall from those realms and are born among humans, endowed with ten kinds of good fortune.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **तत्थ** = there (in the celestial realms)
- **ठिच्चा** = having dwelt, having stayed
- **जहाताणं** = when (the time comes)
- **जक्खा** = celestial beings (yakshas/devas)
- **आउक्खए** = at the exhaustion of lifespan (ayu-kshaya)
- **चुया** = fall, die (cyuta — departed from that state)
- **उवेंति** = are born into
- **मणुएसु** = among humans
- **जोणिं** = birth-form
- **दसंगेहिभिजायइ** = born with ten types of fortune/merits

**5. COMMENTARY**

The cycle continues: even the most radiant gods, when their celestial lifespan expires, must descend. But because of their accumulated merit, they are not born in lowly states — they take birth among humans with ten types of good fortune. These ten blessings (dasanga) typically include: noble family, wealth, good health, beauty, long life, wisdom, good company, fame, and favorable circumstances for spiritual practice. This human birth, enriched by prior celestial merit, becomes the ideal launching pad for the final journey to moksha.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: When the gods' time in heaven runs out, they don't go to a bad place — they're born as privileged humans with every advantage: wealth, health, good family, and the perfect setup for spiritual growth.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

If I already have good fortune in this life, could it be the fruit of something earned long ago — and am I using it wisely?

### SUTRA 3.17

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

खेत्तं वत्थुं हिरण्णं च, पसवो दास पोरुसं ।
चत्तारि काम खंधाणि, तत्थ से उववज्जइ ॥३.१७॥

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

khēttaṃ vatthuṃ hiraṇṇaṃ ca, pasavō dāsa pōrusaṃ |
cattāri kāma khaṃdhāṇi, tattha sē uvavajjai || 3.17 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Fields, property, gold, cattle, servants, and attendants — the four categories of worldly enjoyment — all these arise for such a one.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **खेत्तं** = fields, agricultural land (kshetra)
- **वत्थुं** = property, estates, dwellings (vastu)
- **हिरण्णं** = gold, wealth (hiranya)
- **च** = and
- **पसवो** = cattle, livestock (pashava)
- **दास** = servants (dasa)
- **पोरुसं** = attendants, workforce (purusha)
- **चत्तारि** = four
- **काम** = types of worldly pleasure
- **खंधाणि** = categories, groups (skandha)
- **तत्थ** = there, in that birth
- **से** = for that person
- **उववज्जइ** = arise, are obtained (upapatti)

**5. COMMENTARY**

The "four categories of worldly enjoyment" (chattar kama-skandha) encompass all material prosperity. In Jain classification, these are: (1) wealth that brings sustenance (fields, gold), (2) animate possessions (cattle, servants), (3) dwelling and property, and (4) means of power and influence. The sutra does not condemn these — it describes them as the natural fruit of prior merit. The person born with the ten-fold fortune naturally obtains all worldly comforts. The teaching's deeper point will emerge later: even amidst such abundance, the wise person chooses renunciation.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: In that fortunate human birth, the person naturally gets everything — land, wealth, gold, animals, helpers — all four categories of worldly comfort fall into their lap.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

If material abundance is simply the echo of past merit, what truly new merit am I creating in this life?

मित्तवं णायवं होइ, उच्चागोए य वण्णवं ।
अप्पायंके महापण्णे, अभिजाए जसोबले ॥३.१८॥

mittavaṃ ṇāyavaṃ hōi, uccāgōē ya vaṇṇavaṃ |

Such a one is blessed with friends, kinsmen, noble lineage, fine complexion, freedom from disease, great wisdom, high birth, fame, and strength.

### SUTRA 3.19

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

भोच्चा माणुस्सए भोए, अप्पडिरूवे अहाउयं ।
पुव्वं विसुद्ध-सद्धम्मे, केवलं बोहि बुज्झिया ॥३.१९॥

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

bhōccā māṇussaē bhōē, appaḍirūvē ahāuyaṃ |
puvvaṃ visuddha-saddhammē, kēvalaṃ bōhi bujjhiyā || 3.19 ||

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Having experienced the unparalleled pleasures of human life as they arise, and having purified oneself through the true dharma, one attains kevala-jnana — absolute enlightenment.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **भोच्चा** = having experienced, enjoyed
- **माणुस्सए** = human (manushya)
- **भोए** = pleasures, experiences (bhoga)
- **अप्पडिरूवे** = unparalleled, incomparable
- **अहाउयं** = as they arise, according to their nature
- **पुव्वं** = previously, in due course
- **विसुद्ध** = purified (vishuddha)
- **सद्धम्मे** = in the true dharma (sad-dharma)
- **केवलं** = absolute, complete (kevala)
- **बोहि** = enlightenment, knowledge (bodhi)
- **बुज्झिया** = having attained, awakened to

**5. COMMENTARY**

This sutra describes the culmination of the spiritual journey. The fortunate soul, having lived through its human pleasures naturally and without excessive attachment, turns toward dharma and ultimately attains kevala-jnana — the absolute, omniscient knowledge that is the hallmark of a Tirthankara or Kevali. The phrase "vishuddha-saddhamme" (purified through true dharma) emphasizes that moksha comes not through mere austerity but through the authentic, pure dharma of the Jina tradition. Kevala-bodhi is not intellectual knowledge — it is the direct, unmediated perception of all reality.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: After experiencing the good things of human life and practicing true spiritual discipline, the soul finally reaches the ultimate goal — complete, all-knowing enlightenment, seeing everything as it truly is.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

What would it mean for me to enjoy life's blessings without clinging to them — using them as fuel for awakening rather than chains of attachment?

### SUTRA 3.20

**1. PRAKRIT TEXT**

चउरंगं दुल्लहं णच्चा, संजमं पडिवज्जिया ।
तवसा धुयकम्मंसे, सिद्धे हवइ सासए ॥३.२०॥
–ति बेमि ।

**2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION**

caurangaṃ dullahaṃ ṇaccā, saṃjamaṃ paḍivajjiyā |
tavasā dhuyakammansē, siddhē havai sāsaē || 3.20 ||
–ti bēmi |

**3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION**

Knowing the four-fold rarity, one who adopts self-restraint and through austerity shakes off all karma — that one becomes a Siddha forever.

**4. ANVAYARTHA**

- **चउरंगं** = the four-fold (chaturanga — the four rare attributes)
- **दुल्लहं** = rarity, difficulty of attainment
- **णच्चा** = knowing, understanding (jnatva)
- **संजमं** = self-restraint (sanyama)
- **पडिवज्जिया** = having adopted, undertaken (pratipanna)
- **तवसा** = through austerity (tapas)
- **धुयकम्मंसे** = having shaken off all karma (dhuta-karma-amsha)
- **सिद्धे** = becomes a Siddha (a liberated, perfected soul)
- **हवइ** = becomes
- **सासए** = forever, eternally (shashvata)
- **–ति बेमि** = thus I say (the traditional closing by Bhagavan Mahavira)

**5. COMMENTARY**

The chapter concludes with its most powerful verse. The phrase "nacca" (knowing) is deliberate — the journey begins with awareness. One who truly understands how rare these four attributes are will not waste the opportunity. Through sustained self-restraint (sanyama) and austerity (tapas), all karma is completely destroyed (dhuya-kammanse). The result is the state of Siddha — the eternally liberated, perfected soul that dwells at the summit of the universe in infinite knowledge, infinite perception, infinite bliss, and infinite energy. The closing "iti bemi" (thus I say) is Bhagavan Mahavira's traditional seal, marking these as his own words to his disciples.

**6. SIMPLY PUT**

Simply put: If you truly understand how rare it is to have all four — human birth, hearing truth, faith, and willpower — and you use them to practice self-discipline and burn away all karma, you become liberated forever. That's the promise.

**7. CONTEMPLATE**

If this teaching is the direct word of a fully enlightened being who saw all of reality, what am I waiting for?
