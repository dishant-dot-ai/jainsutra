# Uttaradhyayana Sutra — Chapter 29
# Samyak Parākrama (Right Effort)

**Total Sutras:** 73

**Theme:** This chapter presents a comprehensive dialogue on samyak parākrama (right effort) — the complete path of spiritual striving from initial spiritual awakening (saṃvega) to final liberation. Through a systematic question-and-answer format, each sutra explores one station on the path, showing how each practice generates and supports the next in an interconnected chain of spiritual progress. The chapter covers 73 practices (anuṣṭhāna) spanning the entire arc from the first spark of spiritual urgency to the destruction of all karma.

## SUTRA 29.1

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

सुयं मे आउसं ! तेणं भगवया एवमक्खायं– इह खलु सम्मत्तपरक्कमे
णामे अज्झयणे समणेणं भगवया महावीरेणं कासवेणं पवेइए, जं सम्मं
सद्दहित्ता, पत्तियइत्ता, रोयइत्ता, फासित्ता, पालइत्ता, तीरित्ता, किन्तइत्ता,
सोहइत्ता, आराहइत्ता, आणाए अणुपालइत्ता बहवे जीवा सिज्झंति बुज्झंति
मुच्चंति परिणिव्वायंति सव्वदुक्खाणमंतं करेंति ॥२९.१॥

⚠️ Prakrit partially reconstructed from PDF; long introductory sutra condensed.

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Suyaṃ me āusaṃ! Teṇaṃ bhagavayā evamakkhāyaṃ— iha khalu sammattaparakkame
ṇāme ajjhayaṇe samaṇeṇaṃ bhagavayā mahāvīreṇaṃ kāsaveṇaṃ paveie, jaṃ sammaṃ
saddahittā, pattiyaittā, royaittā, phāsittā, pālaittā, tīrittā, kintaittā,
sohaittā, ārāhaittā, āṇāe aṇupālaittā bahave jīvā sijjhaṃti bujjhaṃti
muccaṃti pariṇivvāyaṃti savvadukkhāṇamaṃtaṃ kareṃti || 29.1 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Thus I have heard, O long-lived one! The Blessed One has declared thus — here indeed, in the chapter called Samyak Parākrama (Right Effort), taught by the ascetic Lord Mahāvīra of the Kāśyapa lineage: that by rightly believing, trusting, being illuminated, experiencing, guarding, crossing over, reflecting, purifying, accomplishing, and following the teachings, many souls attain perfection, attain enlightenment, are liberated, reach nirvāṇa, and make an end of all suffering.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **suyaṃ me** — thus I have heard (śrutam mayā)
- **āusaṃ** — O long-lived one (āyuṣman)
- **teṇaṃ bhagavayā** — by that Blessed One
- **evamakkhāyaṃ** — thus declared (evam ākhyātam)
- **sammattaparakkame** — samyak-parākrama (right effort)
- **ṇāme ajjhayaṇe** — named chapter (nāma adhyayana)
- **samaṇeṇaṃ bhagavayā mahāvīreṇaṃ** — by the ascetic Lord Mahāvīra
- **kāsaveṇaṃ** — of the Kāśyapa clan
- **paveie** — taught / expounded (pravacita)
- **sammaṃ saddahittā** — rightly believing (samyak śraddhā)
- **pattiyaittā** — trusting / having conviction (pratyayita)
- **royaittā** — being illuminated / delighting in (rucita)
- **phāsittā** — experiencing / touching directly (sparśita)
- **pālaittā** — guarding / protecting (pālita)
- **tīrittā** — crossing over / completing (tīrita)
- **kintaittā** — reflecting / contemplating (cintita)
- **sohaittā** — purifying (śodhita)
- **ārāhaittā** — accomplishing / worshipping through practice (ārādhita)
- **āṇāe aṇupālaittā** — following the teachings (ājñā anupālita)
- **bahave jīvā** — many souls
- **sijjhaṃti** — attain perfection (sidhyanti)
- **bujjhaṃti** — attain enlightenment (budhyante)
- **muccaṃti** — are liberated (mucyante)
- **pariṇivvāyaṃti** — reach nirvāṇa (parinirvānti)
- **savvadukkhāṇamaṃtaṃ kareṃti** — make an end of all suffering

### 5. COMMENTARY

This opening sutra establishes the authority and scope of the entire chapter. It begins with the traditional transmission formula *suyaṃ me āusaṃ* (thus I have heard), indicating that Sudharmā Svāmī is relating Mahāvīra's teaching to Jambū Svāmī. The chapter's name — *Samyak Parākrama* (Right Effort) — indicates it covers the complete spectrum of spiritual striving. The sutra lists ten stages of engagement with the teaching: believing, trusting, being illuminated, experiencing directly, guarding, crossing beyond, reflecting, purifying, accomplishing, and following the teacher's instructions. These ten represent a complete arc from initial intellectual acceptance to final realization. The chapter then systematically covers 73 practices (anuṣṭhāna) that together constitute this right effort. The ultimate fruit is explicitly stated: perfection, enlightenment, liberation, nirvāṇa, and the end of all suffering.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: This opening verse says that Lord Mahāvīra taught the path of "Right Effort" — and those who truly believe in it, practice it at every level, and follow through completely can achieve total freedom from suffering.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Do I engage with spiritual teachings at all ten levels — from initial belief through to complete accomplishment — or do I stop at intellectual acceptance?

## SUTRA 29.2

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

संवेगेणं भंते ! जीवे किं जणयइ ?
संवेगेणं अणुत्तरं धम्मसद्धं जणयइ, अणुत्तराए धम्मसद्धाए
संवेगं हव्वमागच्छइ ॥२९.२॥

⚠️ Prakrit reconstructed from शब्दार्थ and Gujarati commentary.

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Saṃvegeṇaṃ bhaṃte! Jīve kiṃ jaṇayai?
Saṃvegeṇaṃ aṇuttaraṃ dhammasaddhaṃ jaṇayai, aṇuttarāe dhammasaddhāe
saṃvegaṃ havvamāgacchai || 29.2 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Through saṃvega (spiritual urgency), O Lord, what does the soul generate? Through saṃvega, it generates supreme faith in dharma; and through supreme faith in dharma, saṃvega itself further deepens.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **saṃvegeṇaṃ** — through saṃvega (spiritual urgency / awakening)
- **bhaṃte** — O Lord / O venerable one
- **jīve** — the soul
- **kiṃ** — what
- **jaṇayai** — generates / produces (janayati)
- **aṇuttaraṃ** — supreme / unsurpassed (anuttara)
- **dhammasaddhaṃ** — faith in dharma (dharma-śraddhā)
- **aṇuttarāe dhammasaddhāe** — through supreme faith in dharma
- **havvamāgacchai** — comes into being / arises (bhavyam āgacchati)

### 5. COMMENTARY

This sutra begins the systematic Q&A that structures the entire chapter. The first question is foundational: what does spiritual urgency (saṃvega) produce? The answer reveals a reciprocal relationship: saṃvega generates supreme dharma-śraddhā (faith in dharma), and dharma-śraddhā in turn deepens saṃvega. This is not a linear progression but a self-reinforcing cycle — the more urgently one seeks truth, the deeper one's faith becomes; and the deeper one's faith, the more urgently one strives. Saṃvega here refers to the initial spiritual awakening — the deep realization that worldly existence is unsatisfactory and that liberation is both necessary and possible. It is the spark that ignites the entire spiritual journey.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: Spiritual urgency (the deep feeling that you must find the truth) creates genuine faith in the spiritual path — and that faith makes the urgency even stronger. They fuel each other in an upward spiral.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Do I feel genuine spiritual urgency in my life, or have I become comfortable with the way things are?

## SUTRA 29.3

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

णिव्वेएणं भंते ! जीवे किं जणयइ ?
णिव्वेएणं जीवे संसारे विरत्तिं जणयइ,
विरत्तीए णिव्वेयं हव्वमागच्छइ ॥२९.३॥

⚠️ Prakrit reconstructed from Gujarati commentary on nirveda.

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Ṇivveēṇaṃ bhaṃte! Jīve kiṃ jaṇayai?
Ṇivveēṇaṃ jīve saṃsāre viratti jaṇayai,
virattīe ṇivveyaṃ havvamāgacchai || 29.3 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Through nirveda (disillusionment), O Lord, what does the soul generate? Through nirveda, the soul generates detachment from worldly existence; and through detachment, nirveda itself deepens.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **ṇivveēṇaṃ** — through nirveda (disillusionment / spiritual weariness)
- **bhaṃte** — O Lord
- **jīve** — the soul
- **kiṃ jaṇayai** — what does it generate
- **saṃsāre** — in worldly existence (saṃsāra)
- **virattiṃ** — detachment / dispassion (virati)
- **virattīe** — through detachment
- **ṇivveyaṃ** — nirveda
- **havvamāgacchai** — arises / comes into being

### 5. COMMENTARY

Nirveda (spiritual disillusionment) is the deep weariness with worldly existence that arises when one truly sees the futility of saṃsāric pleasures. This sutra reveals another reciprocal pair: nirveda generates virati (detachment/dispassion) from saṃsāra, and virati in turn deepens nirveda. When one becomes disillusioned with the world, natural detachment follows — one stops clinging to pleasures that are seen as hollow. And as detachment grows, the vision of worldly futility becomes even clearer. This pair works alongside the saṃvega-dharmaśraddhā pair from the previous sutra. Together they form the motivational foundation of the spiritual path: urgency and faith pull one forward, while disillusionment and detachment push one away from bondage.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: When you truly see that worldly pleasures are empty, you naturally become detached — and that detachment makes you see even more clearly. Disillusionment and letting go strengthen each other.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Have I experienced genuine disillusionment with worldly pursuits, or do I still believe the next achievement or pleasure will finally satisfy me?

## SUTRA 29.4

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

पिण्व्वेपणेणं भंते ! जीवे किं जणयइ ?
पिण्व्वेपणेणं विसुद्धं आहारं जणयइ,
विसुद्धाहारेणं पिण्व्वेपणं भवइ ॥२९.४॥

⚠️ Prakrit heavily reconstructed from Gujarati commentary; exact wording uncertain.

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Piṇḍvepaṇeṇaṃ bhaṃte! Jīve kiṃ jaṇayai?
Piṇḍvepaṇeṇaṃ visuddhaṃ āhāraṃ jaṇayai,
visuddhāhāreṇaṃ piṇḍvepaṇaṃ bhavai || 29.4 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Through piṇḍa-viśodhana (purification of food/alms), O Lord, what does the soul generate? Through food-purification, it generates pure sustenance; and through pure sustenance, the practice of food-purification is maintained.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **piṇḍvepaṇeṇaṃ** — through piṇḍa-viśodhana (purification/examination of alms)
- **bhaṃte** — O Lord
- **jīve** — the soul
- **kiṃ jaṇayai** — what does it generate
- **visuddhaṃ** — pure / purified (viśuddha)
- **āhāraṃ** — food / sustenance (āhāra)
- **visuddhāhāreṇaṃ** — through pure sustenance
- **piṇḍvepaṇaṃ** — food-purification practice
- **bhavai** — becomes / is established

### 5. COMMENTARY

Piṇḍa-viśodhana (examination and purification of food) is a foundational monastic discipline. Before accepting alms, the monk carefully examines the food to ensure it meets all spiritual criteria — that no living beings were harmed in its preparation, that it was not specially prepared for the monk, and that it is free from faults. This discipline generates *viśuddha āhāra* (pure sustenance) — food that nourishes the body without creating karmic bondage. The reciprocal relationship is significant: the practice of careful examination ensures pure food, and the habit of accepting only pure food reinforces the discipline of examination. This sutra establishes that even the most basic bodily need — eating — must be spiritually regulated from the very beginning of the path.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: A monk must carefully examine food before accepting it, ensuring no spiritual rules are broken. This discipline creates a cycle where careful examination leads to pure food, and the habit of pure eating reinforces the discipline.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

How mindful am I about what I consume — not just food, but information, entertainment, and experiences? Do I examine before accepting?

## SUTRA 29.5

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

धम्मसद्धाए णं भंते ! जीवे किं जणयइ ?
धम्मसद्धाए अणुत्तराए जीवे विरज्जइ, अणगारिए णं
जीवे अत्थेगइइ ॥२९.५॥

⚠️ Prakrit reconstructed from शब्दार्थ and Gujarati commentary.

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Dhammasaddhāe ṇaṃ bhaṃte! Jīve kiṃ jaṇayai?
Dhammasaddhāe aṇuttarāe jīve virajjai, aṇagārie ṇaṃ
jīve atthegaii || 29.5 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Through dharma-śraddhā (faith in dharma), O Lord, what does the soul generate? Through supreme faith in dharma, the soul becomes detached; as a homeless renunciant, the soul progresses further.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **dhammasaddhāe** — through dharma-śraddhā (faith in dharma)
- **ṇaṃ** — indeed
- **bhaṃte** — O Lord
- **jīve** — the soul
- **kiṃ jaṇayai** — what does it generate
- **aṇuttarāe** — supreme / unsurpassed
- **virajjai** — becomes detached / renounces (virajyate)
- **aṇagārie** — as a homeless one / monk (anagārika)
- **atthegaii** — progresses / advances (arthagati)

### 5. COMMENTARY

This sutra marks a crucial transition: supreme faith in dharma leads the soul to renounce worldly life entirely. When dharma-śraddhā reaches its full maturity, the practitioner sees that householder life, however virtuous, cannot lead to liberation. The soul then takes the decisive step of becoming an *anagārika* (homeless renunciant / monk). But renunciation is not the end — it is a beginning. The sutra states that the soul *atthegaii* (progresses further), meaning monastic life is itself a platform for deeper spiritual advancement. The commentary emphasizes that this is not mere external renunciation of possessions but an internal shift where the soul genuinely detaches from worldly identity. Without this inner detachment, even monastic robes are merely a costume.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: When faith in the spiritual path becomes deep enough, a person naturally renounces worldly life to become a monk — and from there, the real spiritual journey begins. Faith strong enough creates the courage to leave everything behind.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Is my faith in the spiritual path strong enough that I would change my entire life for it, or do I keep spirituality safely contained within my existing comfort zone?

## SUTRA 29.6

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

गुरुसाहम्मियसुस्सूसणयाए णं भंते ! जीवे किं जणयइ ?
गुरुसाहम्मियसुस्सूसणयाए जीवे सम्मं णाणं जणयइ,
णाणेणं गुरुसाहम्मियसुस्सूसणया भवइ ॥२९.६॥

⚠️ Prakrit reconstructed from शब्दार्थ and Gujarati commentary.

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Gurusāhammiyasussūsaṇayāe ṇaṃ bhaṃte! Jīve kiṃ jaṇayai?
Gurusāhammiyasussūsaṇayāe jīve sammaṃ ṇāṇaṃ jaṇayai,
ṇāṇeṇaṃ gurusāhammiyasussūsaṇayā bhavai || 29.6 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Through service to the guru and co-religionists, O Lord, what does the soul generate? Through such service, the soul generates right knowledge; and through right knowledge, service to guru and co-religionists is sustained.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **guru** — teacher / spiritual guide
- **sāhammiya** — sādharmika (co-religionists / fellow practitioners)
- **sussūsaṇayāe** — through śuśrūṣā (service / devoted attendance)
- **ṇaṃ** — indeed
- **bhaṃte** — O Lord
- **jīve** — the soul
- **kiṃ jaṇayai** — what does it generate
- **sammaṃ ṇāṇaṃ** — right knowledge (samyak jñāna)
- **ṇāṇeṇaṃ** — through knowledge

### 5. COMMENTARY

Service to the guru and fellow practitioners (*guru-sādharmika-śuśrūṣā*) is not mere servitude — it is a spiritual practice that generates *samyak jñāna* (right knowledge). By attending to the guru with humility and devotion, the disciple creates the conditions for knowledge transmission. The guru shares deeper teachings with one who serves sincerely, and fellow practitioners provide the community context in which knowledge is discussed, tested, and internalized. The reciprocal relationship is instructive: service generates knowledge, and knowledge in turn motivates deeper service. One who truly understands the value of the guru's teaching naturally wants to serve more. The commentary identifies ten categories of persons worthy of service, beginning with the ācārya and extending to the broader saṅgha.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: Serving your teacher and spiritual community with devotion is how you gain real knowledge — and the more you learn, the more you want to serve. Knowledge and humble service strengthen each other.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Do I serve my teachers and spiritual community with genuine devotion, or do I approach learning as a purely intellectual transaction?

## SUTRA 29.7

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

आलोयणयाए णं भंते ! जीवे किं जणयइ ?
आलोयणयाए जीवे पावकम्मस्स विसोहिं जणयइ,
पावकम्मविसोहीए आलोयणया भवइ ॥२९.७॥

⚠️ Prakrit reconstructed from शब्दार्थ and Gujarati commentary.

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Āloyaṇayāe ṇaṃ bhaṃte! Jīve kiṃ jaṇayai?
Āloyaṇayāe jīve pāvakammassa visohiṃ jaṇayai,
pāvakammavisohīe āloyaṇayā bhavai || 29.7 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Through ālocanā (confession), O Lord, what does the soul generate? Through confession, the soul generates purification of sinful karma; and through purification of sinful karma, the practice of confession is sustained.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **āloyaṇayāe** — through ālocanā (confession / self-disclosure)
- **ṇaṃ** — indeed
- **bhaṃte** — O Lord
- **jīve** — the soul
- **kiṃ jaṇayai** — what does it generate
- **pāvakammassa** — of sinful karma (pāpa-karma)
- **visohiṃ** — purification (viśodhana)
- **pāvakammavisohīe** — through purification of sinful karma
- **āloyaṇayā** — ālocanā (confession)
- **bhavai** — is established / continues

### 5. COMMENTARY

*Ālocanā* (confession) means openly disclosing one's faults and transgressions to the guru — not hiding, justifying, or minimizing them. This sutra reveals that confession generates *pāpa-karma-viśoddhi* (purification of sinful karma). The mechanism is profound: when a fault is concealed, it festers and compounds; when confessed honestly, it begins to dissolve. The act of confession requires courage and humility — one must overcome ego's resistance to admitting error. The reciprocal relationship shows that as karma is purified through confession, the practice of confession itself becomes easier and more natural. The soul that has experienced the lightness of confession becomes increasingly willing to confess, creating a virtuous cycle of ever-deepening purification.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: Honestly confessing your mistakes to your teacher purifies the karma those mistakes created — and once you experience how freeing confession is, you naturally become more willing to confess. Honesty purifies, and purification encourages more honesty.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

When I make a mistake, do I confess it openly, or do I hide it out of pride and fear of judgment?

## SUTRA 29.8

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

णिंदणयाए णं भंते ! जीवे किं जणयइ ?
णिंदणयाए जीवे अप्पदोसस्स णाणं जणयइ,
अप्पदोसणाणेणं णिंदणया भवइ ॥२९.८॥

⚠️ Prakrit reconstructed from Gujarati commentary on nindanā.

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Ṇiṃdaṇayāe ṇaṃ bhaṃte! Jīve kiṃ jaṇayai?
Ṇiṃdaṇayāe jīve appadosassa ṇāṇaṃ jaṇayai,
appadosaṇāṇeṇaṃ ṇiṃdaṇayā bhavai || 29.8 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Through nindanā (self-censure), O Lord, what does the soul generate? Through self-censure, the soul generates awareness of its own faults; and through awareness of faults, the practice of self-censure continues.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **ṇiṃdaṇayāe** — through nindanā (self-censure / self-criticism)
- **ṇaṃ** — indeed
- **bhaṃte** — O Lord
- **jīve** — the soul
- **kiṃ jaṇayai** — what does it generate
- **appadosassa** — of one's own faults (ātma-doṣa)
- **ṇāṇaṃ** — knowledge / awareness (jñāna)
- **appadosaṇāṇeṇaṃ** — through awareness of one's faults
- **ṇiṃdaṇayā** — self-censure
- **bhavai** — continues / is established

### 5. COMMENTARY

*Nindanā* (self-censure) differs from ālocanā (confession) in that it is internally directed. While ālocanā involves disclosing faults to the guru, nindanā is the practice of personally censuring oneself — recognizing and criticizing one's own shortcomings without external prompting. The sutra shows that this generates *ātma-doṣa-jñāna* (awareness of one's own faults). Self-censure sharpens the inner eye: the more one practices honest self-criticism, the more clearly one sees one's remaining imperfections. This is not self-punishment or negative self-talk — it is a disciplined spiritual practice of unflinching honesty about one's own spiritual condition. The reciprocal cycle is self-correcting: awareness of faults motivates censure, and censure deepens awareness.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: Criticizing your own faults honestly (not someone else's) builds the ability to see your own weaknesses clearly — and the clearer you see them, the more naturally you correct them. It's an inner honesty practice.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Am I willing to honestly criticize my own faults, or do I deflect by focusing on other people's shortcomings?

Simply put: Renouncing the four passions — anger, pride, deceit, and greed — at their root generates the dispassionate state that defines a Jina. This internal renunciation is the heart of the entire path; without it, all external renunciations are incomplete.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Which of the four passions — anger, pride, deceit, greed — has the strongest hold on me? Am I working to renounce it at its root, not just control its symptoms?

## SUTRA 29.39

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

जोगपच्चक्खाणेणं भंते ! जीवे किं जणयइ ?
जोगपच्चक्खाणेणं अजोगिट्ठिइं जणयइ ॥२९.३९॥

⚠️ Prakrit partially reconstructed from शब्दार्थ and Gujarati commentary.

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Jogapaccakkhāṇeṇaṃ bhaṃte! Jīve kiṃ jaṇayai?
Jogapaccakkhāṇeṇaṃ ajogiṭṭhiiṃ jaṇayai || 29.39 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Through yoga-pratyākhyāna (cessation of all activity), O Lord, what does the soul generate? Through yoga-pratyākhyāna, one generates ajogi-sthiti (the state of activity-cessation / non-vibration).

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **jogapaccakkhāṇeṇaṃ** — through yoga-pratyākhyāna (renunciation of all yoga/activity — body, speech, mind)
- **bhaṃte** — O Lord
- **jīve** — the soul
- **kiṃ jaṇayai** — what does it generate
- **ajogiṭṭhiiṃ** — ajogi-sthiti (the state of complete activity-cessation / non-vibration)
- **jaṇayai** — generates

### 5. COMMENTARY

*Yoga* in Jain philosophy refers to the vibrations of body, speech, and mind — the three channels through which karma enters the soul. *Yoga-pratyākhyāna* is the ultimate cessation: stopping ALL vibrations of body, speech, and mind. The fruit is *ajogi-sthiti* — the state of complete non-vibration. The commentary explains that this occurs in the final moments before liberation: the soul, having shed all karma-binding activities, ceases all vibration. In this state, no new karma can enter because the very mechanism of karmic influx (yoga) has been eliminated. This is the penultimate stage before complete liberation.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: Cessation of all activity — body, speech, and mind — generates the state of complete non-vibration. When all channels of karmic influx are shut down, the soul stands on the threshold of liberation.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Can I conceive of a state where all activity — even mental — ceases, and the soul simply is?

## SUTRA 29.40

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT

सरीरपच्चक्खाणेणं भंते ! जीवे किं जणयइ ?
सिद्धाइसयगुणसंपण्णे य णं जीवे लोगग्गमुवगए परमसुही भवइ ॥२९.४०॥

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION

Sarīrapaccakkhāṇeṇaṃ bhaṃte! Jīve kiṃ jaṇayai?
Siddhāisayaguṇasaṃpaṇṇe ya ṇaṃ jīve logaggamuvagae paramasuhī bhavai || 29.40 ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION

Through śarīra-pratyākhyāna (renunciation of the body), O Lord, what does the soul generate? Endowed with the extraordinary qualities of the Siddhas, the soul — having ascended to the top of the universe (lokāgra) — experiences supreme bliss (parama-sukha).

### 4. ANVAYARTHA

- **sarīrapaccakkhāṇeṇaṃ** — through śarīra-pratyākhyāna (renunciation of the body)
- **bhaṃte** — O Lord
- **jīve** — the soul
- **kiṃ jaṇayai** — what does it generate
- **siddhāisayaguṇasaṃpaṇṇe** — endowed with extraordinary qualities of the Siddhas (siddha-atiśaya-guṇa-saṃpanna)
- **ya ṇaṃ** — and indeed
- **logaggamuvagae** — having ascended to lokāgra (the summit of the universe)
- **paramasuhī** — supremely blissful (parama-sukhī)
- **bhavai** — becomes

### 5. COMMENTARY

*Śarīra-pratyākhyāna* is the final renunciation — giving up the body itself. This sutra describes the culmination of all previous practices: the soul, having renounced passions, activity, and now the body, becomes endowed with *siddha-atiśaya-guṇa* — the extraordinary qualities of the perfected beings. These include infinite knowledge (ananta-jñāna), infinite perception (ananta-darśana), infinite bliss (ananta-sukha), and infinite energy (ananta-vīrya). The liberated soul ascends to *lokāgra* — the summit of the universe — and abides in supreme bliss for all eternity. This sutra marks the ultimate fruit of samyak-parākrama (right effort): the complete liberation that the entire chapter has been building toward, practice by practice.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT

Simply put: When the soul finally renounces the body itself, it becomes endowed with the infinite qualities of the Siddhas — infinite knowledge, perception, bliss, and energy — and ascends to the top of the universe to dwell in eternal supreme bliss. This is the ultimate destination of right effort.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE

Can I hold the vision of this ultimate destination — infinite knowledge and eternal bliss at the summit of existence — as the guiding star for every practice on the path?
