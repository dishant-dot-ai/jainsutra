## SUTRA [9.2]

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT
चम्पा णयरी। पुण्णभद्दे उज्जाणं। पुण्णभद्दो जक्खो। दत्ते राया। रत्तवई देवी। महचंदे कुमारे। सिरीकंतापामोक्खाणं पंचसयाणं रायवरकण्णगाणं पाणिग्गहणं। तित्थयरागमणं। पुव्वभव पुच्छा। तिगिच्छिया णयरी। जिवसत्तू राया। धम्मवीरिए अणगारे पडिलाभिए जाव सिद्धे। णिक्खेवो जहा पढमस्स। ।। णवमं अज्झयणं समत्तं ।।

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION
campā ṇayarī | puṇṇabhadde ujjāṇaṃ | puṇṇabhaddo jakkho | datte rāyā | rattavaī devī | mahacaṃde kumāre | sirīkaṃtāpāmokkhāṇaṃ paṃcasayāṇaṃ rāyavarakaṇṇagāṇaṃ pāṇiggahaṇaṃ | titthayarāgamaṇaṃ | puvvabhava pucchā | tigicchiyā ṇayarī | jivasattū rāyā | dhammavīrie aṇagāre paḍilābhie jāva siddhe | ṇikkhevo jahā paḍhamassa | || ṇavamaṃ ajjhayaṇaṃ samattaṃ ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION
"In the city of Champa. The Punnabhadda garden. The guardian spirit was Punnabhaddo. The king was Datta. The queen was Rattavai Devi. Prince Mahachanda. He married Sirikanta, chief among five hundred princesses. The Tirthankara arrived. The question of previous births was asked. In the city of Tigicchiya. King Jivasattu. The monk Dhammavirie was encountered, leading all the way to liberation. The closing is as in the first chapter. The ninth chapter is complete."

### 4. ANVAYARTHA
**चम्पा णयरी** = in the city of Champa; **पुण्णभद्दे उज्जाणं** = the Punnabhadda garden; **पुण्णभद्दो जक्खो** = the guardian spirit Punnabhaddo; **दत्ते राया** = King Datta; **रत्तवई देवी** = Queen Rattavai Devi; **महचंदे कुमारे** = Prince Mahachanda (Mahachandrakumar); **सिरीकंतापामोक्खाणं** = Sirikanta, chief among; **पंचसयाणं रायवरकण्णगाणं** = five hundred princesses; **पाणिग्गहणं** = marriage; **तित्थयरागमणं** = the arrival of the Tirthankara; **पुव्वभव पुच्छा** = the question about previous births; **तिगिच्छिया णयरी** = in the city of Tigicchiya (Chikirtsika); **जिवसत्तू राया** = King Jivasattu; **धम्मवीरिए अणगारे** = the monk Dhammavirie (Dharmavirya); **पडिलाभिए** = was encountered / attained; **जाव सिद्धे** = all the way to liberation; **णिक्खेवो जहा पढमस्स** = the closing is as in the first (chapter); **णवमं अज्झयणं समत्तं** = the ninth chapter is complete

### 5. COMMENTARY
This sutra tells the story of Prince Mahachandrakumar in the well-known city of Champa. The garden was called Punnabhadda, and the guardian spirit shared the same name — Punnabhaddo. King Datta ruled the city, and his queen was Rattavai Devi. Their son, Prince Mahachanda (Mahachandrakumar), was born with the auspicious signs of accumulated merit. He married the princess Sirikanta, who was chief among five hundred royal maidens. When Lord Mahavira arrived in Champa, the prince sought his teachings and asked about his previous births. The Lord revealed that in a former life, Mahachandrakumar had lived in the city of Tigicchiya (Chikirtsika), where King Jivasattu ruled. In that life, he had encountered a monk named Dhammavirie (Dharmavirya), heard the teachings of truth, and practiced renunciation and moral conduct. Through the merit of those past actions, he was born into royal fortune and, following the same spiritual path as all the previous princes in these chapters, was assured of eventual liberation. The closing follows the same pattern as Chapter 1, confirming that Mahachandrakumar will make an end of all suffering.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT
Prince Mahachandrakumar of Champa city was born to King Datta and Queen Rattavai. In his past life in Tigicchiya city, he had met the monk Dharmavirya and practiced righteousness. He followed Lord Mahavira's path and was assured of eventual liberation.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE
Champa was one of the most prominent cities of its era. Does spiritual progress require withdrawing from the world, or can it begin right where you are — even in the busiest of places?
