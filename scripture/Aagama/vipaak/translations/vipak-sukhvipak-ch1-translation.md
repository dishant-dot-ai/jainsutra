# Sukha Vipaak Sutra - Chapter 1: Subahukumar (સુબાહુકુમાર)

**Source:** Vipaak Sutra, Second Shrutaskandha (Sukha Vipaak), Chapter 1
**Subject:** The story of Prince Subahukumar and the fruits of good karma
**Total Sutras:** 21
**Form:** Narrative prose (Ardhamagadhi Prakrit)
**Narrator:** Sudharmaswami to Jambukumar

---

## SUTRA [1.1]

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT
तेणं कालेणं तेणं समएणं रायगिहे णयरे । गुणसिलए चेइए । सुहम्मे समोसढे। जंबू जाव पज्जुवासमाणे ।

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION
teṇaṃ kāleṇaṃ teṇaṃ samaeṇaṃ rāyagihe ṇayare | guṇasilae ceie | suhamme samosaḍhe | jambū jāva pajjuvāsamāṇe |

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION
At that time, in that period, in the city of Rajagriha, at the Gunashilak temple, Sudharmaswami was seated. Jambukumar was serving him with devoted reverence.

⚠️ The term "jāva" (जाव) here abbreviates the standard formulaic description of Jambukumar's reverential service, which in the expanded form includes sitting nearby, listening attentively, and attending with folded hands.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA
**तेणं कालेणं** = that time; **तेणं समएणं** = that period; **रायगिहे णयरे** = in the city of Rajagriha; **गुणसिलए चेइए** = at the Gunashilak temple; **सुहम्मे** = Sudharmaswami; **समोसढे** = was seated (descended/arrived); **जंबू** = Jambukumar; **जाव** = up to (abbreviation for the full description); **पज्जुवासमाणे** = serving with reverence

### 5. COMMENTARY
This opening sutra establishes the standard setting for the narration found across many scriptures in the tradition. The city of Rajagriha was a major center of spiritual learning in ancient India. The Gunashilak temple served as a gathering place where the chief disciple would teach the assembled monks. Sudharmaswami, the fifth chief disciple in the lineage, was the last to have direct knowledge of the teacher's words. Jambukumar, his foremost student, received these teachings in a posture of humble service. This setting frames the entire narrative that follows.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT
The story begins at a temple in Rajagriha where a great monk named Sudharmaswami is teaching his student Jambukumar.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE
What does it mean to truly serve a teacher — is it about physical service or the quality of attention we bring to their words?

## SUTRA [1.2]

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT
जइ णं भंते ! समणेणं भगवया महावीरेणं जाव संपत्तेणं सुहविवागाणं भंते ! सूतस्कंध पण्णत्ते ? - जइ णं भंते ! समणेणं भगवया महावीरेणं सुहविवागाणं भंते ! अध्ययनाइं पण्णत्ताइं ? - हंता जंबू !

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION
jai ṇaṃ bhaṃte ! samaṇeṇaṃ bhagavayā mahāvīreṇaṃ jāva saṃpatteṇaṃ suhavivāgāṇaṃ bhaṃte ! sūtaskaṃdha paṇṇatte ? — jai ṇaṃ bhaṃte ! samaṇeṇaṃ bhagavayā mahāvīreṇaṃ suhavivāgāṇaṃ bhaṃte ! ajjhayaṇāiṃ paṇṇattāiṃ ? — haṃtā jambū !

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION
"O Revered One! Has the blessed ascetic Mahavira, the accomplished one, established the section of scripture on happy fruition?" — "O Revered One! Has the blessed ascetic Mahavira established the chapters on happy fruition?" — "Yes, Jambu!"

The ten chapters are: (1) Subahu, (2) Bhadranandi, (3) Sujat, (4) Suvasav, (5) Jindas, (6) Dhanpati, (7) Mahabal, (8) Bhadranandi, (9) Mahachandrakumar, (10) Vardatta.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA
**जइ णं भंते** = if indeed, O Revered One; **समणेणं भगवया महावीरेणं** = by the ascetic, the blessed Mahavira; **जाव संपत्तेणं** = up to the accomplished one (abbreviated titles); **सुहविवागाणं** = of the happy fruition (Sukha Vipaak); **सूतस्कंध पण्णत्ते** = section of scripture established; **अध्ययनाइं पण्णत्ताइं** = chapters established; **हंता जंबू** = Yes, Jambu

### 5. COMMENTARY
This sutra records the formal question-and-answer exchange that introduces the entire second section of the scripture. The student asks the teacher whether the great teacher established this section, and the teacher affirms it. The ten chapters are then enumerated by name, each telling the story of a different individual whose good deeds bore sweet fruit. This second section on happy fruition serves as the counterpart to the first section on sorrowful fruition, together illustrating how actions inevitably produce corresponding results. The structure of ten stories in each section creates a balanced framework showing both sides of the moral law.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT
Jambukumar asks if Mahavira taught about the good things that happen when people do good deeds, and the answer is yes — there are ten such stories.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE
If we truly believed every good action would bear fruit, how differently would we live each day?

## SUTRA [1.3]

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT
एवं खलु जंबू ! तेणं कालेणं तेणं समएणं हत्थिसीसे णामं णयरे होत्था । रिद्धत्थिमियसमिद्धे, वण्णओ । तत्थ णं हत्थिसीसस्स णयरस्स बहिया उत्तर-पुरत्थिमे दिसीभाए एत्थ णं पुप्फकरंडए णामं उज्जाणे होत्था, सव्वोउय-पुप्फ फल समिद्धे, वण्णओ । तत्थ णं कयवणमालपियस्स जक्खस्स जक्खाययणे होत्था । तत्थ णं हत्थिसीसे णयरे अदीणसत्तू णामं राया होत्था, महया हिमवंत जाव रज्जं पसासेमाणे विहरइ । तस्स णं अदीणसत्तुस्स रण्णो धारिणीपामोक्खाणं पंचसयाणं देवीसहस्सं ओरोहे याविहोत्था ।

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION
evaṃ khalu jambū ! teṇaṃ kāleṇaṃ teṇaṃ samaeṇaṃ hatthisīse ṇāmaṃ ṇayare hotthā | riddhatthi-miya-samiddhe, vaṇṇao | tattha ṇaṃ hatthisīsassa ṇayarassa bahiyā uttara-puratthime disībhāe ettha ṇaṃ pupphakaraṃḍae ṇāmaṃ ujjāṇe hotthā, savvoūya-pupphaPhala samiddhe, vaṇṇao | tattha ṇaṃ kayavaṇamālapiyassa jakkhassa jakkhāyayaṇe hotthā | tattha ṇaṃ hatthisīse ṇayare adīṇasattū ṇāmaṃ rāyā hotthā, mahayā himavaṃta jāva rajjaṃ pasāsemāṇe viharai | tassa ṇaṃ adīṇasatthussa raṇṇo dhāriṇī-pāmokkhaṇaṃ paṃcasayāṇaṃ devīsahasssaṃ orohe yāvi hotthā |

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION
"Thus indeed, Jambu! At that time, in that period, there was a city named Hastisirsha (Elephant-Head City). It was prosperous, wealthy, and well-provided — described in full. To the northeast of Hastisirsha city, there was a garden named Pushpakarandak (Flower Basket Garden), abundant with all seasonal flowers and fruits — described in full. There was the shrine of the guardian spirit named Katavanmalapiya. In that city of Hastisirsha, there was a king named Adinashattru (Ajatashatru). He ruled his kingdom like the great Himalaya mountain. He had a chief queen named Dharini, and a royal household of five hundred queens."

⚠️ "vaṇṇao" (वण्णओ) indicates a standard formulaic description that is abbreviated, meaning "described in the customary manner."

### 4. ANVAYARTHA
**एवं खलु जंबू** = thus indeed, O Jambu; **हत्थिसीसे णामं णयरे** = a city named Hastisirsha; **रिद्धत्थिमियसमिद्धे** = prosperous and wealthy; **वण्णओ** = described (formulaic expansion); **बहिया उत्तर-पुरत्थिमे दिसीभाए** = outside, to the northeast direction; **पुप्फकरंडए** = Pushpakarandak; **उज्जाणे** = garden; **सव्वोउय-पुप्फ फल समिद्धे** = abundant with all seasonal flowers and fruits; **कयवणमालपियस्स जक्खस्स** = of the guardian spirit Katavanmalapiya; **जक्खाययणे** = shrine; **अदीणसत्तू** = Adinashattru (King Ajatashatru); **महया हिमवंत जाव** = like the great Himalaya, up to (abbreviated); **रज्जं पसासेमाणे** = governing the kingdom; **धारिणी-पामोक्खाणं** = with Dharini as chief; **पंचसयाणं देवीसहस्सं** = five hundred queens

### 5. COMMENTARY
This sutra paints the setting of the story by describing the city, its surroundings, and its royal household. The city is identified as Hastisirsha, historically associated with Hastinapur in northern India. The garden to the northeast and the guardian spirit's shrine establish the sacred geography of the narrative. King Adinashattru, known in other traditions as Ajatashatru, was a historical ruler. His comparison to the Himalaya mountain suggests stability, majesty, and unshakeable authority. The mention of Queen Dharini as chief among five hundred queens follows the standard literary convention for describing royal households in these scriptures.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT
The story takes place in a grand city called Hastisirsha, ruled by a powerful king with his chief queen Dharini and a large royal household.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE
Does the grandeur of a kingdom have any lasting value, or is it merely the stage upon which the soul's real drama unfolds?

## SUTRA [1.4]

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT
तए णं सा धारिणी देवी अण्णया कयाइ तंसि तारिसगंसि सीहं सुमिणे पासइ जहा मेहस्स जम्मणं तहा भाणियव्वं जाव सुबाहुकुमारे...

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION
tae ṇaṃ sā dhāriṇī devī aṇṇayā kayāi taṃsi tārisagaṃsi sīhaṃ sumiṇe pāsai jahā mehassa jammaṇaṃ tahā bhāṇiyavvaṃ jāva subāhukumāre...

⚠️ "jāva" abbreviates the entire birth narrative, referring back to the parallel account of Meghkumar's birth in the first section.

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION
Then Queen Dharini, on a certain occasion, saw a lion among the sixteen great dreams. Just as the birth of Meghkumar has been described, so too should the birth of Subahukumar be narrated. He was given the name Subahu. He grew up with great fortune and possessed thirty-two auspicious physical marks.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA
**तए णं** = then; **सा धारिणी देवी** = that Queen Dharini; **अण्णया कयाइ** = on a certain occasion; **तंसि तारिसगंसि** = in such a (night); **सीहं सुमिणे पासइ** = sees a lion in a dream; **जहा मेहस्स जम्मणं** = just as the birth of Meghkumar; **तहा भाणियव्वं** = so it should be narrated; **जाव सुबाहुकुमारे** = up to Subahukumar (abbreviated birth description)

### 5. COMMENTARY
The sixteen great dreams seen by the mother are a standard sign in this tradition that a great soul is about to be born. One of these dreams is the vision of a lion, symbolizing courage, sovereignty, and spiritual strength. The text uses a cross-reference technique common in the scriptural canon: rather than repeating the full birth narrative, it directs the reader to the parallel account in the first section. This indicates that Subahukumar's birth was accompanied by the same celestial celebrations and auspicious events. The thirty-two marks on his body signify accumulated spiritual merit from past lives. His very name, meaning "one with beautiful arms," reflects his physical perfection.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT
Queen Dharini had prophetic dreams before her son Subahukumar was born, signs that a spiritually great soul was entering the world.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE
What signs in your own life might indicate that something meaningful is about to unfold, if only you paid closer attention?

## SUTRA [1.5]

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT
तेणं कालेणं तेणं समएणं धम्मघोसाणं भगवओ अंतिए पंचाणुव्वयाइं सत्तसिक्खावयाइं दुवालसविहं गिहिधम्मं गिहिधम्मं पडिवज्जिऊण...

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION
teṇaṃ kāleṇaṃ teṇaṃ samaeṇaṃ dhammaghosāṇaṃ bhagavao aṃtie paṃcāṇuvvayāiṃ sattasikkhāvayāiṃ duvālasavihaṃ gihidhammaṃ gihidhammaṃ paḍivajjiūṇa...

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION
At that time, in that period, from the blessed Lord Dharmaghosha, King Adinashattru accepted the twelve-fold householder's religion — including the five minor vows and the seven supplementary practices. He undertook the householder's religious discipline.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA
**तेणं कालेणं तेणं समएणं** = at that time, in that period; **धम्मघोसाणं भगवओ अंतिए** = in the presence of Lord Dharmaghosha; **पंचाणुव्वयाइं** = the five minor vows; **सत्तसिक्खावयाइं** = the seven supplementary practices; **दुवालसविहं** = twelve-fold; **गिहिधम्मं** = householder's religion; **पडिवज्जिऊण** = having accepted/undertaken

### 5. COMMENTARY
This sutra describes the king accepting the formal code of conduct prescribed for lay followers. The twelve-fold householder's discipline consists of five minor vows and seven supplementary practices. The five vows are: non-violence in limited form, truthfulness, non-stealing, celibacy within marriage, and limiting possessions. The seven additional practices include directional limits on travel, limiting consumable and non-consumable goods, avoiding unnecessary harm, periodic meditation, periodic fasting, sharing with monks and the needy, and charitable giving. Lord Dharmaghosha here refers to the teacher who administered these vows. This establishes that even the royal household was oriented toward spiritual practice.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT
The king formally committed to twelve rules of ethical living — five vows of restraint and seven additional spiritual practices — under the guidance of a spiritual teacher.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE
Could limiting your possessions and pleasures actually increase your sense of inner freedom?

## SUTRA [1.6]

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT
तए णं से सुबाहुकुमारे समणस्स भगवओ महावीरस्स अंतिए धम्मं सोच्चा णिसम्म...

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION
tae ṇaṃ se subāhukumāre samaṇassa bhagavao mahāvīrassa aṃtie dhammaṃ soccā ṇisamm...

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION
Then Prince Subahukumar, having heard and carefully reflected upon the teachings in the presence of the blessed ascetic Mahavira, accepted the path with faith and began practicing spiritual observances.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA
**तए णं** = then; **से सुबाहुकुमारे** = that Prince Subahukumar; **समणस्स भगवओ महावीरस्स अंतिए** = in the presence of the blessed ascetic Mahavira; **धम्मं** = the teachings (dharma); **सोच्चा** = having heard; **णिसम्म** = having reflected upon / having considered carefully

### 5. COMMENTARY
This sutra marks the pivotal encounter between Prince Subahukumar and the great teacher. The two verbs used — "having heard" and "having reflected" — are significant. They indicate that spiritual transformation requires not just passive hearing but active contemplation. The prince did not merely listen; he internalized the teachings through careful thought. This encounter planted the seed that would eventually lead him to renounce his royal life. The sutra implies that the teachings resonated with the prince's soul because of the spiritual merit he had accumulated in his previous life as the generous householder.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT
Prince Subahukumar listened to Mahavira's teachings, thought deeply about them, and decided to follow the spiritual path.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE
When was the last time you not only heard wise words but actually sat with them long enough to let them change you?

## SUTRA [1.7]

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT
तेणं कालेणं तेणं समएणं सुबाहुकुमारे इहेव जंबूद्दीवे दीवे भारहेवासे विजये... सुबाहुणा कुमारेणं इमा एयारूवा माणुसिड्ढी लद्धा पत्ता अभिसमण्णागया ?

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION
teṇaṃ kāleṇaṃ teṇaṃ samaeṇaṃ subāhukumāre iheva jambūddīve dīve bhārahevāse vijaye... subāhuṇā kumāreṇaṃ imā eyārūvā māṇusiḍḍhī laddhā pattā abhisamaṇṇāgayā ?

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION
"At that time, in that period, in this very island of Jambudvipa, in the Bharata region of the continent — has Prince Subahukumar obtained, attained, and reached such great human prosperity as this?"

⚠️ "jāva" abbreviation is implied in the ellipsis, covering the standard description of the Bharata region.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA
**तेणं कालेणं तेणं समएणं** = at that time, in that period; **सुबाहुकुमारे** = Prince Subahukumar; **इहेव** = right here; **जंबूद्दीवे दीवे** = in the island of Jambudvipa; **भारहेवासे विजये** = in the Bharata region; **इमा एयारूवा** = this kind of; **माणुसिड्ढी** = human prosperity; **लद्धा पत्ता अभिसमण्णागया** = obtained, attained, and fully reached

### 5. COMMENTARY
This sutra poses the key question of the chapter: how did Subahukumar come to enjoy such extraordinary human prosperity? The question is structured as a teaching device, prompting the listener to seek the cause behind the prince's good fortune. The geographical context — the island continent and the Bharata region — places the story within the traditional cosmological framework. The three synonyms for "obtained" emphasize the completeness of his attainment. This question serves as the bridge from describing Subahukumar's current life to revealing his past life, which explains the karmic cause of his present happiness.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT
The question is asked: what did Subahukumar do in a past life to deserve such incredible good fortune in this one?

### 7. CONTEMPLATE
If someone asked what you had done to deserve your current circumstances, what would the honest answer be?

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT
एवं खलु गोयमा ! तेणं कालेणं तेणं समएणं द्वारवईसे हत्थिणाउरे णयरे सुमुहे णामं गाहावइ होत्था...

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION
evaṃ khalu goyamā ! teṇaṃ kāleṇaṃ teṇaṃ samaeṇaṃ dvāravīse hatthiṇāure ṇayare sumuhe ṇāmaṃ gāhāvai hotthā...

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION
"Thus indeed, O Gautama! At that time, in that period, in the city of Dvaravati (in the Hasthinapur region), there was a householder named Sumukha. He was wealthy, engaged in honest commerce, and earned his livelihood through righteous means."

### 4. ANVAYARTHA
**एवं खलु गोयमा** = thus indeed, O Gautama (addressing the listener); **तेणं कालेणं तेणं समएणं** = at that time, in that period; **द्वारवईसे** = in Dvaravati (island/region); **हत्थिणाउरे णयरे** = in the city of Hasthinapur; **सुमुहे णामं** = named Sumukha; **गाहावइ** = householder; **होत्था** = there was

### 5. COMMENTARY
The narrative now shifts to the past life that explains Subahukumar's present fortune. In the city of Dvaravati, there lived a householder named Sumukha. The name Sumukha means "one with a beautiful face," perhaps reflecting his inner goodness. As a householder, he belonged to the merchant class and earned his wealth through honest trade. This is significant because the tradition teaches that wealth earned through righteous means, when used for charitable purposes, generates powerful positive merit. The story establishes that Sumukha was not an ascetic but an ordinary family man whose extraordinary generosity earned him a princely rebirth.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT
In his previous life, Subahukumar was an honest and wealthy merchant named Sumukha who lived in the city of Dvaravati.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE
Does it matter more how you earn your wealth or how you spend it — or are both equally important?

Beyond accepting vows, Sumukha actively practiced austerity. The word for patient endurance here is significant — it suggests he did not merely follow rules but embraced hardship willingly. Austerity in this context includes fasting, limiting food, limiting sleep, and various forms of physical restraint. These were not performed as punishment but as training for the mind and body.
