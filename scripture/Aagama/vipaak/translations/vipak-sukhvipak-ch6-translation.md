# Sukha Vipaak Sutra - Chapter 6: Dhanpati (ધનપતિ)

**Source:** Vipaak Sutra, Second Shrutaskandha (Sukha Vipaak), Chapter 6
**Subject:** The story of Dhanpati Kumar and the fruits of good karma
**Total Sutras:** 2
**Form:** Narrative prose (Ardhamagadhi Prakrit)
**Narrator:** Sudharmaswami to Jambukumar
**Theme:** Dhanpati, son of Prince Vaishramana in the city of Kanagpura, follows the spiritual path after encountering Lord Mahavira's teachings. His present good fortune is the direct result of generosity and virtue practiced in a previous life.

## SUTRA [6.2]

### 1. PRAKRIT TEXT
कणगपुरं णयरं। सेयासोये उज्जाणं। वीरभद्दो जक्खो। पियचंदो राया। सुभद्दा देवी। वेसमणे कुमारे जुवराया। सिरीदेवी पामोक्खाणं पंचसयाणं रायवरकण्णगाणं पाणिग्गहणं। तित्थयरागमणं। जिणदासो पुव्वभवो। मणिपुरं णयरे। मेहरहो राया। संभूतिविजए अणगारे पडिलाभिए जाव सिद्धे। णिक्खेवो जहा पढमस्स। ।। छट्ठं अज्झयणं समत्तं ।।

### 2. ROMAN TRANSLITERATION
kaṇagapuraṃ ṇayaraṃ | seyāsoye ujjāṇaṃ | vīrabhaddo jakkho | piyacaṃdo rāyā | subhaddā devī | vesamaṇe kumāre juvarāyā | sirīdevī pāmokkhaṇaṃ paṃcasayāṇaṃ rāyavarakaṇṇagāṇaṃ pāṇiggahaṇaṃ | titthayarāgamaṇaṃ | jiṇadāso puvvabhavo | maṇipuraṃ ṇayare | meharaho rāyā | saṃbhūtivijae aṇagāre paḍilābhie jāva siddhe | ṇikkhevo jahā paḍhamassa || chaṭṭhaṃ ajjhayaṇaṃ samattaṃ ||

### 3. LITERAL ENGLISH TRANSLATION
In the city of Kanagpura (City of Gold), there was a garden called Setashoka (Setasoy). The guardian spirit was Yaksha Virabhadra. King Priyachanda ruled there. His queen was Subhadda Devi. Prince Vaishramana (Vesamane) was the crown prince. He married Siridevi, chief among five hundred royal maidens. The coming of the Tirthankara is described. Dhanpati's previous birth was as Jinadasa. In the city of Manipur, there was King Meharaha. Through the monk Sambhutivijaya, he attained spiritual progress, up to final liberation. The closing follows the pattern of the first chapter. The sixth chapter is completed.

⚠️ The text refers to Dhanpati's previous birth as "Jinadasa" — this is the same Jindas from Chapter 5. The name Dhanpati (meaning "lord of wealth") does not appear explicitly in the compressed Prakrit but is understood as the son of Prince Vaishramana and Siridevi, the main subject of the chapter's expanded narrative.

### 4. ANVAYARTHA
**कणगपुरं णयरं** = the city of Kanagpura (City of Gold); **सेयासोये उज्जाणं** = Setashoka garden; **वीरभद्दो जक्खो** = Yaksha Virabhadra (guardian spirit); **पियचंदो राया** = King Priyachanda; **सुभद्दा देवी** = Queen Subhadda; **वेसमणे कुमारे जुवराया** = Prince Vaishramana, the crown prince; **सिरीदेवी पामोक्खाणं** = Siridevi as chief; **पंचसयाणं रायवरकण्णगाणं** = among five hundred royal maidens; **पाणिग्गहणं** = marriage (literally: taking of the hand); **तित्थयरागमणं** = the coming of the Tirthankara; **जिणदासो पुव्वभवो** = Jinadasa as the previous birth; **मणिपुरं णयरे** = in the city of Manipur; **मेहरहो राया** = King Meharaha; **संभूतिविजए अणगारे** = the monk Sambhutivijaya; **पडिलाभिए** = having attained; **जाव सिद्धे** = up to liberation; **णिक्खेवो जहा पढमस्स** = the closing follows the first chapter; **छट्ठं अज्झयणं समत्तं** = the sixth chapter is completed

### 5. COMMENTARY
The sixth chapter introduces Dhanpati Kumar, whose story unfolds in Kanagpura, literally "the City of Gold." The setting carries symbolic weight: just as the city is named for material wealth, Dhanpati's name means "lord of wealth," yet the true wealth of the story is spiritual, not material.

King Priyachanda and Queen Subhadda rule the city. Their son, Prince Vaishramana — named after Vaishravana, the celestial guardian of wealth — serves as crown prince. He marries Siridevi, described as chief among five hundred royal maidens, following the standard literary convention for grand royal marriages. Dhanpati is born as their son.

The sutra mentions the arrival of the Tirthankara, indicating the moment when Lord Mahavira's teachings reach Kanagpura. Upon hearing the dharma, Dhanpati accepts the householder's path and eventually takes full renunciation.

The past-life connection is notable: the text identifies Dhanpati's previous birth as Jinadasa — the same character from Chapter 5. This creates a narrative thread linking the two chapters. In the city of Manipur, under King Meharaha, guided by the monk Sambhutivijaya, the soul that would become Dhanpati had practiced virtue and accumulated merit. That merit now manifests as Dhanpati's noble birth, his encounter with the teachings, and his ultimate liberation.

The chapter reinforces the principle that generosity and spiritual practice are the true sources of lasting prosperity. The names in this chapter — City of Gold, Lord of Wealth, Vaishramana — all point to worldly riches, yet the story's resolution is always the same: renunciation and liberation.

### 6. SIMPLY PUT
In the golden city of Kanagpura, Prince Vaishramana's son Dhanpati heard Lord Mahavira's teachings and chose the spiritual path. His good fortune came from merit earned in a past life — the same virtuous life described in Chapter 5. He eventually attained complete liberation.

### 7. CONTEMPLATE
The "City of Gold" gave Dhanpati everything the world could offer, yet he found his greatest treasure in renunciation. What would it take for you to recognize that true wealth lies beyond what can be possessed?
