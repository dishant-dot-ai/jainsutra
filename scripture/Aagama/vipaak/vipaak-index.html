विपाक सूत्र — The Scripture of Karmic Ripening

The Vipaak Sutra is the eleventh Anga of the Jain canonical scriptures. It presents twenty stories — ten of suffering born from past evil deeds, and ten of happiness born from past virtue — demonstrating with precise detail how karma ripens across lifetimes. Every condition has a traceable cause. No karma is infinite. The soul's natural state is liberation.

Taught By

Lord Mahavira

Transmitted By

Arya Sudharmashvami

Translated By

Dishant Shah

Pratham Shrutaskandha — Duhkha Vipaak

Ten stories of beings experiencing intense suffering as the direct fruit of evil deeds performed in a previous birth. Karma ripens — without exception, without mercy, without a moment's delay beyond its appointed time.

शकटकुमार

बृहस्पतिदत्त

Systematic falsehood and the collapse of a merchant's world

नन्दिवर्धन

Arrogance, the misuse of prosperity, and how excess destroys itself

Umbaradatt

उम्बरदत्त

Betrayal of trust, deception in sacred bonds, and many lives of atonement

Anjushri

Feminine suffering, the ripening of past cruelty, and the soul's passage through shame

Dvitiya Shrutaskandha — Sukha Vipaak

Ten stories of beings experiencing great happiness and blessing as the direct fruit of virtuous deeds performed in a previous birth. Karma ripens in both directions — what we plant in goodness, goodness returns.

1

Subahukumar

सुबाहुकुमार

The prince whose virtue across a past life blossomed into radiant fortune

→
2

Bhadranandi

भद्रनन्दि

The story of a soul whose generosity and devotion brought joyful fruition

→
3

Sujatkumar

सुजातकुमार

Non-violence, compassion toward all beings, and the happiness it plants in the future

→
4

Suvasankumar

सुवसंकुमार

Right livelihood, ethical conduct in commerce, and the fruit of a clean conscience

→
5

Jindas

जिनदास

Faith in the teachings, the power of right knowledge, and liberation drawn near

→
6

Dhanpati

धनपति

How generosity compounds across lifetimes like interest on gold

→
7

Mahabhal

महाबल

The strength that comes from righteousness, and the kingdom that virtue builds

→
8

Bhadrandiikumar

भद्रन्दीकुमार

Protecting the weak, the merit of protection, and the radiant life it generates

9

Mahachandrakumar

महाचन्द्रकुमार

Renunciation, the beauty of a life given wholly to the path, and liberation within reach

10

Varadatt

वरदत्त

The final ripening of virtue, the soul that has earned its freedom
