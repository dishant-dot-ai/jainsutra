Anuttarovavai Sutra

The Ninth Anga (અનુત્તરોવવાઈ સૂત્ર)

Thirty-three souls who reached the highest realm reachable from human birth.

The Anuttaropapātika Daśā — “the decads of those reborn in the heavens beyond which nothing is higher” — is the ninth Anga of the Jain canon. It records the lives of thirty-three great souls whose monastic discipline was so pure that, at the close of their human lives, they ascended directly into the five highest celestial realms: Vijaya, Vaijayanta, Jayanta, Aparājita, and Sarvārthasiddha — the Anuttara Vimānas.

The scripture is organized in three Vargas (sub-books) containing thirty-three Adhyayanas (chapters) in total. The first two Vargas tell of twenty-three princes of Rajagriha — sons and grandsons of King Shrenika — who renounced kingdoms to follow Lord Mahavira. The third and longest Varga is centered on the climactic narrative of Dhanya Anagar, whose nine-fold abhigraha (the ninefold lifelong vow of austerity) Mahavira himself called the foremost penance among his entire fourteen-thousand-strong order of monks.

None of these thirty-three souls attained final liberation in that birth. Each, after a single further human life in the great continent of Mahavideha, will reach moksha — the absolute end of suffering. The Ninth Anga is therefore the canon's portrait of the threshold: the souls who stand one heartbeat away from the freedom that all spiritual practice ultimately seeks.

account_tree

The structure of the canon

Three Vargas, ten chapters in the first, thirteen in the second, ten in the third — thirty-three souls in all. The text uses atidesha (reference by analogy) to compress repeated narratives without losing meaning.

self_improvement

The path of pure discipline

From Jali Kumar's renunciation of crores of wealth to Dhanya Anagar's nine-fold abhigraha, the scripture is a study of how a soul standing one step from liberation actually lives, eats, walks, and dies.

ac_unit

The Anuttara heavens

Five celestial realms beyond which nothing is higher: Vijaya, Vaijayanta, Jayanta, Aparājita, and Sarvārthasiddha. The scripture maps which soul ascended to which heaven and exactly when each will attain final liberation.

The Roster

All thirty-three souls, by name

The full named ascent — every prince, every monk, every soul listed in the Ninth Anga, each with the heaven they reached at the end of their human life.
