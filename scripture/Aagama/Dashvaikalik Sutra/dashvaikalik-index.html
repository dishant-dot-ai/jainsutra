Dashvaikalik Sutra દશવૈકાલિક સૂત્ર

The Curriculum for the New Monk. Composed by Acharya Shyambhava for his eight-year-old son Manak when Manak became a monk and only six months of life remained. Ten chapters and two chulikas teach every aspect of the monastic life — from how to bow and how to walk, to how to take alms, how to speak, how to sleep, and finally what defines a true monk.

Composed By

Ach. Shyāmbhava

Composed For

Manak Muni

Translated By

Dishant Shah

Sections

10 + 2 Chulikas

Adhyayan 01 arrow_outward

Drumapuṣpikā

The Tree-and-Flower simile. The bee-and-blossom image for how the right monk takes alms — never burdening any household, never uprooting the source.
Adhyayan 02 arrow_outward

Śrāmanyapūrvaka

The Foundation of Monkhood. The celebrated dialogue of Rajemati and Rathnemi — a wavering renunciate restored to the path by his sister-in-law turned nun.
Adhyayan 03 arrow_outward

Kṣullakācārakathā

The 52 Anachara. A precise enumeration of every act forbidden to the monk — the boundary line of monastic life.
Adhyayan 04 arrow_outward

Ṣaḍ-Jīvanikāya

The Six Classes of Life. The doctrinal heart of the sutra: earth, water, fire, air, plants, and mobile beings — every drop alive, every blade conscious.
Adhyayan 05 arrow_outward

Piṇḍeṣaṇā

The Search for Food. The most extensive treatment of monastic alms-conduct in the Jain canon — in two uddeshas covering both the alms-tour and the meal that follows.
Adhyayan 06 arrow_outward

Mahācārakathā

The Great Account of Conduct. Eighteen sthanas — pillars — of right monastic conduct: five mahavrats, night-eating prohibition, life-protection, and twelve more.
Adhyayan 07 arrow_outward

Suvākyaśuddhi

Purity of Speech. Four kinds of language, careful word-choice about plants and beings, the discipline of measured speech.
Adhyayan 08 arrow_outward

Ācāra-Praṇidhi

The Resolution of Conduct. The complete daily curriculum — care for life, restraint of senses, control of passions, the inner discipline.
Adhyayan 09 arrow_outward

Vinaya-Samādhi

Humility and Stillness. Four uddeshas on vinaya — the foundation of all spiritual progress — and the four samadhis: vinaya, shruta, tapa, achar.
Adhyayan 10 arrow_outward

Sa-Bhikṣu

Such a One Is a True Monk. Twenty-one verses, each ending with the unforgettable refrain "sa bhikkhū" — a living portrait of what the path produces.
Chulika 1 arrow_outward

Rativākyā

The Speech Against Falling Back. Eighteen similes that restore the wavering monk to the path.
Chulika 2 arrow_outward

Vivikta-Caryā

Solitary Conduct. The principles of solitary residence and the qualities of the truly liberated monk.
