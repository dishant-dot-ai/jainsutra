The Doctrine of Karma

घाती / अघाती कर्म

In Jain philosophy, the soul's journey is a struggle against the subtle matter of Karma. Understanding the two primary divisions is the first step toward spiritual awakening.

lock

Ghati Karmas

These are the **Destructive Karmas**. They directly obscure the soul's inherent qualities, preventing the manifestation of its infinite potential.

  • auto_awesome Infinite Knowledge (Jnanavaraniya)
  • auto_awesome Infinite Perception (Darshanavaraniya)
  • auto_awesome Infinite Bliss (Mohaniya)
  • auto_awesome Infinite Power (Antaraya)
body_system

Aghati Karmas

The **Non-Destructive Karmas**. These do not affect the soul's purity directly but instead shape your physical existence, determining the conditions of your life, body, and lifespan.

Vedaniya Nama Gotra Ayu

"As a weaver weaves a cloth with many threads, so does the soul weave its destiny with the threads of karma. To find the truth is to unravel the weave."

— Ancient Sutra Insights

The Dissolution

Liberation is achieved only when the Ghati Karmas are destroyed first. Only then, at the time of final emancipation, do the Aghati Karmas dissipate.

Meditation & Focus

Sustained internal focus weakens the bond of Mohaniya karma, the most powerful of the Ghati group.

Right Conduct

Ethical living prevents the influx of new karmic matter (Samvara) and burns old stock (Nirjara).
