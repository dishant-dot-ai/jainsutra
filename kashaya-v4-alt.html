Ancient Wisdom

The Four Passions
(Kashaya)

In the path toward liberation, one must first recognize the shadows that bind the soul. These are the kashayas—the sticky passions that trap our consciousness in the cycle of worldly existence.

The Burning One

Anger (Krodha)

Like a wildfire that consumes the very forest it inhabits, anger destroys the peace of the self before reaching others. It is the sudden storm that clouds judgment and fractures relationships.

"Patience is the cooling rain that quenches the fire of Krodha."

The Heavy Chain

Ego (Mana)

The elephant of the mind is often drunk on the wine of pride. Mana creates a wall between the soul and the truth, making one believe they are the mountain rather than a grain of sand.

"Humility is the gate through which the wise enter the sanctuary."

The Veil of Illusion

Deceit (Maya)

As the crane stands motionless to deceive the fish, Maya works through subtle layers of dishonesty and pretense. It is a labyrinth of mirrors that hides the true self from view.

"Simplicity and honesty are the lights that dissolve the shadows of Maya."

The Bottomless Jar

Greed (Lobha)

Lobha is the root of all other passions. It is the unquenchable thirst for more—more power, more wealth, more life. It weighs the soul down like heavy stones until it can no longer rise.

"Contentment is the wealth that no thief can ever steal."

"Victory over oneself is a greater victory than victory over thousands in a battle."
— Mahavira
