Dated: Vikram Samvat 2081

Foundations

Why this site exists

For centuries, the core tenets of Jainism—Ahimsa, Anekantavada, and Aparigraha—have been simplified into mere dietary restrictions or passive rituals. We believe these are not just rules, but a sophisticated technology of the soul.

Jain Awaken was born to bridge the gap between ancient Prakrit manuscripts and the modern seeker. We strip away the cultural dogma to reveal the raw, rigorous scholarship of liberation.

Scholarly Depth

Verified translations and ontological breakdowns of the Tattvartha Sutra and beyond.

Digital Sanctuary

A non-sectarian space for the preservation and contemplation of Tirthankara wisdom.

I. Foundation

01 — 03

Navkar Mantra

UNIVERSAL SALUTATION

The most powerful prayer in Jainism — a universal salutation to all enlightened souls, not to any one god.

Samyaktva

RIGHT PERCEPTION

Right perception — the foundation of the spiritual path, where you see reality as it truly is.

Ratnatraya

THE THREE JEWELS

The Three Jewels — right faith, right knowledge, and right conduct — the complete path to liberation.

II. The Soul & Reality

04 — 07
THE CONSCIOUS

Jiva

The soul — the eternal conscious being with infinite knowledge, perception, bliss, and energy.

Ajiva

Non-living matter — everything in the universe that is not a soul, from atoms to space and time.

6 Dravyas

The six eternal substances that make up the entire universe — soul, matter, motion, rest, space, and time.

Gati

THE FOUR REALMS

The four realms of existence — celestial, human, animal/plant, and hellish — where souls are born based on karma.

III. Karma System

08 — 13
08

Nav Tattvas

The nine realities — the complete framework that explains how karma binds the soul and how to break free.

 09

Karma

Not punishment or reward — karma is physical matter that sticks to the soul through every action, thought, and word.

 10

Ghati & Aghati

The two types of karma — destructive ones that block the soul's true nature, and non-destructive ones that shape the body and life.

 11

Kashaya

The four passions — anger, pride, deceit, and greed — the root causes that attract karma to the soul.

 12

Asrav, Samvar, Nirjara

Influx, stoppage, and shedding — the three-part process of how karma enters the soul, how to stop it, and how to burn it off.

 13

Leshya

The six colors of the soul — from black to white — that reflect its spiritual purity based on thoughts and actions.
SECTION IV

The Path

The practical discipline of daily spiritual practice — how to live, think, and act on the path to liberation.

BEGIN READING

Charitra

Right conduct — the ethical discipline of non-violence, truthfulness, and self-control in daily life.

Bhavnas

The twelve reflections — meditative contemplations that develop detachment, compassion, and inner peace.

Pratikraman

The practice of self-reflection and repentance — reviewing your actions and seeking forgiveness for harm caused.

Gunsthanaks

The fourteen stages of spiritual progress — a precise map from complete delusion to total liberation.
FINAL INTEGRATION

Anekantavada

The doctrine of many-sidedness — no single viewpoint captures the whole truth. Reality is infinite, and every perspective reveals only a part of it.
