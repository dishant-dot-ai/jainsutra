GATI • TRANSITIONS OF THE SOUL

The Four Destinations (गति)

"In Jain cosmology, the soul wanders through four states of existence until it achieves liberation."

Understanding the cycles of birth and rebirth is the first step toward the eternal path of the Arihants.

person
03 MANUSHYA

Human Beings (मनुष्य)

The only state where spiritual growth and liberation (Moksha) are possible through conscious effort. It is considered a rare and precious opportunity for the soul to break the cycle.

Path to Liberation arrow_right_alt
01 NARAK

Hellish Beings (नरक)

dark_mode

Intense suffering and dark environments where karma is purged through pain. A state of deep spiritual slumber and heavy karmic bondage.

02 TIRYANCH

Sub-human (तिर्यञ्च)

nature

Instinct-driven existence in varied forms of life, from microscopic to mammalian. Governed by fear, hunger, and basic survival needs.

auto_awesome
04 DEV

Celestial Beings (देव)

Long-lived, pleasure-filled states of existence. While blissful, these souls are still bound by the cycle of karma and often lack the drive to seek ultimate liberation due to their comforts.

The Essence of Gati

"The soul is the master of its own destiny. The Gati is but a temporary shadow of the karma we carry, yet through Viveka (discernment), one can transcend all four destinations to reach the Siddha-shila."

— Tattvartha Sutra, Chapter IV
