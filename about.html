About

Why this site exists

Most Jains grow up knowing two things about their religion: don't eat meat, and don't eat after sunset. Some add: don't eat potatoes. A few more know about Paryushana and Mahavir Jayanti.

But ask about the Nav Tattvas — the nine realities that form the complete map of how karma works — and you get silence. Ask about the Gunsthanaks — the fourteen stages of spiritual progress that Jain philosophy maps with extraordinary precision — and you get blank stares. Ask why the Navkar Mantra names Arihantas before Siddhas, and most Jains have never even considered the question.

This is the gap JainAwaken exists to fill.

Not with religious instruction. Not with sectarian interpretation. Not with devotional content designed to make you feel good. With clear, honest, accessible explanations of the philosophical framework that makes Jainism one of the most rigorous and complete systems of thought ever developed.

Who this is for

Jains who were never taught. You grew up in the tradition but were handed rituals without reasons. You follow the diet but don't know the doctrine. This site gives you the 'why' behind the 'what.'

Non-Jains who are curious. You've encountered Jain ideas — non-violence, karma, Anekantavada — and want to understand the complete system, not just fragments. This site respects your intelligence and doesn't oversimplify.

The strayed. You grew up Jain, drifted away, and now wonder if there was more to it than what you were shown. There was. Much more.

Students and seekers. You're studying comparative philosophy, Indian thought, or ethics, and want a clear entry point into Jain metaphysics and moral theory.

What this is not

This is not a religious authority. It is not affiliated with any sect, organization, or guru. It does not claim to represent the 'correct' interpretation of Jain philosophy. It is an educational resource — a starting point for understanding, not a substitute for deep study with qualified teachers.

Source material

The content on this site is informed by classical Jain texts and modern educational resources, including the excellent Jainism Simplified resource from the University of Michigan Jain community. All explanations are original — written to be clear, modern, and accessible while remaining faithful to the philosophical tradition.
