Conceptual Framework

लेश्या

"The soul's karmic color—from deepest black to purest white."

In Jain philosophy, Leshya refers to the state of mind and the coloring of the soul based on its karmic vibrations and emotional intensity.

The Six Colorations

Every thought leaves a hue upon the spiritual canvas of the being.

Krishna

Black Leshya

Representing the state of intense cruelty, anger, and darkness. The lowest spiritual vibration.

Neel

Blue Leshya

Symbolizing pride, laziness, and a lack of moral direction. Deep attachment to worldly desires.

Kapot

Grey Leshya

Indicative of fear, sorrow, and deceit. A soul caught in the turmoil of worldly anxiety.

Tejo

Red Leshya

The dawn of spiritual awakening. Representing a soul that is becoming prudent and self-aware, though still passionate.

Padma

Yellow Leshya

The color of the lotus. High spiritual purity, benevolence, and a profound sense of compassion toward all beings.

Shukla

White Leshya

"The state of purest transcendence. Absolute equanimity, freedom from attachment, and the brilliance of the liberated soul."

The Significance of Hue

Leshya is not a physical color but a spiritual vibration. It serves as a visual metaphor for the weight of our intentions. Just as white light can be refracted into many shades, the pure soul is colored by the 'Kasayas' (passions) of greed, ego, deceit, and anger.
