Mantra of Universal Reverence

णमो अरिहंताणं

The most fundamental Jain prayer—not a wish, but a bow to greatness.

The Pancha Paramesthi

Bowing to the 5 supreme beings

01

Arihants

The enlightened souls who have conquered inner passions like greed, anger, and ego, showing the path of liberation to the world.

Namo Arihantanam arrow_forward
02

Siddhas

Liberated souls who have attained the state of Moksha, forever free from the cycle of birth and death.

auto_awesome
03

Acharyas

Spiritual leaders and heads of the monastic community who guide followers through their wisdom and discipline.

04

Upadhyayas

The learned teachers who master the sacred scriptures and transmit the profound knowledge of Dharma.

05

Sadhus & Sadhvis

All monks and nuns who follow the path of non-violence, truth, and asceticism in their pursuit of spiritual purity.

"The Navkar Mantra is the most auspicious of all that is auspicious. It destroys all sins."
— Jain Kalpa Sutra

Universal Reverence

The beauty of the Navkar Mantra lies in its selflessness. It does not mention a single name or ask for personal favors. Instead, it offers salutations to the virtues and the stages of spiritual evolution, acknowledging the divinity within all.
