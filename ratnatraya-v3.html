रत्नत्रय

The Three Jewels: The path to spiritual liberation.

The Complete Path

In Jain philosophy, the **Ratnatraya** (The Three Jewels) constitute the essential path to Nirvana. They are not independent steps, but three facets of a single, unified spiritual journey.

Explore the Trinity arrow_forward
visibility

Samyak Darshan

Right Faith

The unwavering belief in the true nature of reality. It is the conviction in the soul's potential and the teachings of the Tirthankaras.

auto_stories

Samyak Jnan

Right Knowledge

A deep, accurate understanding of the self and the universe. It is knowledge free from doubt, delusion, or error.

self_improvement

Samyak Charitra

Right Conduct

The practical application of faith and knowledge. It involves living a life governed by non-violence (Ahinsa), truth, and discipline to shed karmic particles.

format_quote

"Knowledge without faith is blind; Faith without knowledge is hollow; Both without conduct are fruitless."

Ancient Sutra

The Inseparable Bond

Like a medicine that requires faith in the doctor, knowledge of the dose, and the act of swallowing—liberation requires the union of all three jewels.

To possess Samyak Darshan without Jnan is to have a map but no light. To have Jnan without Charitra is to have the light but refuse to walk the path. Only together do they form the *Moksha Marga*—the path to liberation.
