Non-Attachment

Samyaktva is the soul's correct self-orientation — recognizing its own nature as separate from karma, body, and external identity. Without it, all practice is ritual. With it, everything becomes a path.

It is not merely an intellectual agreement with philosophical tenets. Samyaktva is a radical shifts in the vantage point of consciousness. Imagine a mirror covered in centuries of dust; Samyaktva is the moment the first corner is wiped clean, allowing the light to reflect truly for the first time.

In Jain ontology, this is the Fourth Gunasthana—the fourth stage of spiritual development. It marks the permanent break from the cycle of aimless wandering. Once Samyaktva is achieved, even for a moment, the soul is destined for Siddhashila (liberation).
