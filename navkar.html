FOUNDATION // THE SACRED SALUTATION
णमो अरिहंताणं

Navkar Mantra

Not a wish. A bow to greatness.

No petition. No plea. No bargain with the divine.

The Navkar Mantra is pure reverence — a bow to five supreme beings who represent the highest spiritual accomplishment. It contains zero requests. Genuine reverence transforms the one who offers it.

01
THE ENLIGHTENED MASTERS

Namo Arihantanam

I bow to the Arihants, the conquerors of inner enemies like anger, greed, and ego, who have attained omniscience but remain to guide humanity.

02
THE LIBERATED SOULS

Namo Siddhanam

I bow to the Siddhas, the perfected souls who have discarded the physical body and broken free from the cycle of birth and death forever.

03
THE SPIRITUAL LEADERS

Namo Ayariyanam

I bow to the Acharyas, the heads of the ascetic orders who embody self-discipline and preserve the ancient teachings.

04
THE LEARNED PRECEPTORS

Namo Uvajjhayanam

I bow to the Upadhyayas, the master teachers who dedicate their existence to the study and dissemination of the sacred scriptures.

05
THE ASCETICS EVERYWHERE

Namo Loe Savva Sahunam

I bow to all the Sadhus and Sadhvis in the universe who follow the path of non-violence, truth, and detachment.

Why this order?

Arihantas before Siddhas — because while Siddhas have achieved liberation, Arihantas are still here, actively teaching. They bridge the liberated and the seeking.

By bowing to these qualities — conquest of passion, liberation, leadership, knowledge, and renunciation — you begin cultivating them within yourself.

Every Jain prayer, ceremony, and meditation begins here. It is the foundation.

KEY TERMS
Arihant One who has destroyed all inner enemies and attained omniscience.
Siddha A fully liberated soul, free from birth and death.
Vitrag Complete detachment from worldly passions.
