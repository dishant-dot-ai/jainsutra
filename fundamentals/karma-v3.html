Extensity of Kashaya

The Four Spiritual Ceilings

In Jain philosophy, the duration of an emotion determines your spiritual ceiling. Like marks left on different surfaces, our attachments and aversions define the depth of our karmic bondage.

Stage I

Anantanubandhi

"The Line in Rock"

This represents an infinite duration of kashaya. Like a line etched in solid rock, it refuses to fade. It forms a total spiritual block (Samyaktva), preventing the soul from even perceiving the truth of its own nature.

Spiritual Block

Samyaktva (Right Perception)

Stage II

Apratyakhyaniya

"The Crack in Dried Mud"

Emotions that persist for more than one year. Like a fissure in parched earth, it is stubborn but eventually weathered. This intensity blocks the soul's ability to take even Partial Vows (Desh-Vrata).

Spiritual Block

Partial Vows (Anuvratas)

Stage III

Pratyakhyaniya

"The Line in Sand"

Kashaya lasting up to four months. It is easily disturbed and wiped away by the wind of awareness. Yet, its presence still prevents the soul from committing to Full Vows (Maha-Vrata).

Spiritual Block

Full Vows (Renunciation)

Stage IV

Sanjwalan

"The Line in Water"

The mildest form, lasting no more than 15 days. Like a ripple on a pond, it vanishes almost as soon as it appears. This allows for the highest states of meditation but prevents Total Purity.

Spiritual Block

Total Purity (Yathakhyat)
