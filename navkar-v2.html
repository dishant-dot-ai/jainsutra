Supreme Prayer

Navkar Mantra

णमो अरिहंताणं Namo Arihantanam

The most fundamental Jain prayer — not a wish, but a bow to greatness.

The Navkar Mantra is the most significant mantra in Jainism. Unlike many traditional prayers, it does not seek material wealth, protection, or favors from the divine. Instead, it is a mantra of profound respect and equanimity.

By bowing to the five supreme beings, the practitioner acknowledges the qualities of liberation and peace. It reminds the soul what it can become—shedding the weight of ego to mirror the purity of those who have attained absolute knowledge.

The Philosophy of Anvaya

"It doesn't ask for anything. It reminds the soul what it can become."

Focus on the quality, not the person. The mantra honors the state of being, offering a map for the internal journey toward liberation.

The Fivefold Greeting

Pancha Paramesthi

The five supreme authorities of the soul's evolution.

01

Arihants

Souls who have conquered internal enemies like anger, pride, and greed while still in physical form.

02

Siddhas

Liberated souls who have shed the physical body and reside in the state of eternal bliss.

03

Acharyas

Spiritual leaders who head the monastic orders and exemplify disciplined conduct.

04

Upadhyayas

The learned teachers who master and instruct the sacred scriptures and philosophy.

05

Sadhus

All monks and seekers who have renounced worldly life to follow the path of non-violence.
